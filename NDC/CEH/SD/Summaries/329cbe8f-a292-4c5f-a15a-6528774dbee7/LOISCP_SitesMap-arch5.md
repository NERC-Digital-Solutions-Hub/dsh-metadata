# HUMBER CATHCMENT

## Purpose and scope
- Data Stewards ensure Humber Catchment datasets meet appropriate standards, are stored, shared, and kept up to date so they can be readily discovered and used.

## Who they are
- Work for organisations or networks with large datasets, ensuring appropriate data governance.
- Understand good data management practice; collaborate with data creators/sharers; informed by understanding data user needs.

## How they meet their aims in Humber Catchment
- Understand user needs to create usable datasets; may adopt a publishing mindset.
- Work with data creators to ensure standards are met (accuracy, consistency, metadata, format); provide guidance and tools.
- Identify data availability and limitations (disclosure risks, proprietary issues, embargoes).
- Receive data (including chasing up when needed).
- Quality assure, clean, and transform datasets before sharing.
- Ensure sharing limitations are respected and establish systems for updates.
- Upload datasets to relevant portals and catalogue data holdings.
- Document work done on datasets.

## Particular challenges
- Incomplete picture of user needs and priorities.
- Getting data in a timely manner.
- Getting data creators to meet standards, including metadata.
- Working with many different systems and formats, including difficult ones.
- Working with large datasets that are hard to transfer.
- Working with outdated databases that necessitate bespoke, non-interoperable solutions.
