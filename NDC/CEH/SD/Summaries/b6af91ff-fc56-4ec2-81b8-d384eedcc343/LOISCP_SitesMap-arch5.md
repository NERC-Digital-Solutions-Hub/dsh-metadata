# HUMBER CATHCMENT

- Context and purpose
  - Aligning with the Data Stewards aim to ensure datasets are governed, stored, shared, and kept up to date so they are readily discoverable and usable.
  - Applies to Humber Catchment datasets within an organization or network with many datasets, focusing on appropriate data governance.

- What a Data Steward would do for Humber CATHCMENT
  - Understand user needs to ensure Humber Catchment data are usable, potentially adopting a publishing mindset to create usable datasets.
  - Collaborate with data creators/sharers to enforce standards (accuracy, consistency, metadata, formats) and provide guidance/tools.
  - Identify data availability and restrictions (disclosure risks, proprietary issues, embargoes) and document these.
  - Manage data receipt, including follow-ups when necessary.
  - Quality assure, clean, and transform datasets prior to sharing.
  - Ensure sharing limitations are respected and implement update mechanisms and versioning.
  - Upload datasets to relevant portals and catalogue Humber Catchment data holdings.
  - Document work performed on datasets where appropriate.

- Typical challenges in this context
  - Incomplete picture of user needs and priorities.
  - Timely acquisition of data from providers.
  - Encouraging data creators to meet standards, including metadata.
  - Handling many different systems and formats, including difficult ones.
  - Managing large datasets that are hard to transfer.
  - Dealing with outdated databases requiring bespoke, non-interoperable solutions.
