# Joint Code of Practice for Research (JCoPR)

- A governance framework for research funded by UK bodies to ensure fitness for purpose and high scientific and research process quality.
- Endorsed and implemented by multiple government departments, agencies, and research councils; launched in 2003 and effective from 1 June 2004.
- Includes periodic monitoring and third-party audits (e.g., UKAS) to assess compliance; revised after audit findings.

## Background
- Developed by BBSRC, Defra, FSA, NERC; subsequently endorsed by Devolved Administrations and other agencies (APHA, Fera, FC, MMO, NE, VMD).
- Applies to all funded research across listed organisations and institutes.
- Emphasises alignment of research quality (QS) and research process quality (QP); aims for diligent, competent, and trained researchers.

## Principles behind the Code
- Contractors must pursue both QS (aims, approaches, knowledge extraction) and QP (rigour, repeatability, auditable processes).
- Commitment to best scientific practice from the top levels of funded organisations.
- Overriding principle: fitness for purpose; researchers should be competent and adequately trained.

## Compliance with the Code
- Funding bodies require acceptance of the Code at proposal submission; discussions on inappropriate clauses can be documented and included in funding terms.
- Risk assessments may be conducted; contractors must ensure subcontractors adhere to JCoPR.
- For BBSRC/NERC-funded work, annual acceptance by Institute Director is required.

## Monitoring of compliance with the Code
- Organisations should have policies and processes to ensure compliance, and ideally operate a formal quality assurance system audited by an independent third party.
- Bodies reserve the right to audit funded projects or contractor systems to verify standards.

## 1. Responsibilities
- Contractor organisation is accountable for overall research quality and compliance with internal policies.
- Managers, group leaders, and supervisors foster good scientific practice and skill development.
- The Principal Investigator/Project Leader holds responsibility for all project work, including subcontractors.

## 2. Competence
- All personnel must be competent for their tasks; trainees must be supervised to protect quality.
- Contractors must provide documented evidence of capability and maintain training records.

## 3. Project planning
- Conduct appropriate risk assessments to anticipate factors affecting success.
- Maintain a clear written project plan addressing design, statistical methods, and other requirements; plan to be agreed with the Funding Body.
- Schedule regular timetable reviews; major amendments require pre-approval.

## 4. Quality Control
- Implement planned processes to assure research quality; conduct regular fit-for-purpose reviews.
- Outputs require authorization by Funding Body or senior management; notify and address post-publication errors quickly.
- Embrace continual improvement of procedures.

## 5. Health and Safety
- All research must comply with applicable health and safety regulations.

## 6. Handling of samples and materials
- Label samples accurately and durably; retain for a period agreed with the Funding Body.
- Store and handle samples per project plans; monitor critical storage conditions; track samples through analysis; define disposal routes and dates.

## 7. Facilities and equipment
- Ensure a safe, appropriate working environment; regular maintenance of facilities and equipment.
- Calibrate equipment at appropriate intervals; have contingency plans for disruptions.
- Maintain standard operating procedures for critical equipment.

## 8. Documentation of procedures and methods
- Document all procedures and methods; maintain an auditable trail with version control.
- Include validation of methods and ensure traceability from primary data to processed information.

## 9. Research / work records
- Records must be detailed enough to replicate work; regularly reviewed by the Project Leader.
- Store and secure records (hard copy and electronic); prevent unauthorized modification; retain for the period required by the Funding Body.

## 10. Field-based research
- Comply with relevant environmental legislation; manage discharges and emissions to minimise pollution risk.
- Apply all JCoPR provisions to field-based research as appropriate.
