# East Stoke gauging station, Dorset, UK

- What is depicted
  - A geographic visualization featuring the East Stoke gauging station in Dorset, UK.
  - Nearby rivers labeled: River Hooke, Sydling Water, River Cerne, South Winterbourne, River Frome, River Piddle.
  - A distance scale indicating 0 to 16 kilometers.

- Data landscape implications for Data Leaders
  - Indicates a network of hydrological data assets across multiple rivers that should be catalogued and managed cohesively.
  - Highlights the need for standardized metadata and data formats to enable discovery and combined use across stations.
  - Suggests potential for cross-partner collaboration to consolidate data products and minimize duplication.

- User needs and governance considerations
  - Data products should support diverse users (e.g., policymakers, researchers) and seek avenues for co-ownership of data outputs.
  - Emphasizes the importance of discoverability, timeliness, provenance, and data quality indicators for usable data assets.

- Opportunities and recommended actions
  - Develop an integrated inventory of gauging stations and associated data streams in the Dorset region.
  - Standardize metadata (station location, measurement type, frequency, units, QA/QC, provenance).
  - Ensure regular data updates and accessible data delivery through a central platform or catalog.
  - Foster sector-wide coordination to reduce duplication and strengthen communities of practice around hydrological data.
