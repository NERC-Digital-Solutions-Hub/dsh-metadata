# SOIL PROFILE OF RIGG Foot, Sourhope : BLOCK 4

- Overview of soil profile and accompanying analytical data for Block 4 at Rigg Foot, Sourhope, including horizon descriptions, soil chemistry, moisture, and vegetation data.
- Data are organized by horizons (LF, FH, H, Ah, Abg, B sg, C gx) with corresponding qualitative descriptions and quantitative analyses.

## Data types and structure

- Soil profile descriptions by horizon with depth ranges and qualitative characteristics (grain identifiability, moisture, root presence, boundary types).
- Quantitative soil analyses by horizon:
  - Weight (mg)
  - %N, %C
  - C:N ratio
  - Exchangeable cations: Ca, Na, K, Mg (meq/100 g)
  - %Moisture loss, %Loss on Ignition
  - pH: H2O and CaCl2
- Auger samples (4A–4F) with corresponding same metrics as above.
- Vegetation survey data by subplots (4A, 4B, 4C, 4D, 4E, 4F) including species counts and totals.
- MLURI barcodes linked to each horizon and auger sample for traceability.

## Key horizon details (Block 4)

- LF: 0–1 cm; fibrous, moist; no identifiable mineral grains.
- FH: 1–4 cm; fibrous, moist; abundant fine roots; sharp boundary.
- H: 4–8 cm; black matrix colour; moist; weak blocky structure; roots present.
- Ah: 8–29 cm; dark reddish brown sandy silt loam; friable; abundant roots; presence of igneous stones.
- Abg: 29–40 cm; pinkish grey with mottling; coarse sandy silt loam; mixed stone content.
- B sg: 40–61 cm; brown with mottling; sandy silt loam; moist to firm; roots present.
- C gx: 61–95 cm; reddish brown to brown; sandy clay loam; massive to platy structure; few roots.

## Soil analyses (Block 4)

- Depths span from LH to C horizons with horizon-specific values:
  - %N ranges approximately from 0.12% to 2.14% across horizons.
  - %C shows high surface values (e.g., FH around 38.12% C) decreasing with depth (Ah, AB, Bs, Cx).
  - C:N ratios generally in the 12–20 range, decreasing with depth in some horizons.
  - Ca, Mg, K, Na, and other cations vary by horizon, with Ca often highest in surface horizons (e.g., Ca around 10.05 meq/100 g in FH) and lower in deeper horizons.
  - %Moisture loss and %Loss on Ignition indicate organic matter content and combustion-based assessments; surface horizons show higher LOI.
  - pH(H2O) typically in the mid-4s to near 5, with pH(CaCl2) around 3.8–4.2 in several horizons.
- Notable patterns:
  - Higher carbon and nitrogen in surface horizons (FH/Ah) with decreasing trends downward.
  - Acidic surface conditions (low pH) with variable exchangeable cations, reflecting mineralogy and organic matter.

## Auger samples (Block 4)

- Auger samples 4A–4F include the same suite of measurements as horizon data:
  - %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, moisture, LOI, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
- Observed ranges show modest N and C values compared with horizon data, with horizon-like variability across sample points.
- C:N ratios and cation concentrations indicate spatial heterogeneity within the block’s surface to subsoil layers.

## Vegetation survey (Block 4)

- Subplots 4A, 4B, 4C, 4D, 4E, 4F include species counts and totals.
- Totals indicate varying species richness by subplot, with some plots showing higher counts of indicator or prevalent grasses and forbs.
- Data capture presence/absence and potential abundance notes, enabling linkages between vegetation patterns and soil properties across the block.

## ECOTRON subplot (Rigg Foot, Sourhope)

- Soil analysis: weight, %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, moisture, LOI, pH(H2O), pH CaCl2.
- Vegetation survey: Ac, Ao, At, Br, Cp, Cxp, Dc, Df, Fo, Fr, Gs, Hm, Je, Lm, Lzm, Mc, Ns, Pe, P, etc., with counts per category and totals per subplot.

## Data management, metadata, and archiving (Block 4)

- Provenance and traceability:
  - MLURI Barcodes assigned to each horizon and auger sample.
  - Depths, horizon designations, and boundary types clearly recorded.
- Data are organized by horizon, auger sample, and vegetation subplot with associated sample IDs and coordinates.
- Archival considerations:
  - Soil samples and analytical residues stored and linked to external programs (ECN, SERAD Micronet) for long-term analysis.
  - Documentation and analyses referenced via appendices and separate data files.

## Beginning-to-end data considerations and quality

- Sampling coverage: data exist for multiple horizons and subplots, but some horizons or subplots have limited or missing measurements.
- Variability: substantial horizon-to-horizon and plot-to-plot variation in chemical properties and pH, reflecting heterogeneity in parent material, moisture regime, and organic matter.
- Metadata needs: consistent units and metadata schemas across blocks to support cross-block comparability (e.g., uniform reporting of mg weights, %N, %C, pH, and cation units).

## Particular data challenges and gaps (Block 4)

- Some Na measurements are missing in certain horizons.
- Subplot-level vegetation data are complex and may require normalization for cross-block comparisons.
- Spatial heterogeneity within Block 4 necessitates careful QC and normalization when aggregating across horizons or plots.

## Practical implications for data leadership

- Establish a unified metadata dictionary with consistent units and horizon nomenclature across blocks (e.g., LF, FH, H, Ah, AB, Bg/BCg, Cg).
- Ensure robust data provenance by linking every horizon and auger sample to MLURI Barcodes, plot IDs, and depth intervals.
- Maintain long-term storage arrangements and clear archival pathways (e.g., -80°C storage for residues, ECN data feeds) to support future biodiversity and soil-ecology analyses.
- Develop data pipelines that harmonize soil, vegetation, and climate data across blocks, enabling cross-block Twinspan/NVC/Association-style analyses.
- Acknowledge data gaps (partial subplot sampling, missing Na, horizon-specific gaps) in reporting and plan targeted sampling to improve coverage in future seasons.

## Endnotes and references (Block 4 context)

- References to data files and appendices (e.g., Twinspan, Association datasets) and connections to ECN and SERAD Micronet projects highlighted, underscoring data interoperability across networks.

# SOIL PROFILE OF RIGG Foot, Sourhope : BLOCK 5

- Continuation of soil and vegetation data for Block 5, including site details, soil profile descriptions, soil analyses, auger samples, vegetation surveys, and plot-level summaries.
- Block 5 introduces a different horizon sequence (LF, FH, H, Ah, AB, Bg, BCg, Cg) with its own set of measurements and notes.

## Block 5 site details and soil profile

- Site details: grid reference NT8544019520; altitude ~315 m; slope ~5 degrees; Imperfect drainage.
- Major soil subgroups: Brown forest soil with gleying; parent material includes undifferentiated intermediate igneous and andesite.
- Horizon sequence:
  - LF: 0–1 cm; fibrous; moist; no stones.
  - FH: 1–5 cm; semi-fibrous; moist; roots present.
  - H: 5–7 cm; black matrix; semi-fibrous; roots present.
  - Ah: 7–25 cm; very dark brown; sandy silt loam; friable; roots; many medium to large igneous stones.
  - AB: 25–35 cm; grey; coarse sandy silt loam; roots; mottles.
  - Bg: 35–49 cm; reddish brown/yellowish red mottling; clayey loam; roots; abundant stones.
  - BCg: 49–70 cm; mottled reddish brown; clay loam; massive-to-blocky structure.
  - Cg: 70–100 cm; light reddish brown; clay loam; massive structure.

## Block 5 soil analyses (profile)

- Horizon-specific metrics mirror Block 4 but with Block 5 values:
  - %N and %C vary by horizon, with Ah and AB showing elevated carbon in surface horizons, decreasing with depth.
  - C:N ratios generally in mid-teens to low twenties across horizons.
  - Exchangeable Ca, Mg, K, Na show horizon-dependent patterns with surface horizons often higher in Ca and Mg.
  - %Moisture loss and %Loss on Ignition indicate organic matter distribution, typically higher in Ah and surface horizons.
  - pH values (H2O and CaCl2) reflect acidic to slightly acidic conditions, with surface horizons tending to slightly lower pH.

## Block 5 auger samples

- Auger samples 5A–5F include standard measurements:
  - %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, %Moisture loss, %Loss on Ignition, pH(H2O), pH CaCl2.
- Variability observed among auger samples consistent with horizon- and plot-level heterogeneity.

## Block 5 vegetation data

- Vegetation surveys by plot 5A, 5B, 5C, 5D, 5E, 5F with subplot-level species presence/absence and totals.
- Species totals indicate varying richness by subplot and overall community composition aligned with soil and moisture gradients.

## ECOTRON data context (Block 5 ECOTRON)

- ECOTRON soil analysis and vegetation data available, enabling cross-plot comparisons with other blocks and integration into broader ecosystem studies.

## Beginning-to-end data considerations (Block 5)

- Horizon sequence provides a deeper, more developed soil profile than Block 4, with a notable BCg horizon reflecting gleying and mottling patterns.
- Data show coherent links between soil chemistry and horizon development, with pronounced organic matter in Ah and AB horizons, and complex mineralogical patterns in Bg and BCg horizons.

## Data management implications (Block 5)

- Ensure cross-block comparability by harmonizing horizon nomenclature and sampling schemes.
- Maintain traceability via barcodes and plot identifiers; preserve raw data and link to archival storage and external data networks.
- Acknowledge gaps in subplot sampling and Na data in some horizons; plan for targeted sampling to improve coverage.
