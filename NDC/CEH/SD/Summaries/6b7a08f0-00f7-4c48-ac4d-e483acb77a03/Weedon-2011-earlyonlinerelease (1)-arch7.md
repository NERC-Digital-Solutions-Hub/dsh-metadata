## GIS relevance and usage notes (brief)

- The data provide diurnal-cycle-resolving PET estimates and forcing fields suitable for driving regional hydrological and land-surface models within the 20th century.
- PET_rc and PET_pt offer bounding estimates of potential evapotranspiration to support water-balance analyses across basins.
- For early decades (1901–1957), treat regional trends with caution due to sparser observations and randomized ERA-40 basis years.
- Use both PET formulations to assess sensitivity of hydrological outcomes to PET choice.
- Regridding to analysis-ready resolutions should preserve diurnal cycles and consider regional coherence of rainfall across grid cells.

## Practical considerations

- WATCH Forcing Data offers a harmonized, diurnal-cycle-resolving forcing framework with bias corrections, enabling global-to-regional evaporation analyses.
- The 1901–1957 extension preserves statistical properties but may underrepresent exact historical variability and climate-change trends in certain basins.
- Regional biases can persist in variables such as wind, precipitation, and aerosol-forced radiation, especially in data-sparse decades or regions.
