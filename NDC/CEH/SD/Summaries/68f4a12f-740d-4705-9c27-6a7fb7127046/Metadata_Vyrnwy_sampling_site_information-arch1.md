# PRIMARY Dyfnant Forest draining in to Llyn Vyrnwy

## Overview
- A hydrochemical study of a plantation spruce forest catchment in mid Wales (Vyrnwy area), focusing on rainfall, streams, and boreholes to understand the interaction between rainfall, groundwater, and surface water.
- Primary publication: Neal C., Reynolds B., Neal M., Williams B. 2004. The hydrochemistry of plantation spruce forest catchments with brown earth soils, Vyrnwy in mid Wales. Hydrology and Earth System Sciences, 8, 460-484.

## Spatial and temporal scope
- Datasets cover multiple interconnected components (rainfall, streams, boreholes) within the Dyfnant Forest area feeding into Llyn Vyrnwy.

## Data connections and context
- Location data include precise eastings/northings and latitude/longitude (WGS84) and altitude for each site.
- Sites are tied to plantation spruce forestry with brown earth soils, enabling investigation of how land management and soil type influence hydrochemistry.

## Relevance for analysis
- Enables analysis of hydrochemical relationships among rainfall inputs, ephemeral streams, and groundwater in a plantation-dominated catchment.
- Supports comparative analysis between disturbed (clearfelled) and control sites to assess impact of forestry practices on hydrology and chemistry.
