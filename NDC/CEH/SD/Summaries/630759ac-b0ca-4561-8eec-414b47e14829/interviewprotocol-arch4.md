# Experimental Design

## Overview
- Qualitative study conducted to complement quantitative assessment of antibiotic resistant bacteria among poultry, humans, and the environment in Bangladesh.
- Aimed to explore practices and perceptions related to antibiotic use, poultry rearing, hygiene, waste disposal, and biosecurity.

## Study Settings and Population
- Rural households with domestic poultry.
- Small-scale commercial poultry farmers in rural areas.
- Urban live poultry market stall holders in Dhaka.
- Locations: Mirzapur sub-district, Tangail District (rural) and Dhaka city (urban).

## Methodology and Themes
- In-depth interviews guided by three settings to explore:
  - Poultry rearing practices (care, feed, decision-making, disease prevention)
  - Waste-disposal practices (solid/liquid waste, carcasses, processing water)
  - Biosecurity, hygiene, and personal protection around poultry work
  - Sources of advice and influences on poultry business decisions
  - Perceptions of antibiotics and reasons for their use
- Formative research approach; no a priori sample size calculation.
- Interviews conducted until saturation of responses and themes was reached.

## Ethics and Governance
- Written informed consent with right to withdraw.
- Ethical clearance: International Centre for Diarrhoeal Disease Research, Bangladesh (PR16071) and Loughborough University (R17-P037).

## Data Collection and Sample
- Location details:
  - Rural: Mirzapur sub-district, Tangail District
  - Urban: Dhaka city live poultry markets
- Total of 18 interviews:
  - Rural poultry owners: 3
  - Small-scale commercial farm workers: 10
  - Urban live poultry stall holders: 5

## Data Handling and Quality Control
- Interviews conducted and transcribed by an experienced researcher from ICDDR,B.
- Transcripts checked against original audio recordings.
- Data structure: anonymised interview transcripts, conducted and transcribed in Bangla.

## Data Structure and Accessibility
- Anonymised Bangla transcripts available for analysis.
- Emphasis on maintaining data quality through transcription verification and ethical handling.
