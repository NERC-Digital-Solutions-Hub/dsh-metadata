# Experimental Design

## Overview
- Qualitative study conducted to complement quantitative assessment of antibiotic-resistant bacteria among poultry, humans, and the environment in Bangladesh.
- Aims to explore poultry rearing, waste disposal, biosecurity, decision making, sources of advice, and perceptions/reasons for antibiotic use.

## Study design and themes
- In-depth interviews used to explore:
  - Poultry rearing practices (care, feed, disease prevention, business decisions)
  - Waste-disposal practices (solid/liquid waste, carcasses, processing wastewater)
  - Biosecurity, hygiene, and personal protection for humans
  - Sources of advice and influences on decision making
  - Perceptions of antibiotics and reasons for use
- Formative research approach; no a priori sample size calculation; interviews continued until thematic saturation.

## Setting and participants
- Study locations: Mirzapur sub-district, Tangail District (rural) and Dhaka city (urban).
- Participant groups:
  - Rural poultry owners (n=3)
  - Small-scale commercial farm workers (n=10)
  - Urban live poultry market stall holders (n=5)
- Total: 18 interviews

## Data collection and instruments
- Method: In-depth interviews guided by interview guides tailored for each setting.
- Interview guides used to elicit relevant themes and topics.

## Sampling and saturation
- No formal sample size calculation due to formative nature.
- Sampling continued until saturation of responses and themes across settings.

## Ethics and consent
- Written informed consent obtained from all participants; right to withdraw at any time.
- Ethical clearance:
  - International Centre for Diarrhoeal Disease Research, Bangladesh (PR16071)
  - Loughborough University (R17-P037)

## Data handling and quality control
- Interviews conducted and transcribed by an experienced researcher from ICDDR,B.
- Transcripts checked against original audio recordings.

## Data structure and language
- Anonymised interview transcripts presented.
- Interviews conducted and transcribed in Bangla.
