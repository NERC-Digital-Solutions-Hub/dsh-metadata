# Water discharge data for Trout Beck, Cumbria.  1961 - 1980

## Overview
- Dataset of water discharge data for Trout Beck, Cumbria, UK (1961â€“1980).
- Collected by various staff at Moor House Research Station for the Wear and Tees River Board and the NRA.
- Names of recording staff not recorded.
- Stored in a database described in Cuthbertson (1993); linked in J. Env. Management 37(4): 291-300. DOI: https://doi.org/10.1006/jema.1993.1023

## Site location
- Trout Beck Weir located at grid reference 375900 533600.

## Variables in dataset
- DATID: Recording location
- REC_TIME: Date (recording day)
- TCMD: Total Mean Daily Discharge (cumecs)
- MEAN: Mean discharge (cumecs)
- HIGHEST: Highest discharge of the day (cumecs)
- LOWEST: Lowest discharge of the day (cumecs)
- HGH_TIME: Time of greatest discharge (hour)
- LOW_TIME: Time of lowest discharge (hour)

## Recording locations and temporal resolutions
- DATID indicates recording location; two locations are present:
  - 39: Trout Beck gauging station (data originally in Imperial units, converted to metric)
    - Earliest date: 01-OCT-1961; Last date: 30-SEP-1968
  - 17: TROUTBECK gauging station (recording from Northumbrian Water Authority)
    - Earliest date: 01-OCT-1968; Last date: 30-JUN-1980

## Data notes
- A large peak in discharge occurs in March 1979; possible relation to snow melt after a major UK snow event. No explanatory notes within the database.
