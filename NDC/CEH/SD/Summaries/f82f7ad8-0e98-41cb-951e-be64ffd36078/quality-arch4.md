# Field protocols and interview methodology

- Overview
  - Documents the procedures used to conduct interviews and focus groups, emphasizing multilingual fieldwork, training, validation, and data preservation.

- Key practices
  - Field protocols: Exercise field protocols prepared in Portuguese for each session.
  - Training of assistant/translator: Initial joint sessions with lead researcher and assistant; assistant trained on interview objectives and questioning method; later conducted exercises independently.
  - Triangulation and focusing: Repeated discussions across diverse groups to validate information and capture multiple perspectives; interview context informs and sharpens focus over time.
  - Composition of group: Village leader gathers a group per request; groups include men and women with explicit invitation for all to contribute.
  - Translation: A native-speaking translator accompanies the researcher; questions translated between Portuguese and Elomwe as needed.
  - Note taking: Recorded by the lead researcher and the assistant.
  - Discussion: Post-interview discussions with an assistant or colleague to improve understanding; clarification sought from locals when needed.
