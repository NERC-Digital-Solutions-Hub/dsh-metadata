# Field protocols

## 1) Field protocols
- Protocols prepared in Portuguese to align with local language and context.

## 2) Training of assistant/translator
- The lead researcher and one assistant/translator conducted initial exercises together.
- Assistant trained on interview objectives and question delivery.
- Later, the trained assistant conducted exercises independently.

## 3) Triangulation and focusing
- Repeated discussions with diverse groups to validate information.
- Context from interviews, conversations, and focus groups used to refine interview focus.

## 4) Composition of group
- Village leader recruited participants as requested.
- Focus groups included both men and women; ensured inclusive participation.

## 5) Translation
- Researcher always accompanied by a translator/native speaker.
- Questions translated between Portuguese and Elomwe as needed.

## 6) Note taking
- Notes recorded by the lead researcher and the assistant.

## 7) Discussion
- After each interview, information reviewed with the assistant or a colleague to enhance understanding.
- Clarification sought from locals when necessary.

## 9) Back-up

## Implications for GIS data work
- Standardized, multilingual field protocols support high-quality, auditable data suitable for GIS analyses.
- Triangulation and iterative focus improve reliability of information that can be geospatially contextualized.
- Clear documentation, translation, and backup practices facilitate metadata creation and data integration across datasets.
