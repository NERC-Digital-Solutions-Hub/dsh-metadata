# 1) Field protocols

- Field protocols were prepared in Portuguese for each exercise.

# 2) Training of assistant/translator

- Initially, exercises were conducted by the lead researcher with one assistant/translator.
- Before each exercise, the assistant was trained on the interview objective and how to ask questions.
- Later, the trained assistant conducted exercises by himself.

# 3) Triangulation and focusing

- Topics were discussed repeatedly with different groups/persons to validate information and highlight multiple perspectives.
- Context information from interviews, conversations, and focus group discussions helped gradually better focus the interviews.

# 4) Composition of group

- Usually, the village leader gathered a group of people as requested.
- Focus groups included both men and women.
- Everyone was explicitly invited to contribute.

# 5) Translation

- The researcher was accompanied by a translator/assistant fluent in the local language.
- All questions were translated between Portuguese and Elomwe as needed.

# 6) Note taking

- Notes were taken by the lead researcher and the assistant.

# 7) Discussion

- After each interview, information was discussed with the assistant or a colleague to improve understanding.
- If there was a lack of understanding, clarification was sought via communications with locals.

# 8) Transcript in English

- Back at the office, all notes were written in English.
- Notes taken by the assistant were recorded in Portuguese and translated to English.

# 9) Back-up

- Several backups were prepared from all documents.
