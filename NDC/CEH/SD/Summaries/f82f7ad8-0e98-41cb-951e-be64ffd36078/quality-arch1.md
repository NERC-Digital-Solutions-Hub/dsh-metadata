# Field protocols and data collection procedures

## Overview
- Field protocols for each exercise were prepared in Portuguese.

## Training of assistant/translator
- Initially, exercises were conducted by the lead researcher with one assistant/translator.
- Before each exercise, the assistant was trained on the interview objective and question approach.
- Later, the trained assistant conducted exercises independently.

## Triangulation and focusing
- Information was validated by discussing topics repeatedly with different groups or persons.
- Context information from interviews, conversations, and focus groups helped refine interview focus over time.

## Composition of group
- The village leader typically gathered a group as requested.
- Focus groups included both men and women, with an explicit invitation for everyone to contribute.

## Translation
- The researcher was accompanied by a translator/assistant fluent in the local language.
- Questions were translated between Portuguese and Elomwe as needed.

## Note taking
- Notes were taken by the lead researcher and the assistant.

## Discussion
- After each interview, information was discussed with the assistant or a colleague to improve understanding.
- If something was not understood, clarification was sought through local contacts.

## Transcript in English
- Back in the office, all notes were written in English.
- Notes taken by the assistant were recorded in Portuguese and translated to English.

## Back-up
- Several backups were prepared for all documents. 

## Data integrity and accessibility
- Triangulation and context-rich discussions support validity of findings.
- Multilingual data handling (Portuguese and Elomwe) with subsequent English transcription enhances accessibility for analysis.
- Systematic backups ensure data preservation and recoverability.
