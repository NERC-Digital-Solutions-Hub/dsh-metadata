# SOIL ANALYSIS OF RIGG FOOT, SOURHOPE : BLOCK 4 AUGER SAMPLES

## Overview
- Part of the Sourhope long-term monitoring program within the ECN framework.
- Provides detailed soil chemical and physical data from Block 4 auger samples, plus supporting Block 5 profiles and ECOTRON plot data.
- Includes associated vegetation survey data and metadata to support multi-parameter environmental health assessments.

## Sampling and Laboratory Analyses
- Auger sampling (Block 4): 30 cores per plot (0–30 cm) in a “W” pattern; samples bulked for analysis and archiving.
- Soil profiling (Block 5): 1 m profile pits described across horizons FH, H, Ah, AB, Bg, BCg, Cg; horizon-specific samples archived at -80°C.
- Measurements reported for each sample: weight (mg); %N; %C; C:N ratio; Ca, Na, K, Mg (meq/100 g); %Moisture loss; %Loss on Ignition (LOI); pH(H2O); pH(CaCl2).
- Data management: samples linked to MLURI codes and MLURI barcodes; results tabulated by site, block, plot, and subplot; archived samples stored at CEH Merlewood.

## Vegetation Surveys (Rigg Foot, Sourhope)
- Block-level surveys accompany soil data to relate floristic composition with soil profiles and treatments (e.g., depth horizons in Block 5; species totals per plot).
- Recorded species occurrences and totals across plots and subplots, illustrating floristic diversity associated with horizon development and land-use history.

## Data Management and Access
- Data organized by site, block, plot, and subplot; archived samples stored at CEH Merlewood (-80°C) to ensure long-term preservation.
- Data contributed to ECN long-term environmental monitoring; designed for cross-site comparability and integration with broader environmental datasets and data portals.
- Emphasis on reproducibility, data sharing, and leveraging underlying datasets for policy-relevant environmental health indicators.

## Implications for Monitoring and Policy-Relevant Insights
- Demonstrates long-term, standardized soil and vegetation monitoring at an upland site with acidic, organic-rich soils and distinct horizon development.
- Enables assessment of nutrient amendments (e.g., lime/N), grazing management effects, and their interactions with soil chemistry and vegetation dynamics.
- Data support understanding of soil–vegetation relationships, soil acidity, organic matter dynamics (LOI), and carbon/nitrogen cycling within hill-farming ecosystems.
- Provides a robust, scalable dataset framework for environmental health assessments and policy evaluation over time.
