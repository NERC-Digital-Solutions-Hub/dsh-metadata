# Windermere Lake Temperature 1944-2002

## Overview
- Inshore surface water temperature (°C) for Windermere, presented for 1944–2002 (data collection began in 1933).
- Part of a data set used in Vindenes et al. (2013) American Naturalist on climate impacts in freshwater ecosystems.
- Data custodians: Freshwater Biological Association (FBA) and Centre for Ecology & Hydrology (CEH) / Institute of Freshwater Ecology (IFE) predecessors.

## Data Details
- Format: Comma separated values (CSV)
- Size: 24 KB
- Columns:
  - TEMP, 1 = Annual mean surface temperature of the lake (°C)
  - YEAR, 1 = Year to which the annual temperature pertains

## Location and Sampling
- Location: Windermere Lake, Cumbria, Great Britain
- Approximate centre coordinates: OS Grid SD393960; WGS84 54.360193, -2.935836
- Sampling regime: Inshore surface temperature monitored near the middle of the lake’s long axis from 1933 to present.

## Methods
- Collection: Inshore surface temperature recorded to ±0.1 °C using a handheld mercury thermometer at around 09:00 h, at near-daily intervals.
- Data processing: Daily records aggregated to produce annual means (as described in Winfield et al. 2008).

## Quality Control and Documentation
- Quality control: Measurements checked by two individuals.
- Relevant references:
  - Winfield, I.J., Fletcher, J.M. & James, J.B. 2008. The Arctic charr populations of Windermere, UK: population trends associated with eutrophication, climate change and increased abundance of roach. Environmental Biology of Fishes 83, 25-35.
  - Associated dataset citation for temperature: Winfield et al. 2008.
