# HUMBER CATHCMENT

- Aim
  - To ensure their organisation is working effectively with data. They hold management roles focused on implementing effective data strategy, applying previous data analysis knowledge to think about data functions within their organisation or wider networks.

- Who they are
  - They hold management roles focused on implementing an effective data strategy.
  - They apply past data analysis knowledge to the organisational data function.
  - They consider how data serves a wider community and may work across networks of partners.

- How they meet their aims
  - They think about the whole data system.
  - They often understand user needs (e.g., policy colleagues) as an ongoing process, potentially encouraging 'co-ownership' of data products.
  - They understand what data is available and where gaps are, verifying data suitability, which helps prioritise efforts.
  - They find data through platforms, online searches, and reaching out to others.
  - They may commission new data and think creatively about data gathering.
  - They support good analytical, presentation, and dissemination practices within their team.
  - They ensure data assets are managed correctly (format, quality, metadata, discoverability, updates).
  - They ensure products meet user needs through feedback and iteration.
  - They may collaborate with the wider data community to support an effective system.

- Particular Challenges
  - Understanding user needs, especially for diverse public audiences.
  - Getting an overview of available data due to its scattered nature.
  - Lack of data, particularly at the required granularity.
  - Paywalls and data protection barriers hinder access.
  - Difficulty quickly verifying data content and format.
  - Lack of data standards, variable data quality, and unclear metadata.
  - Lack of coordination in the sector, duplicated effort, and interest in stronger communities of practice.
