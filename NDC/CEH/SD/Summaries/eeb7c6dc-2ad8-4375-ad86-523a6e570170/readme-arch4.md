# Audible and ultrasound recordings to survey birds and bats on Barro Colorado Island and in the Gamboa forest region, Panama, January 2023

## Overview
- Purpose: Survey birds and bats in Panama to trial the ability of song meter devices to record surrounding audio and ultrasound noise.
- Devices: Wildlife Acoustics SM4 (audible) and SM4Bat (ultrasound).
- Recording capabilities: Both devices operate for extended deployments; configurable sampling around sunrise and sunset.
- Data focus: Audible recordings (SM4) and ultrasound recordings (SM4Bat) collected January 21â€“26, 2023 across multiple sites.

## Data collection details
- Locations: Four sites in Barro Colorado Island (BCI) and the Gamboa forest region.
- Time windows:
  - Audible: around sunrise and sunset (e.g., 1 hour before/after sunset).
  - Ultrasound: during daylight hours (e.g., 30 minutes before sunset to 30 minutes after sunrise).
- Deployment data: Metadata.csv contains per-deployment details including survey start/end times and precise latitude/longitude.
- File organization:
  - Audible: folders named yy_mm_dd_Gamboa or yy_mm_dd_BCI (region indicated by folder name).
  - Ultrasound: folders named Bat_yy_mm_dd (date reflects survey start).
  - The yy_mm_dd portion corresponds to the survey start date.
- Documentation:
  - For a comprehensive overview of file naming structure, see dataset details on the EIDC catalogue.

## Data management and governance
- Metadata and discoverability: Metadata.csv provides key deployment metadata to support data understanding and reuse.
- Provenance and reproducibility: clear association of data with device models, dates, times, and locations; organized folder structure facilitates traceability.
- Community and standards: dataset designed to enable evaluation of data collection methods for acoustic biodiversity studies; alignment with established instrument configurations and naming conventions.
- Access and references: device product pages and EIDC catalogue entry linked for deeper instrument details and dataset context.
