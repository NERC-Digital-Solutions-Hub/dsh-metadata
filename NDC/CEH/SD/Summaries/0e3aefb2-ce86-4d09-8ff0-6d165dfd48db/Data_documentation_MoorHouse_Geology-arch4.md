# Geological map of the Moor House National Nature Reserve (1963)

## Dataset purpose and contents
- Shapefile: MH_Geology.shp
- Contains polygons representing rock types mapped in the 1950s/1960s
- Originally published as: "Geological Map of the Moor House National Nature Reserve (1963) Westmorland" in The Geology of Moor House (1963)
- Digitized in the 1990s by the Institute of Terrestrial Ecology (now the Centre for Ecology & Hydrology)

## Data structure and fields
- GEOLOGY_ID: numeric, arbitrary unique code per polygon
- GEOLOGY: text, description of rock type
- ERA: text, geological era
- Mapped rock types with accompanying codes include: ALLUVIAL FAN; FRESHWATER ALLUVIUM; LIMESTONE UPPER/MIDDLE/LOWER; LIMESTONE; BASEMENT GROUP; SKIDDAW SLATE SERIES; SANDSTONE; QUARTZ DOLERITE; WATER

## Spatial reference and extent
- Coordinate system: British National Grid (OSGB1936)
- Projection: Transverse Mercator
- Extent: Great Britain
- Original map resolution: 3 arc-minutes to a mile

## Data lineage and provenance
- Background: Moor House acquired as a National Nature Reserve in 1951; ecological work initiated
- Geological re-survey: started in 1954 (Johnson & Dunham, 1963)
- Survey conducted by G.A.L. Johnson under a grant from The Nature Conservancy
- Publication: The Geology of Moor House, a National Nature Reserve in north-east Westmorland (1963)
- Digitization: 1990s by Institute of Terrestrial Ecology (now CEH)

## Context and background
- Geological setting: Carboniferous rocks extensive within the Reserve; continuous sequence of upper Carboniferous beds best exposed on Great Dun Fell
- Key features: western escarpment exposures, Cross Fell Inlier foundations, Great Whin Sill (quartz-dolerite) within Carboniferous strata
- Mineralization: veins of lead, zinc, fluorite, barytes; Force Burn Vein exposed along the Reserve boundary
- Quaternary and recent deposits: glacial boulder clay, moraines, peat deposits up to >12 ft thick in some areas
