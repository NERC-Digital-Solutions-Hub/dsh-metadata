# WildCrickets06H_EarlyDominanceVsSurvival

## Dataset purpose
- Describes individual male crickets with lifespan classified by early-life fight dominance (high vs low) relative to the population median.
- Provides a simple, standardized set of fields to track survival and behavior over time.

## Variables and coding
- Year: calendar year in which the cricket was alive.
- Year alive: the year associated with the individualâ€™s lifespan entry.
- Lifespan: number of days the cricket lived.
- EarlyDominance: High (H) or Low (L) dominance in early life, determined by whether early-life dominance is above or below the population median.

## How this supports environmental monitoring
- Enables standardized analysis of how early behavioral dominance relates to survival.
- Suitable for integration with other environmental data to explore drivers of lifespan and behavior.
- Outputs can be formatted as reports, charts, or maps, and stored in data portals for reuse.
