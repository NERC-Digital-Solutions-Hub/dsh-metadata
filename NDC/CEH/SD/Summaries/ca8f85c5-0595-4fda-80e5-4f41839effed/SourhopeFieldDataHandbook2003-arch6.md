# SOIL PROFILE OF RIGG Foot, Sourhope : BLOCK 4

## Overview
- Detailed soil profile description, chemical analyses, auger samples, and vegetation data for Block 4 of the Rigg Foot/Sourhope site.
- Includes horizon-by-horizon description, quantitative soil chemistry (N, C, C:N, Ca, Mg, K, Mn? etc.), moisture loss, LOI, pH (H2O and CaCl2), and texture features.
- Supplements field sampling with associated vegetation surveys and links to Ecotron and long-term data context.

## Soil profile description (Block 4)
- Horizons and depths:
  - LF: 0–1 cm — fibrous, moist, no mineral grains, smooth boundary.
  - FH: 1–4 cm — fibrous, moist, abundant very fine fibrous roots, sharp smooth boundary.
  - H: 4–8 cm — black, amorphous, moist, weak medium angular blocky structure, roots.
  - Ah: 8–29 cm — dark reddish brown, sandy silt loam; moist; very friable; many fine roots; medium subangular igneous stones; sharp smooth boundary.
  - Abg: 29–40 cm — pinkish grey with brown mottles; coarse sandy silt loam; roots; abundant small igneous stones; sharp smooth boundary.
  - B sg: 40–61 cm — brown with strong brown mottles; sandy silt loam; moist; roots; many igneous stones; sharp smooth boundary.
  - C gx: 61–95 cm — reddish brown andesitic loam/clay loam; massive to platy structure; moist; few roots; abundant igneous stones.
- Visual indicators of soil development include increasing depth of organic-rich horizons up to the Ah, then transitional mineral horizons with mottling and igneous stone content in lower horizons.

## Vegetation survey (Block 4)
- Dominant vegetation characteristics across the plot set:
  - Dominant species include Agrostis capillaris, Anthoxanthum odoratum, Festuca rubra, Nardus stricta.
  - Other frequent species: Poa pratensis, Trifolium repens, Holcus mollis, Potentilla erecta, Galium saxatile.
  - Main community designation: U4d (Festuca ovina-Agrostis capillaris-Galium saxatile grassland with Luzula multiflora-Rhytidiadelphus loreus subcommunity); some plots align with U5b (Nardus stricta-Galium saxatile grassland) in moister blocks.
  - Indicator species identified: Festuca ovina, Rhytidiadelphus squarrosus, Deschampsia flexuosa, Deschampsia cespitosa, Agrostis canina, Holcus mollis, Ranunculus repens, Carex binervis, Juncus effusus, among others.
- Subplot-level patterns show localized associations guiding community delineations.

## Ecotron and long-term data context (Block 4)
- Ecotron plots and sub-plots enable controlled environment experiments to complement field treatments.
- Sourhope site part of the UK Environmental Change Network (ECN) with Micronet-funded work on soil microbial-plant interactions and nutrient cycling under varying management.

## Data access and references
- The data are part of a broader Soil Biodiversity Programme with Appendix A data tables and linked documents (Twinspan, Association analyses) for deeper exploration.
- Emphasizes long-term, multi-year data usefulness for upland grassland sustainability studies.
