# Metaschoepite dissolution in sediment column systems - groundwater/soil geochemistry and uranium X-ray spectroscopy data

## Overview
- A comprehensive data collection accompanying a study on metaschoepite dissolution and uranium speciation/transport in sediment column systems.
- Datasets cover solid and solution phase geochemistry, microbial communities, and uranium X-ray spectroscopy across oxic and electron-donor amended groundwater conditions.
- Timepoints include 6 and 12 months, with horizons sampled at 5 mm intervals; four metaschoepite-doped columns plus two controls.
- Full experimental details and data interpretation are described in the associated peer-reviewed publication.

## Experimental design and conditions
- Sediment columns doped with metaschoepite; subjected to flowing oxic or electron-donor amended synthetic groundwater.
- Reacted for 6 or 12 months before sacrifice and analysis.
- System types labeled as OXIC (oxic) or E-D AM (electron-donor amended).
- Sediment horizons quantified by distance from column base (in millimeters) for spatially resolved analyses.

## Data resources and datasets
- 1) Aqua_Regia_Sediment_Digests_ICPAES_UO3
  - Aqua regia extractable uranium and iron across multiple sediment horizons (5 mm intervals), across oxic and electron-donor amended systems, at 6 and 12 months.
- 2) Column_Effluents_ICPMS_Control_UO3
  - U, Fe, Mn, Mg concentrations in column effluents from four metaschoepite-doped columns and two controls; time series up to 12 months; includes pore volumes and system labels.
- 3) Column_Effluents_IC_Control_UO3
  - Ion chromatography data for effluent sulfate, nitrate, volatile fatty acids, nitrite, and phosphate; up to 12 months; system labels as above.
- 4) Column_Effluents_pH_Control_UO3
  - pH measurements of column effluents at selected time points; up to 12 months; system labels as above.
- 5) Sediment_digests_Ferrozine_UO3
  - Ferrozine-based Fe(II) proportion (as % of total Fe) in sediment horizons; 6- and 12-month systems; oxic and electron-donor amended.
- 6) Phylogenetic_abundance_control_UO3
  - Microbial sequencing data showing relative abundance of Fe- and sulfate-reducing strains across selected horizons; includes oxic and E-D AM conditions and timepoints.
- 7) Bulk_MicroXANES_UO3
  - Aligned U L3-edge XANES data (bulk and microfocus) from sediments and regions of elevated U; sections span oxic vs E-D AM and 6/12 months.
- 8) MicroEXAFS_UO3
  - Microfocus EXAFS data (U L3-edge) from discrete high-U regions; includes horizon, system type, and timepoint information.
- 9) BulkEXAFS_UO3
  - Core EXAFS data (U L3-edge) from discrete elevated-U regions; includes system type, timepoint, and horizon information.

## Data structure and labeling
- Datasets consistently labeled with system type (OXIC vs E-D AM), timepoint (6MO vs 12MO), and horizon (in millimeters) where applicable.
- Multiple complementary modalities (chemical concentrations, pH, redox-sensitive Fe(II), microbial community composition, and spectroscopic analyses) integrated to characterize uranium speciation and transport.

## Publication and access
- Primary study: Bower, W. R. et al. Metaschoepite Dissolution in Sediment Column Systems-Implications for Uranium Speciation and Transport, Environmental Science & Technology 2019, 53(16), 9915-9925.
- DOI: 10.1021/acs.est.9b02292
- Data and supporting documentation are available alongside the publication, with explicit dataset descriptions and file formats noted (e.g., .txt and .nxs for XRF maps).

## Relevance for monitoring and policy evaluation
- Demonstrates an integrated, multi-method approach to monitoring environmental health indicators related to uranium fate and transport in subsurface environments.
- Combines solid-phase and solution-phase chemistry, microbial ecology, and advanced spectroscopy to inform risk assessment and remediation strategies under varying redox conditions.
- Highlights data governance considerations for monitoring frameworks: need for robust metadata (system type, timepoint, horizon), data availability and sharing, harmonized data formats, and clear documentation to enable reuse and policy-informed decision-making.
