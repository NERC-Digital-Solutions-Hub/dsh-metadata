# SOIL ANALYSIS OF RIGG FOOT, SOURHOPE : BLOCK 4 AUGER SAMPLES

- Scope: Reporting chemical and physical properties from Block 4 auger samples at Rigg Foot, Sourhope, complemented by accompanying vegetation data and related site context from Block 5, ECOTRON plots, and broader Sourhope datasets.
- Key focus: elemental composition, soil chemistry, moisture, organic content, pH, and profile context to support environmental monitoring and basin-wide comparisons.

## Data collection and methods (Block 4 Auger Samples)

- Sampling units: Auger cores labelled 4A–4F, representing Block 4 locations.
- Measurements recorded per core:
  - Weight (Wt in mg)
  - %N, %C, C:N ratio
  - Cations: Ca, Na, K, Mg (meq/100g)
  - %Moisture loss and %Loss on Ignition (LOI)
  - pH: H2O and CaCl2
- Sample weights and key indicators:
  - Wt: 6.012 to 5.674 mg across samples
  - %N: 0.51 to 0.58
  - %C: 6.80 to 8.02
  - C:N: 12.62 to 14.26
  - Ca: 1.01 to 2.96 meq/100g
  - Na: 0.12 to 0.15 meq/100g
  - K: 0.58 to 0.81 meq/100g
  - Mg: 0.58 to 1.59 meq/100g
  - %Moisture loss: 4.29 to 5.15
  - LOI: 14.60 to 17.96
  - pH(H2O): 4.45 to 4.82
  - pH(CaCl2): 3.64 to 4.05
- General interpretation:
  - Soils are acidic (pH ~4.4–4.8) with moderate organic matter (LOI ~14–18%).
  - Nutrient concentrations (Ca, Mg, K, Na) show modest variability across the six samples.
  - Carbon content and C:N ratios indicate organic matter with typical soil processes in this environment.

## BLOCK 5: site details, soil profile, analyses, and vegetation

- Site details (Block 5):
  - Grid reference: NT8544019520
  - Altitude: 315 m; Slope: ~5°
  - Soil drainage: Imperfect
  - Major soil subgroup: Brown forest soil with gleying
  - Parent material: Undifferentiated intermediate igneous and andesite
- Soil profile (Block 5): soil horizons from surface to depth (0–100 cm)
  - Horizons described: LF, FH, H, Ah, AB, Bg, BCg, Cg
  - Key observations: dark surface Ah with high organic matter; AB and Bg horizons with mottling; BCg with red/yellow mottles; Cg horizon clay loam with abundant igneous stones; significant root density in upper horizons with increasing stone content and clay-downward texture
- Block 5 soil analysis (auger samples 5A–5F and related 5A–5F analyses):
  - Wt (mg): ~5.2–6.0 mg per sub-sample
  - %N: ~0.53–0.62 (5A–5F)
  - %C: ~7.0–9.07 (5A–5F)
  - C:N: ~12.9–14.7 (5A–5F)
  - Ca (meq/100g): 2.58–5.07 (5A–5D); up to 5.08 in Cg horizon
  - Na (meq/100g): 0.14–0.17
  - K (meq/100g): ~0.53–0.83
  - Mg (meq/100g): 1.51–3.50
  - %Moisture loss: 2.79–9.43
  - LOI: 4.83–67.00 (notably high in FH horizon)
  - pH(H2O): 3.53–4.95
  - pH(CaCl2): 3.53–4.14
- Auger sample analysis (Block 5, samples 5A–5F):
  - Reflect wide variation in nutrient content and acidity across horizons and sub-samples
  - Notable high LOI in FH horizon (67.00%) and variable Ca and Mg concentrations
  - pH values consistently acidic, with CaCl2 readings generally below 4.0 in several horizons
- Vegetation survey (Block 5):
  - Subplots (5A, 5B, 5C, 5D, 5E, 5F) show species totals and distributions across multiple taxa
  - Totals indicate diverse vegetation with varying abundance by subplot, contributing to soil-vegetation interaction analyses within Block 5

## Data management, sharing, and monitoring context

- Data storage and accessibility:
  - Results and derivative data stored on MLURI/Macaulay server; samples archived at -80°C for future analyses
  - Standardized methods enable cross-block, cross-plot temporal comparisons and reanalysis
- Monitoring context:
  - Part of Sourhope ECN site network; linked to Micronet program (1998–2002) for integrating soil microbial-plant interactions with broader environmental-change research
  - Data are designed to be combined with other environmental datasets to enhance long-term environmental health assessments and policy-relevant monitoring
- Reuse and value:
  - Underlying data and metadata prepared for external access; datasets intended to support broader environmental monitoring beyond single-output analyses

## End note: baseline significance for environmental monitoring

- The Block 4 and Block 5 soil analyses, together with vegetation surveys and horizon descriptions, establish a robust baseline for long-term monitoring of soil biodiversity, nutrient cycling, and grassland vegetation under controlled perturbations.
- The data provide standardized, repeatable methods for soil chemistry, soil profile characterization, and multivariate analyses, enabling data-driven assessments of land-use and management strategies over time.
- Emphasis on data sharing and interoperability supports scrutiny of environmental health and policy performance within a broader environmental monitoring framework.
