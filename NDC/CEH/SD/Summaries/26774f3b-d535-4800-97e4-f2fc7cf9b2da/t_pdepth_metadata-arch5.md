# High-resolution temperature and precipitation measurements for fire-affected locations in Australia (2019-2020) and Tenerife (2020-2021)

## Overview
- Dataset of high-resolution environmental measurements (temperature and precipitation depth) from two fire-affected locations: Madre del Agua, Tenerife, Spain, and Thompson reservoir, Victoria, Australia.
- Data collected at 10-minute intervals.

## Data files
- T_Pdepth_ma.csv (Madre del Agua, Tenerife)
- T_Pdepth_ts.csv (Thompson reservoir, Australia)

## What, Where, When, How, Why
- What: Temperature (°C) and precipitation depth (mm) at 10-minute intervals.
- Where: Madre del Agua (Tenerife) and Thompson reservoir (Victoria, Australia); spatial metadata included.
- When: Madre del Agua 17/11/2020 to 19/11/2021; Thompson 28/03/2019 to 13/01/2020.
- How: RainWise RainGauge coupled to Onset HOBO pendant datalogger UA-003-64.
- Why: Monitoring environmental parameters related to runoff occurrence.

## Who
- Authors: Dr Jonay Neris Tome, Dr Carmen Sánchez-García, Dr Cristina Santín, Prof. Stefan Doerr, Dr Gary Sheridan.

## Completeness and quality
- Completeness: Full record for both sites.
- Sensor and data cadence are clearly defined, with standard fields for time, temperature, and precipitation depth.

## Data structure and fields
- Features: Date and time (dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm), Temperature (°C), Precipitation depth (mm).
- Periodicity: 10 minutes.
- Observations: Madre del Agua (9,723 records); Thompson (10,050 records).

## Spatial details
- Madre del Agua, Tenerife, Spain: UTM 28N 28R, coordinates 343340, 3119657.
- Thompson Reservoir, Victoria, Australia: UTM 55H, coordinates 441956, 5822296.
