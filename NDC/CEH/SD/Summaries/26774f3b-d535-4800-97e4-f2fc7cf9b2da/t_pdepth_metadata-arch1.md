# Dataset name: High-resolution temperature and precipitation measurements for fire-affected locations in Australia (2019-2020) and Tenerife (2020-2021)

## Overview
- High-resolution (10-minute interval) measurements of temperature and precipitation depth for two fire-affected locations.
- Two CSV data files accompany the dataset.

## Data files
- T_Pdepth_ma.csv
- T_Pdepth_ts.csv

## What and where
- What: Temperature and precipitation depth recorded at 10-minute intervals.
- Where:
  - Madre del Agua (Tenerife, Spain)
    - UTM: 28N 28R, 343340 3119657
    - Data file: T_Pdepth_ma.csv
  - Thompson reservoir (Victoria, Australia)
    - UTM: 55H, 441956 5822296
    - Data file: T_Pdepth_ts.csv

## When
- Madre del Agua: 17/11/20 to 19/11/2021
- Thompson reservoir: 28/03/19 to 13/01/2020

## How (instrumentation)
- RainWise Rainew raingauge coupled to Onset HOBO pendant datalogger (UA-003-64)

## Why
- Monitoring environmental parameters related to runoff occurrence in fire-affected locations.

## Completeness and data content
- Completeness: Full record
- Features (fields): Date and time (dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm), temperature (Temp: °C), precipitation depth (P: mm)
- Periodicity: 10 minutes
- Observations: Madre del Agua 9723; Thompson 10050
- Treatments / Replication / Controls: NA

## Potential uses
- Analyze correlations between temperature and precipitation in fire-affected areas.
- Compare hydrological responses between Tenerife and Australia.
- Develop and validate models of runoff occurrence following fire events at high temporal resolution.
