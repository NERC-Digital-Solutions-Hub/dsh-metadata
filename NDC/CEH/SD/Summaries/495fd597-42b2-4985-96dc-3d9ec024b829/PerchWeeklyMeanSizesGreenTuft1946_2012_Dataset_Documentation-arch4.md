# Perch weekly mean sizes from Green Tuft 19462012

- Overview
  - A dataset of weekly mean sizes (cm) of mature perch (Perca fluviatilis) from Green Tuft, Windermere, Cumbria, sampled 1946–2012.
  - Tracing lineage from Freshwater Biological Association (FBA) to Centre for Ecology & Hydrology (CEH) and its predecessor IFE since 1989.
  - Used in broader ecological analyses (e.g., Ohlberger et al. 2014) on phenology and population responses.

- Data content and structure
  - File: PerchWeeklyMeanSizesGreenTuft1946_2012.csv; ~10 KB; CSV format.
  - Columns: Year, Week, Mean_size (cm), QA_code (NS = week not sampled; ID = week with insufficient data).
  - Location details: Green Tuft, Windermere north basin; approximate grid NY374019; OS Easting/Northing 337402, 501968.

- Temporal and spatial coverage
  - Temporal: 1946–2012.
  - Spatial: Green Tuft sampling site in Windermere’s north basin; multi-year trapping regime along the perch spawning ground.

- Sampling design
  - Perch monitored in north and south basins of Windermere; Green Tuft in north basin data presented here.
  - Six weekly trap sets per spawning season; five unbaited wire-netting traps deployed 2–7 m deep; daylight setting and lifting; typical collection window late April to early June.

- Collection and laboratory methods
  - Traps: 740 × 590 × 640 mm with 12 mm mesh; opening at one end; 6 weeks of sampling per year.
  - Processing: all perch enumerated, measured (total length to 1 mm), weighed (to 1 g), and sex/reproductive state determined.

- Data processing and outputs
  - Weekly mean size of mature perch derived from the processed catch.
  - Quality control: measurements validated by permutations of three individuals.

- Provenance and references
  - Original collection by FBA; CEH/IFE involvement since 1989.
  - Related methodological and metadata references: Paxton et al. 2004; Winfield et al. (data centre citations) 2014; Ohlberger et al. 2014.
