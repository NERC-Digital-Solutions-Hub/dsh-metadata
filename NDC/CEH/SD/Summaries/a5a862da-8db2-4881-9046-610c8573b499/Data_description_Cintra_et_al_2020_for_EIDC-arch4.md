# Amazon tree ring isotope records of Macrolobium acaciifolium and Cedrela odorata

- Overview
  - Open-access dataset of tree-ring oxygen (δ18Otr) and carbon (δ13Ctr) isotope data from five Amazon basin sites.
  - Two species chosen to represent different forest types: Macrolobium acaciifolium (seasonal floodplain) and Cedrela odorata (terra firme).
  - Spatial coverage across Peru, Colombia, and Bolivia to capture geographic variation in isotope signals related to climate.

- Data content and scope
  - Isotopes: δ18Otr (oxygen) and δ13Ctr (carbon).
  - Resolution: high-resolution sub-annual or annual ring data.
  - Sites: Pacaya (Peru), Maranon (Peru), Leticia (Colombia), Manuripi (Bolivia), Riberalta (Bolivia).
  - Year: Year of ring formation (per sample).
  - Data accessibility: Data prepared and measured across specified laboratories with clear provenance.

- Data structure and standards
  - Data fields:
    - Year
    - Delta (isotope value relative to standard; permille)
    - Isotope (δ18O or δ13C)
    - Data_type (high-resolution sub-annual or annual)
    - Ring_subsection (ring count for high-resolution sampling)
    - Core (increment core ID)
    - Species (scientific name)
    - Site (location name)
    - Site_longitude
    - Site_latitude
  - Standards:
    - δ13C reference: Vienna-PDB
    - δ18O reference: Standard Mean Ocean Water (SMOW)

- Provenance, processing, and collaboration
  - Sample preparation: University of Leeds (ring section cutting, cellulose extraction, weighing, packing).
  - Isotope measurements: Stable Isotope Laboratory, University of Leicester.
  - Funding: Multiple NERC-UK grants and Brazilian CNPQ scholarships.
  - Dataset citation: Cintra, B.; Boom, A.; Schöngart, J.; Gloor, E.; Brienen, R. (2020). Amazon tree ring isotope records of Macrolobium acaciifolium and Cedrela odorata. NERC Environmental Information Data Centre (Dataset). DOI provided; Open Government Licence.
