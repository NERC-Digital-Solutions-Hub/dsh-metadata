## Data Support archetype: Overview

- Aim: Help others explore data and obtain insights by finding, combining, analyzing data, and creating accessible data products (dashboards, pivot tables, reports) for self-service and broader use.
- Who they are: Data analysts who support a wide range of topics, often in less specialized organisations (e.g., local councils, private companies); strong data analysis and communication skills; may work on policy support and public data communication.
- How they meet their aims:
  - Understand the ask: determine whatâ€™s needed, why, and the best approach.
  - Source and verify sufficient data; consult topic experts when needed.
  - Clean, transform, and combine data from multiple sources; quality assure outputs.
  - Analyse patterns to identify significant features.
  - Create self-serve products (dashboards, pivot tables) and provide guidance on use; produce reports and charts; promote outputs and gather feedback.
  - Share early prototypes; advocate for better data creation where possible.
- Particular challenges:
  - Time spent understanding the ask due to limited data consideration in broader teams.
  - Patchy data across a wide range of formats and systems.
  - Difficulty communicating complex information simply.
  - Promoting outputs to drive wider use and re-use.
