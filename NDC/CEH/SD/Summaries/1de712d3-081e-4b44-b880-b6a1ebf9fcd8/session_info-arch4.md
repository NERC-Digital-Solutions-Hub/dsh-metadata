## How Data Leaders meet their aims

- Think in terms of the whole data system and lifecycle.
- Engage with user needs as an ongoing process, promoting co-ownership of data products where feasible.
- Assess what data is available, identify gaps, and verify data suitability to prioritize work.
- Use multiple channels to discover data (platforms, online searches, outreach to others) and may commission new data.
- Promote good analytical, presentation, and dissemination practices within their teams.
- Ensure data assets are managed properly (format, quality, metadata, discoverability, updates).
- Iterate on products through user feedback to align with needs.
- Collaborate with the wider data community to strengthen the data ecosystem.

## Particular Challenges (detailed)

- Understanding user needs across diverse public audiences.
- Obtaining an overview of available data due to fragmentation.
- Lack of data, especially at needed granularity.
- Paywalls and data protection barriers limiting access.
- Difficulty quickly verifying data content and format.
- Absence of data standards, variable data quality, and unclear metadata.
- Coordination gaps in the sector, duplicated efforts, and limited communities of practice.
