# PANG LAMBOURN CATCHMENT: RIVER WATER QUALITY SITE (LCWQ)

## Overview
- Focuses on the Pang Lambourn Catchment river water quality site within the LCWQ framework, highlighting data-related aspects such as collection, management, and accessibility.

## Data Landscape and Stakeholders
- Identify and engage key users (e.g., policy colleagues) and consider co-ownership of data products.
- Understand how data serves a wider network of partners and communities.

## Data Availability, Access, and Gaps
- Map available data sources and identify gaps, including data at needed granularity.
- Address barriers to access such as paywalls and data protection constraints.
- Improve data discoverability and understand where data resides.

## Data Quality, Standards, and Metadata
- Verify data suitability and quality, including format and provenance.
- Establish and apply clear metadata standards to support reuse and interoperability.
- Recognize inconsistent or unclear metadata as a barrier to effective use.

## Governance, Coordination, and Collaboration
- Coordinate data activities across teams and sectors to reduce duplication.
- Foster communities of practice and wider collaboration to strengthen data systems.

## Recommendations for Data Leaders
- Map the full data ecosystem around the Pang Lambourn Catchment site.
- Define and develop data products that meet user needs with iterative feedback.
- Implement robust data governance, including format, quality, metadata, and updates.
- Ensure data assets are highly discoverable and accessible, while complying with protection requirements.
- Promote data standards and interoperability across the sector.
- Build partnerships and participate in broader data community efforts to enhance the LCWQ system.

## Risks and Mitigations
- Risks include limited data granularity, access barriers, poor metadata, and fragmented coordination.
- Mitigations involve proactive data cataloging, stakeholder engagement, standardized metadata, and cross-sector collaboration.
