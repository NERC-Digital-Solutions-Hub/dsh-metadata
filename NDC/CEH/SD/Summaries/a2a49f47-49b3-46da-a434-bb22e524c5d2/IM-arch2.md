# IM Protocol

## Purpose and Context
- MOTHS aims to collect and identify daily macrolepidoptera (butterflies and moths) using a standard Rothamsted light trap.
- Lepidoptera are used as an indicator group for environmental change; the protocol leverages the Rothamsted Insect Survey (RIS) expertise and a 25-year national database to analyze population trends.
- Data are stored in RIS and ECN databases to support long-term monitoring and trend analysis.

## Sampling Methodology
- Sampling follows the Rothamsted Insect Survey light-trap methodology; daily empties preferred, but samples can be accumulated (e.g., weekends) if needed.
- Specimens are posted to Rothamsted for identification unless local expertise is available.
- For large sites, multiple traps are desirable if operators are available.
- Time commitments: trap emptying about 40 minutes per trap per week plus travel; identification up to 15 days per site per year for highly experienced identifiers.

## Equipment and Site Setup
- Equipment: standard RIS light trap (approx. 1.2 m stand; 200 W tungsten lamp; mains electricity; RCD). Lamp operates from dusk to dawn with an automatic timer adjusted for daylength.
- Collection method: insects are collected in a jar treated with approximately 5 ml tetrachloroethane to immobilize specimens for transfer to storage boxes.
- Site requirements: trap near the TSS when possible, near laboratories/homes/farms for access, sheltered by vegetation, and ideally more than 20 m from artificial light.

## Operational Procedure
- Daily routine (RIS staff): verify RCD, ensure clock/time is correct (GMT), confirm lamp works, collect sample using a tetrachloroethane-dosed jar, transfer insects to tissue-lined boxes, re-dose jar for next use, label boxes with site name and date.
- Date conventions: the date should reflect the period the trap was set to the period it was emptied; if samples span multiple nights, use the appropriate multi-night date range (e.g., 27/30 January 1995); if using two boxes, annotate 1/2 accordingly.
- Non-operation reporting: document any dates when the trap did not operate (e.g., lamp failure).

## Safety and Compliance
- Tetrachloroethane: effective for rapid killing and 24+ hour persistence but toxic; precautions include avoiding vapour inhalation, skin/eye contact, ventilation, PPE, and proper spill handling.
- Electricity safety: operate on mains power with power-off procedures before bulb changes; consult a electrician for exposed circuits; regular RCD testing per manufacturer guidance.
