# NERC Data Repository - Supporting Information

- Overview
  - Documents data availability for the manuscript “Exploring the smart-natural city interface; re-imagining and re-integrating urban planning and governance” and the data repository “Record of expert inputs shaping future city discourses for Urban Living Birmingham.”
  - Describes the multi-disciplinary, anonymised expert inputs and the outputs from the Birmingham Innovation Workshop (12 September 2017) and subsequent discussions (Sept–Nov 2017).

- Data and outputs included in the repository
  - Record of group/table outputs from the Innovation Workshop
  - Photographs of collective ‘stickies’ contributions
  - Original transcripts of semi-structured interviews
  - Records of group telephone or meeting conversations
  - Work-in-progress collations of comments for sharing with contributors and co-authors

- Purpose and use of transcripts
  - Transcripts used to identify new ways of integrating urban futures discourses, particularly shaping the Smart City – Natural City interface with Birmingham as a case study

- Researchers, institutions and project context
  - Mike Grace and Prof. David Proverbs (Birmingham City University)
  - Prof. Alister Scott (Northumbria University)
  - Prof. Jonathan Sadler (University of Birmingham)
  - Nick Grayson (Birmingham City Council)
  - Funded by Research Councils UK (RCUK) and Innovate UK as part of the Urban Living Partnership (EP/P002021/1), 2015–2018; Urban Living Birmingham project (one of 5 ULP cities)

- Data governance, access, and anonymisation
  - Manuscript describes anonymised experts; data sharing involves underlying data, with potential barriers related to anonymisation and governance
  - Includes visual data (photographs) and transcripts; data capture of expert input materials

- Relevance for monitoring frameworks
  - Demonstrates collecting and organizing multi-disciplinary expert inputs to identify policy-relevant environmental health and urban governance measures
  - Highlights importance of data provenance, metadata, and transparency to support verification, reuse, and governance of data
  - Provides a concrete example of stakeholder engagement workflows and data management practices informing monitoring design and future decision-making
