# NERC Data Repository - Supporting Information

## Overview
- Describes multi-disciplinary anonymised experts who contributed to research on urban futures, focusing on the Smart City – Natural City interface with Birmingham as a case study.
- Includes a meta-table that organises and summarizes outputs from the Innovation Workshop (Birmingham, 12 Sept 2017) and subsequent group discussions (Sept–Nov 2017) plus deeper dive conversations.

## Data in the repository
- Record of group/table outputs from the Innovation Workshop.
- Photographs of collective “stickies” contributions from the workshop.
- Original transcripts of semi-structured interviews.
- Records of group telephone or meeting conversations.
- Work-in-progress collations of comments shared with contributors and co-authors.

## Data provenance and purpose
- Transcripts and outputs were used to identify new ways of integrating urban futures discourses.
- Specifically shaped the Smart City – Natural City interface, using Birmingham, UK as a case study.

## People and organisations
- Researchers: Mike Grace; Prof. David Proverbs (Birmingham City University); Prof. Alister Scott (Northumbria University); Prof. Jonathan Sadler (University of Birmingham); Nick Grayson (Birmingham City Council).
- Institutions: Birmingham City University; Northumbria University; University of Birmingham; Birmingham City Council.

## Funding and project context
- Supported by Research Councils UK (RCUK) and Innovate UK under the Urban Living Partnership (grant EP/P002021/1), 2015–2018.
- Part of the Urban Living Birmingham project, one of five ULP cities.

## Data access and usability for practice
- Data and accompanying materials are anonymised and described to support reuse in similar governance or urban planning research.
- Available as a data depository with transcripts, workshop outputs, and multimedia records (including photographs) to enable analysis, replication, and development of data products for end users in urban living contexts.
