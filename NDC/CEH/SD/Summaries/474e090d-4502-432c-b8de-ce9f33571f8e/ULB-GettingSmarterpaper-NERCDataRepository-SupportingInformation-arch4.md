# NERC Data Repository - Supporting Information

- The manuscript describes multi-disciplinary anonymised experts who contributed to the research on exploring the smart-natural city interface and re-imagining urban planning and governance.
- A meta-table organises and summarises outputs from the Birmingham Innovation Workshop (12 September 2017) and subsequent group discussions and deeper dive conversations (Sept–Nov 2017).

## Data repository contents

- Record of group or table outputs from the Innovation Workshop.
- Copies of photographs of collective 'stickies' contributions from the workshop.
- Original transcript records of semi-structured interview conversations.
- Records of group telephone or meeting conversations.
- "Work in progress" collations of comments received, shared with contributors and co-authors.

## How the data informed the research

- Transcripts were used to identify new ways of integrating urban futures discourses, with a focus on shaping the Smart City - Natural City interface.
- Birmingham served as a case study for these explorations.

- The data were collected from expert inputs and described in the manuscript; the supporting information emphasises anonymised expert contributions.
