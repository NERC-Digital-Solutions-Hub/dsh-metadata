# Interview guideline

- Purpose and scope: Guidelines with questions in Portuguese for interviews with different actor groups.

- Training and roles: Lead researcher works with an assistant/translator; pre-interview training on objectives and questioning approach; the assistant may conduct exercises independently later.

- Triangulation and focus: Repeated discussions with various groups to validate information and capture multiple perspectives; context from interviews, conversations, and focus groups helps refine interview focus.

- Group composition: Village leader typically gathers participants; focus groups include men and women; everyone is explicitly invited to contribute.

- Translation and language: Translator/assistant fluent in the local language accompanies the researcher; questions translated between Portuguese and Elomwe as needed.

- Note taking: Observations and notes recorded by the lead researcher and the assistant.

- Discussion and clarification: Post-interview discussions with the assistant or colleagues to improve understanding; clarify uncertainties with locals as needed.

- Transcription and language transfer: In the office, notes are transcribed into English; assistant notes in Portuguese are translated to English.

- Data backups: Multiple backup copies prepared for all documents.
