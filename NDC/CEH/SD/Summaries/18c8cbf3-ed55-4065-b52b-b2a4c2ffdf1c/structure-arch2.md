# Details of data structure

- Dataset scope
  - Contains 19 RTF documents; transcripts from interviews conducted in various villages.
  - Interviewees’ names are kept anonymous.

- Focus Group Discussions (FGD)
  - FGD_Mahara: 6 small-scale soya producers (2 women, 4 men); ~1 hour; data: 19.10.2015; transcript in English; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe.
  - FGD_Mcunua: 5 producers (5 men + school coordinator); ~1 hour; data: 10.10.2015; transcript in English; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe.
  - FGD_Natxoa: 5 producers (3 men, 2 women); ~1 hour; data: 19.10.2015; transcript in English; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe.
  - FGD_Nuisse: 4 producers (4 men, secretario); ~1 hour; data: 10.10.2015; transcript in English; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe.
  - FGD_Moquito: 6 producers (6 men); ~1 hour; data: 24.10.2015; transcript in English; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe; question catalogue with 33 questions.
  - FGD_Serra: 8 producers (4 men, 4 women); ~1 hour; data: 11.10.2015; transcript in English; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe.
  - FGD_Muhelo: 5 producers (5 men); ~1 hour; data: 25.11.2015; transcript in Portuguese and English; 33-question catalogue; notes in Elomwe.
  - FGD_Nakuphi: 8 producers (6 men, 2 women); ~1 hour; data: 25.11.2015; transcript in Portuguese and English; notes in Elomwe; 33-question catalogue.
  - FGD_Namiepe: 6 producers (4 men, 2 women); ~1.5 hours; data: 22.10.2015; transcript in Portuguese and English; notes in Elomwe; 33-question catalogue.
  - FGD_Palacue: 8 producers (5 men, 3 women); ~1 hour; data: 25.11.2015; transcript in Portuguese and English; notes in Elomwe; 33-question catalogue.
  - FGD_emergent farmer: 4 emergent soya farmers (3 men, 1 woman) supported by Technoserve; ~1 hour; data: 11.10.2015; transcript in English; notes; translator: Portuguese-Elomwe.

- Interviews (non-FGD)
  - Interview_TechnoServe: one TechnoServe technician; open interview ~1 hour; data: 14.07.2015; conducted in Portuguese; transcript in English.
  - Interviews_trader1: traders with buying points along main roads; ~10 minutes each; Portuguese; data: 11.07.2015–16.07.2015; transcript in English.
  - Interviews_trader2: two informal traders in Gurue; ~20 minutes each; Portuguese; data: 22.10.2015 and 03.12.2015; transcripts in English.
  - interview_trader.large: manager of a larger trading company; questions answered in Portuguese; data: 08.12.2015 and 03.12.2015; transcript in English and Portuguese.
  - Interview_company1: management of a private soya-producing company; semi-structured; ~1.5 hours; data: 11.10.2015; conducted in English; transcript in English.
  - Interview_company2: management of another private company; ~1 hour; data: 16.07.2015; conducted in English; transcript in English.
  - Interview_company3: management of a third private company; ~1.5 hours; data: 08.07.2015; conducted in English; transcript in English.
  - Interview_SDAE: technician from SDAE Gurue (Serviços Distritais das Actividades Económicas); Portuguese; data: 16.07.2015; transcript in English.

- Language and translation workflow
  - Transcripts created in English; some originals in Portuguese or Elomwe.
  - Notes taken in Elomwe and translated to Portuguese and English where applicable.
  - Anonymization applied to interviewees.

- Temporal and logistical context
  - Data collection primarily between July and December 2015.
  - Mix of group discussions and individual interviews; some sessions used a 33-question catalogue for structure.

- Data management notes relevant to monitoring analysts
  - Each document corresponds to a separate village or stakeholder group.
  - Transcripts provide qualitative insights from producers, traders, technicians, and private companies along the soya value chain.
  - Anonymization and multilingual translation considerations are documented, highlighting potential translation-related nuances in analysis.

- Relevance for environmental monitoring
  - Captures stakeholder perspectives on agricultural practices, markets, and institutional interactions that may influence environmental health and policy performance (e.g., input use, sustainability of soya production, supply chain dynamics).
  - Dataset structure supports standardized analysis across villages with consistent participant categorization and interview formats.
