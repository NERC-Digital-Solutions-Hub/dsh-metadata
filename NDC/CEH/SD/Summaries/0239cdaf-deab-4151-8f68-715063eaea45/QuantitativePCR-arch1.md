# Procedure for Real-time PCR assay

## Objective
- Assess the presence of bla CTX-M-1 and bla NDM-1 in environmental and human fecal samples using quantitative PCR (qPCR).

## Equipment
- CFX96 Touch Real-Time PCR Detection System (Bio-Rad)
- PCR hood with UV light
- Vortex mixer
- Rotor
- Refrigerator (2–8°C)
- Refrigerator (-20°C)

## Reagents

- Amplification and detection of the bla CTX-M-1 (159 bp) and bla NDM-1 (152 bp) amplicons using TaqMan technology (Bio-Rad)
- In-house designed primer sequences:
  - bla CTX-M-1 F: 5-ACGTGGCGATGAATAAGCTG-3
  - bla CTX-M-1 R: 5-CCCGAGGTGAAGTGGTATCA-3
  - bla NDM-1 F: 5-CAA CAC AGC CTG ACT TTC GC-3
  - bla NDM-1 R: 5-CAG CCA CCA AAA GCG ATG TC-3
- In-house designed probes:
  - bla CTX-M-1 Probe: 5-FAM-ACGTTAAACACCGCCATTCC-3BHQ
  - bla NDM-1 Probe: 5-FAM-TGGCCCGCTCAAGGTATTTT-3BHQ
- Probes labeled with 6-carboxyfluorescein (FAM) at 5' and Black Hole Quencher (BHQ) at 3'

## Methods

- Optimization
  - Evaluate different primer concentrations: 200, 300, 500, 900 nM
  - Evaluate different probe concentrations: 100, 200, 300 nM
  - Determine best detection limits; final recommended concentrations: primers 500 nM, probe 200 nM
- Master mix (20.0 µL total)
  - 10.0 µL of SsoAdvanced Universal Pro mix (2x) (Bio-Rad)
  - 0.2 µL of each primer (500 nM)
  - 0.2 µL of probe (200 nM)
  - 7.4 µL nuclease-free water
  - 2.0 µL of the DNA sample
- qPCR cycling conditions
  - 3.0 min at 95°C
  - 35 cycles: 15 sec at 95°C, 25 sec at 57°C

## Interpretation of qPCR results using standard curve

- A plasmid containing conserved sequences of both bla CTX-M-1 and bla NDM-1 is considered positive
- Construct a standard curve by measuring CT values across a range of plasmid DNA concentrations
  - Plot CT (Y-axis) versus log DNA quantity (X-axis)
- Quantify bla CTX-M-1 and bla NDM-1 using the standard curve according to Naas et al., 2011

## Reference

- Naas T, Ergani A, Carrèr A, Nordmann P. Real-time PCR for detection of NDM-1 carbapenemase genes from spiked stool samples. Antimicrob Agents Chemother. 2011 Sep;55(9):4038-43.
