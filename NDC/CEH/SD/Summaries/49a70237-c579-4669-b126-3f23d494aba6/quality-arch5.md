# Field protocols and data collection procedures

- Overview
  - Describes field work procedures including language-specific field protocols (Portuguese), training of an assistant/translator, triangulation to validate information, group composition for focus groups, translation workflow, note-taking, post-interview discussion, English transcription, and data backups.

- Field protocols and training
  - Field protocols are prepared in Portuguese for each exercise.
  - Training starts with the lead researcher and an assistant/translator; the assistant is trained on interview objectives and questioning approach, then conducts exercises independently.

- Triangulation and focus
  - Information is validated by discussing topics with varied groups and individuals.
  - Context from interviews, conversations, and focus groups helps progressively sharpen interview focus.

- Group composition
  - Village leader assembles groups as requested; focus groups include both men and women with broad invitation to contribute.

- Translation
  - A translator/assistant fluent in the local language accompanies the researcher.
  - Questions are translated between Portuguese and Changana as needed.

- Note taking and discussion
  - Notes are taken by the lead researcher and the assistant.
  - After each interview, information is discussed with the assistant or a colleague to improve understanding; clarifications sought from locals if necessary.

- Transcription
  - In the office, notes are written in English; assistant notes are recorded in Portuguese and translated to English.

- Back-ups
  - Multiple backups are created for all documents.
