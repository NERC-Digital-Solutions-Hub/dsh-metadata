# Details of data structure

- Overview
  - Dataset contains transcripts of interviews conducted in study villages.
  - 10 RTF and 8 CSV documents.
  - Interviewee names are anonymized.

- Datasets and content
  - Seasonal_calendar (3)
    - Led by a researcher and a translator (Portuguese-Changana) with 3–8 villagers in three study villages.
    - Field protocol followed; notes in Portuguese/English.
    - English transcripts compiled after field work.
  - Trend_analysis (7)
    - Led by the lead researcher and a translator (Portuguese-Changana) with 3–8 villagers in each study village.
    - Field protocol followed; notes in Portuguese/English.
    - English transcripts compiled after field work.
  - Village_profiles (1)
    - Two-week pre-visit in July 2014; 11 villages in Mabalane visited.
    - Interviews with village leaders and/or other representative people.
    - Data recorded in Portuguese and English; transcripts compiled after field work.
  - Wealthranking_qual (7)
    - Qualitative information recoded during wealth rankings.
    - In each study village a wealth ranking was undertaken.
    - Data recorded in Portuguese and English; transcripts compiled after field work.

- Data formats and languages
  - Transcripts available in RTF and CSV formats.
  - Languages used: Portuguese and English; some interactions supported by a translator (Portuguese-Changana).

- Collection and transcription process
  - Fieldwork followed a defined protocol.
  - Transcripts compiled after field work; interview content anonymized.
  - Notes and transcripts reflect collaboration between researchers, translators, and villagers.

- Anonymization and provenance
  - Interviewee names are kept anonymous.
  - Data originate from field visits to villages in the Mabalane area during 2014.

- Potential uses for Data Support
  - Create searchable, cross-dataset views (seasonal calendars, trends, village profiles, and wealth rankings).
  - Develop dashboards or pivot-table style summaries to enable self-serve exploration by end users.
  - Combine datasets to analyze patterns across villages and over time (e.g., linking wealth rankings with seasonal insights).
  - Produce reports and visualizations that communicate findings in plain language to stakeholders.

- Access, quality, and limitations
  - Data are qualitative and depend on translations; consistency across languages and formats may vary.
  - Anonymization helps privacy but may limit re-identification or follow-up with specific respondents.
  - Field-protocol adherence and documentation of translations are critical for data quality and reuse.
