# Field protocols

- Purpose: Establish standardized procedures for data collection via interviews and focus groups, with emphasis on translation, documentation, and data quality.

- Field protocols language: Prepared in Portuguese to ensure clear guidance for field teams.

- Training of assistant/translator:
  - Initially conducted by the lead researcher with one assistant/translator.
  - Assistant trained on interview objectives and question delivery.
  - Later, the trained assistant conducts exercises and interviews independently.

- Triangulation and focusing:
  - Repeated discussions with different groups/persons to validate information.
  - Context from interviews, conversations, and focus groups helps progressively refine interview focus.

- Composition of group:
  - Village leader invites a group according to guidance.
  - Focus groups include both men and women; everyone is explicitly invited to contribute.

- Translation:
  - Researcher is always accompanied by a translator fluent in the local language.
  - Questions translated between Portuguese and Changana as needed.

- Note taking:
  - Taken by both the lead researcher and the assistant.

- Discussion:
  - After each interview, information is discussed with the assistant or a colleague to improve understanding.
  - Clarifications sought from locals when necessary.
