# Details of data structure

- Dataset scope and formats
  - Contains 18 documents in total: 10 RTF and 8 CSV files.
  - Each document holds transcripts from interviews conducted in one village.
  - Interviewees are anonymized.

- Study components and document counts
  - Seasonal_calendar: 3 documents
    - Led by a researcher and a translator (Portuguese – Changana) with groups of 3–8 villagers per village.
    - Field notes in Portuguese/English; English transcripts compiled after fieldwork.
  - Trend_analysis: 7 documents
    - Led by the lead researcher and a translator with groups of 3–8 villagers per village.
    - Field notes in Portuguese/English; English transcripts compiled after fieldwork.
  - Village_profiles: 1 document
    - Two-week pre-visit in July 2014; interviews with village leaders/representatives across 11 villages (Mabalane).
    - Data recorded in Portuguese and English; transcripts compiled after fieldwork.
  - Wealthranking_qual: 7 documents
    - Qualitative data recoded during wealth rankings in each study village.
    - Data recorded in Portuguese and English; transcripts compiled after fieldwork.

- Data collection and transcription workflow
  - Fieldwork conducted following a defined protocol.
  - Translation from Portuguese (and potentially Changana) to English during or after fieldwork.
  - Transcripts compiled into English after field activities.

- Language and anonymization
  - Primary data languages: Portuguese and Changana (where applicable); English transcripts produced post-fieldwork.
  - Interviewee identities are anonymized.

- Data governance and usability considerations
  - Mixed formats (RTF and CSV) may require harmonization for analysis.
  - Anonymization and translation introduce potential fidelity considerations; metadata on translators and field dates would support reuse.
  - Data are organized by study type and village, enabling cross-component analyses but requiring careful metadata alignment.
