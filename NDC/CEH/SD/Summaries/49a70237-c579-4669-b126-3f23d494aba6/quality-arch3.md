# Field protocols

## 1) Field protocols
- For each exercise field protocols were prepared in Portuguese.

## 2) Training of assistant/translator
- At the beginning exercises were conducted by the lead researcher together with one assistant/translator.
- Prior to each exercise the assistant was trained on the objective of the interview and how to ask the questions.
- At a later stage, the trained assistant conducted exercises by himself.

## 3) Triangulation and focusing
- By discussing various topics repeatedly with different groups/persons, information could be validated and highlighted from different perspectives.
- Context information gained through interviews, conversations and focus group discussions helped to gradually better focus the interviews.

## 4) Composition of group
- Usually, the leader of a village was asked to gather a group of people according to the request.
- Focus groups were usually composed of men and women.
- Everyone was explicitly invited to contribute.

## 5) Translation
- The researcher was always accompanied by a translator/assistant speaking the local language (native speaker).
- If needed, all questions were translated from Portuguese to Changana and vice versa.

## 6) Note taking
- Notes were taken by the lead researcher and the assistant.

## 7) Discussion
- After each interview, all information was discussed either with the assistant or a colleague.
- In case of lack of understanding, clarification was sought via communications with locals.

## 8) Transcript in English
- Back in office, all notes were written down in English.
- Notes taken by the assistant were recorded in Portuguese and translated to English.

## 9) Back-up
- Several back-ups were prepared from all documents.
