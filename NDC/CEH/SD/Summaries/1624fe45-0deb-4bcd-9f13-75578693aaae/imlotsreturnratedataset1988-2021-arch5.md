# The dataset:

- This dataset contains calculated return rates for five seabird species from representative colonies on the Isle of May, off the East coast of Scotland.
- Return rates are measured annually as the proportion of colour-marked individuals seen in a given year who were also observed in the previous year; these are not survival estimates.
- Each year is a single row with five columns corresponding to the return rate (RR) for each species, plus the yearâ€™s counts of individuals seen that year and in the previous year (including new marks).
- The dataset is free to use under a standard UKCEH data licence, with a request for potential users to contact relevant UKCEH staff before publication.

## Data structure and contents

- Rows: one per year.
- Columns: year; five species RR values (common name + RR suffix); counts for individuals seen in the year and in the previous year (including newly marked individuals).
- The reported figures represent the proportion of marked birds observed by the end of the breeding season in that year.

## Background and context

- Seabird studies on the Isle of May NNR began in 1973 as part of the Isle of May Long-Term Study (IMLOTS), within the UKJNCC seabird monitoring programme.
- Standardised methods for calculating return rates have been consistent since 1987.
- Standardised monitoring started in 1987; first return-rate year data appeared in 1988 after individuals were colour marked.
- Marking effort varies by species and by year; new individuals are marked annually to maintain a sufficient marked population for analysis.
  - Puffins: one plot (>100 marked birds).
  - Guillemots: five plots.
  - Razorbill: four plots.
  - Kittiwakes: seven plots (with two plots later lacking birds; a new plot added in 2013).
  - Shags: one plot until 2005, then two additional plots added due to population decline.
- Colour-marked birds (and nest sites for several species) are visited and checked regularly to identify breeding pairs and mark status.

## Field methods and data collection

- Fieldwork conducted by trained staff following defined protocols.
- Data collected in notebooks and field data sheets, then transcribed to spreadsheets; backups stored in UKCEH directories.
- Notebooks and field sheets archived at UKCEH Edinburgh.
- For shags, razorbills, and guillemots, nest sites are checked until all breeding pairs are identified, whether marked or unmarked.
- Colour-marked birds are actively searched for throughout the island colony to account for occasional movement between sites.

## Data management and quality control

- Field data handled by experienced staff according to objective-driven protocols.
- Databases used are quality controlled from the source; Access databases include error-checking queries to verify and correct uncertain information at the field station.

## Access, licensing, and reuse

- The dataset is freely available under UKCEH data licence.
- Users are encouraged, particularly for publication, to contact relevant UKCEH staff beforehand.
- Data handling and sharing adhere to management vetting processes within UKCEH (see Output section).

## Storage, archiving, and provenance

- Data files are backed up and stored in UKCEH directories; notebooks and field sheets are archived at UKCEH Edinburgh.
- Provenance is maintained through standardized long-term monitoring practices since 1987, with documented field protocols and plotting schemes.
