# SOIL PROFILE OF RIGG Foot, Sourhope

## Overview and context
- Site: Sourhope, Rigg Foot; blocks 4 and 5 with Ecotron plot; soil series described as Brown forest soil with gleying; parent material includes undifferentiated intermediate igneous and andesite.
- Location details: Block 5 grid NT8544019520, altitude ~315 m, slope ~5°, imperfect drainage.
- Purpose: Document multi-depth soil profiles, chemical properties, auger samples, and vegetation surveys to support long-term soil and ecosystem studies.

## Data structure and content

### BLOCK 4
- SOIL PROFILE: Depths and horizons
  - LF 0–1 cm: fibrous, moist; no mineral grains.
  - FH 1–4 cm: fibrous roots present; moist.
  - H 4–8 cm: black colour; amorphous; weak blocky structure; roots present.
  - Ah 8–29 cm: very friable; sandy silt loam; roots; presence of stones.
  - Abg 29–40 cm: sandy/ coarse materials with mottling; roots.
  - B sg 40–61 cm: sandy silt loam; blocky structure; roots.
  - C gx 61–95 cm: sandy clay loam; massive to platy structure; few roots.
- SOIL ANALYSIS (per horizon; MLURI barcodes)
  - Variables: weight (mg), %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, %Moisture loss, %LOI, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
  - Horizons analyzed: FH, H, Ah, AB, Bs, Cx (plus Abg).
- AUGER SAMPLES (4A–4F)
  - Variables: weight, %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, %Moisture loss, %LOI, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
- VEGETATION SURVEY (BLOCK 4)
  - Plots 4A–4F; records of subplot-level abundances, species totals, and presence data across Ac, Ao, At, Br, etc. Includes per-plot species totals and tallies.
- ECOTRON PLOT (BLOCK 4)
  - SOIL ANALYSIS: single MLURI barcode; Wt, %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, %Moisture loss, %LOI, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
  - VEGETATION SURVEY: per-subplot counts by Ac/Ao/At/Br/Cp/Cxp/Dc/Df/Fo/Fr, with species totals and counts.

### BLOCK 5
- SITE DETAILS: Block 5; grid NT8544019520; altitude ~315 m; slope ~5°; soil drainage imperfect; Major soil subgroup: Brown forest soil with gleying; rock type includes undifferentiated intermediate igneous and andesite.
- SOIL PROFILE: Depths and horizons
  - LF 0–1 cm: fibrous; moist.
  - FH 1–5 cm: semi-fibrous; roots present.
  - H 5–7 cm: black; semi-fibrous; roots present.
  - Ah 7–25 cm: very dark brown; sandy silt loam; friable; many roots.
  - AB 25–35 cm: grey; coarse sandy silt loam; friable; abundant fine fleshy roots.
  - Bg 35–49 cm: reddish brown/yellow mottles; abundant roots.
  - BCg 49–70 cm: reddish brown/yellow mottles; clay loam; massive to weak blocky structure.
  - Cg 70–100 cm: light reddish brown; clay loam; massive; very abundant igneous stones.
- SOIL ANALYSIS (per horizon; Block 5 profile)
  - Variables: Wt, %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, %Moisture loss, %LOI, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
  - Horizons analyzed: FH, H, Ah, AB, Bg, BCg, Cg.
- AUGER SAMPLES (5A–5F)
  - Variables: weight, %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg, %Moisture loss, %LOI, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
- VEGETATION SURVEY (BLOCK 5)
  - PLOTS 5A–5F; subplots (Ac, Ao, At, Br, Cp, Cxp, Dc, Df, Fo, Fr, Gs, Hm, Je, Lm, Lzm, Mc, Ns, Pe, Pp, Pt, Ra, Rr, Rs, Tr, Vc) with species totals and counts; abundance per subplot for listed taxa.
- ECOTRON PLOT
  - SOIL ANALYSIS and VEGETATION SURVEY analogous to Block 4 ECOTRON data.

### ECOTRON SITE
- ECOTRON PLOT SOIL ANALYSIS
  - Single soil sample with Wt, %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Mg, Na, K, pH H2O, pH CaCl2.
- ECOTRON PLOT VEGETATION SURVEY
  - per-subplot and plot-level counts; taxa presence and totals across Ac, Ao, At, Br, Cp, Cxp, Dc, Df, Fo, Fr, Gs, Hm, Je, Lm, Lzm, Mc, Ns, Pe, P, etc.

## Data collection methods and quality
- Standardized horizon labeling and sampling patterns
  - Profiles with LF, FH, H, Ah, AB, Bg, BCg, Cg; depth ranges clearly defined.
  - 0–30 cm sampling for horizons; augmented with profile pits for horizon descriptions.
- Laboratory measurements
  - Comprehensive soil chemistry: %N, %C, C:N, Ca, Na, K, Mg; moisture loss; LOI; pH in water and CaCl2.
- Data traceability and provenance
  - MLURI barcodes assigned to each horizon and auger sample; explicit Wt measurements and horizon depths provided.
- Vegetation data
  - Detailed plot- and subplot-level tallies; species presence/absence and counts; cross-plot comparability through consistent coding (Ac, Ao, At, Br, Cp, etc).
- Quality considerations
  - Note blocks with atypical values or incomplete Na data; potential legacy data interoperability considerations across blocks and horizons; emphasis on consistent metadata for discoverability and reuse.

## Metadata, documentation, and data access
- Documentation includes precise grid references, altitudes, slope, drainage, soil series, and parent material.
- Datasets are organized by Block and Plot with horizon- and plot-level data, plus appendices of detailed soil measurements and vegetation inventories.
- Data are linked to MLURI barcodes, enabling traceability and future re-analysis or re-measurement.
- Long-term integration potential with environmental monitoring programs (ECN, Micronet) for broader discovery and reuse.

## Governance implications for Data Stewards
- High-value, multi-parameter, horizon-level soil data across multiple blocks require robust metadata schemas, consistent horizon nomenclature, and units.
- Interoperability needs across blocks (4 and 5) and Ecotron plots; ensure harmonized data formats and coding schemes.
- Ensure secure, persistent storage, clear data provenance, archiving plans, and defined data-sharing/update policies to support re-use and ongoing long-term studies.
- Manage potential gaps (e.g., missing Na values) and legacy data compatibility; document data quality flags and any block-specific deviations.

## Key takeaways for Data Stewards
- The dataset offers rich, multi-dimensional soil and vegetation information across multiple horizons, plots, and blocks, including Ecotron contexts.
- It is well-documented with explicit methodologies, spatial design, horizon-based soil data, and thorough vegetation inventories, enabling reproducibility and re-analysis.
- Governance needs include rigorous metadata standards, standardized data formats, secure long-term storage, archiving, and clear data-sharing policies to support reuse and ongoing updates.
