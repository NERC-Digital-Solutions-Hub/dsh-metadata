# CHANGES IN THE RURAL ENIRONMENT FIELD SURVEY HANDBOOK

## Purpose and scope
- 1984 field handbook for Project 424 Ecological Survey of Great Britain.
- Aims to establish a quality baseline of rural environment by ground surveys and aerial photography, enabling future monitoring and comparisons over time.
- Surveys 384 squares (12 per 32 land classes) across GB to assess land cover, land use, habitats, and landscape features.
- Data are intended for standardised, comparable outputs (maps, reports, charts) and for storage in appropriate data portals.

## Study design and sampling
- Land classes derived from multivariate classifications; sampling from 32 classes across GB.
- Each class contributes 12 squares, totaling 384 survey units (with some exceptions when original square cannot be surveyed).
- Teams from six ITE research stations conduct surveys; liaison with a central team for coherence.
- Planning emphasizes travel efficiency and cost control; multiple scheduling options exist (daily travel, short overnights, or longer blocks).

## Data collection framework and standardization
- Data items collected cover Ownership, Farmer/Landowner information, Physiography/Inland Water/Coastal, Agriculture/Natural Vegetation, Forestry/Woodland/Trees, Boundaries, Recreation, Buildings/Structures/Communications, Photography.
- Data are recorded on 6" (1:10,000) maps and compiled into a Field Assessment Booklet (FAB) using predefined codes to enable consistent recording and later analysis.
- Systematic coding allows combinations of codes to describe complex features on the map; boxes on FAB pages support numeric and alpha codes.
- Emphasis on recording dominant categories first, but allowing qualifiers and multiple components where necessary.

## Data recording and coding
- FAB structure includes cover pages, Ownership, Farmer/Landowner Information, Physiography/Inland Water/Coastal, Agriculture/Natural Vegetation, Forestry/Woodland/Trees, Boundaries, Recreation, Buildings/Structures/Communications, Photography.
- Examples of coding: cover types (e.g., 100 Amenity grassland, 101 Permanent pasture, 102 Short-term grassland, 103 Upland grassland), vegetation communities, crops, livestock, and habitat components.
- Boundary types and descriptions coded for precise mapping; boundaries are mapped even if features have changed or no longer exist.
- Trees and woodland recorded with detailed species, age, density, regeneration, and management codes; hedgerows, belts, and individual trees are distinguished.
- Special notes on curtilages, mosaic vegetation, and areas too complex for simple coding; coloured pen usage or additional notes are allowed to avoid map clutter.
- Photography guidelines require five slides per square, with exact geolocation and directional markers on FAB.

## Field procedures and permissions
- On-site workflow: quick site reconnaissance, secure permissions for the entire square, plan a route for full coverage, and allocate time for review after surveying.
- Obtaining permissions is essential to access all parts of the square and to maintain goodwill with farmers/landowners; prior contact is recommended to avoid delays.
- Surveyors carry identity (ITE ID cards) and should update ownership lists and addresses based on prior surveys.

## Equipment and logistics
- Minimal field equipment: FABs, 1:10,000 maps, field films, pens, navigation tools, clipboard, weather-appropriate clothing, camera, and basic reference materials.
- Equipment use is geared toward mapping and recording rather than measurement; emphasis on mobility and efficiency.

## Post-survey data management
- After surveying, reviewers should read through FABs to check for omissions; data transposition to fresh maps is discouraged unless necessary to avoid damage to originals.
- Completed FABs and batches are coordinated with Merlewood for transport and processing; timely communication about completed squares is required.

## Outputs, accessibility, and reuse
- Data are designed to be stored in a standardised format and uploaded to appropriate data portals to enable long-term accessibility and cross-study comparison.

- The manual frames the broader aim of increasing dataset value by enabling reuse and integration with other data sources.

## Photography and documentation
- Visual documentation via five photographs per square; locations and directions must be precisely marked on the FAB to support spatial context and future comparisons.
