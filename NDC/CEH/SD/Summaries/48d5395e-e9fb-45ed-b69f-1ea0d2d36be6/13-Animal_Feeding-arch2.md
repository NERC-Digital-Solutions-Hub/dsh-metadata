# Supporting documentation for 13-Animal_Feeding.csv

- Purpose and scope
  - Provides metadata and field definitions for the 13-Animal_Feeding.csv dataset to support standardized data collection, quality assurance, transformation, and integration within environmental monitoring outputs.
  - Aims to ensure consistency across datasets used to assess environmental health and policy performance over time.

- Core data fields and definitions
  - Animal
    - Explanation: General type of animal
    - Units: n/a
    - Notes: n/a
  - Location
    - Explanation: Name of the location where the sample was collected
    - Units: n/a
    - Notes: n/a
  - Feeding
    - Explanation: Description of the feeding regime
    - Units: n/a
    - Notes: n/a
  - Daily_Ingestion
    - Explanation: Amount of dry matter ingested by an animal per day
    - Units: kg dry matter per day
    - Notes: n/a
  - Composition_Animal_Feed
    - Explanation: Equation for the weighted composition of the total diet
    - Units: n/a
    - Notes: Information about animal feed composition provided by producers
  - Abbreviations
    - Explanation: Abbreviations are based on Sample Codes
    - Units: n/a
    - Notes: Used for interpreting codes in the dataset

- Data quality and standardization considerations
  - Emphasizes consistent definitions and coding (e.g., n/a units where appropriate) to facilitate reliable aggregation and comparison across monitoring efforts.
  - Notes that feed composition details come from producers, underscoring the need for clear provenance and documentation.

- How this supports environmental monitoring workflows
  - Provides a clear, standardized schema to enable verification, cleaning, and transformation of feeding-related data.
  - Facilitates reporting outputs (e.g., reports, maps, charts) that communicate animal feeding practices and ingestion metrics within environmental health assessments.
  - Supports proper storage and portal upload of processed datasets as part of ongoing monitoring programs.
