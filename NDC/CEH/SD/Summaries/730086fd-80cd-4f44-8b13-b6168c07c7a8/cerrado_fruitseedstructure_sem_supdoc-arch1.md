# Botanical material

## Overview
- Seeds from 14 Cerrado-native species collected across multiple Brazilian reserves and parks to study seed/integument ultrastructure and its relation to fire adaptation.
- Primary aim: provide insight into how seed/fruit structure relates to fire responses, using scanning electron microscopy (SEM).

## Sampling and data structure
- Species collected from diverse sites: Parque Nacional da Serra do Cipó (Minas Gerais), Reserva Natural Serra do Tombador (Goiás), Parque Estadual do Jalapão (Tocantins), Estação Ecológica de Itirapina and Assis (São Paulo).
- For each species: seeds collected from at least ten individuals across different populations.
- Species list (families): Acisanthera alsinaefolia (Melastomataceae); Cambessedesia hilariana (Melastomataceae); Microlicia sp. (Melastomataceae); Pleroma cardinale (Melastomataceae); Pleroma frigidula (Melastomataceae); Pleroma stenocarpum (Melastomataceae); Stenodon campestre (Melastomataceae); Tibouchina melastomoides (Melastomataceae); Vellozia seubertiana (Velloziaceae); Vellozia squamata (Velloziaceae); Vellozia variabilis (Velloziaceae); Xyris hilariana (Xyridaceae); Xyris jupicai (Xyridaceae); Xyris paradisiaca (Xyridaceae).
- Collection dates span 2014–2020; site abbreviations include EEA, EEI, RNST, PNSC, PEJ.
- Seeds stored in Ziplock bags at 20°C with relative humidity below 40%.

## SEM methods and purpose
- Representative sampling: 1 seed per species for ultrastructural analysis.
- Fixation: Karnovsky fixative (formaldehyde-glutaraldehyde).
- Dehydration: graded alcohol series.
- Drying: critical point drying with CO2 (Leica EM CPD300).
- Mounting and coating: aluminum supports with conductive carbon tape; gold coating 30–40 nm.
- Imaging: Carl Zeiss EVO LS15 SEM.
- Objective: characterize seed surface/integument structure to illuminate links between morphology and fire-adaptation.
