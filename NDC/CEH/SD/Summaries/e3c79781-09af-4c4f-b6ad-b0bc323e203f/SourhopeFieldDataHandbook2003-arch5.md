# SOIL PROFILE OF RIGG Foot, Sourhope : BLOCK 4

- Purpose and scope
  - Detailed description and analysis of the soil profile for Block 4 at Rigg Foot, Sourhope, including horizon descriptions, laboratory soil chemistry, auger Sampling, vegetation surveys, and data provenance.
  - Part of the Sourhope long-term field data, with accompanying ecotron plots and multi-block context.

- Profile description by horizon (Block 4)
  - LF (0–1 cm): No identifiable mineral grains; fibrous; moist; clear smooth boundary.
  - FH (1–4 cm): No identifiable mineral grains; fibrous with abundant very fine roots; sharp smooth boundary.
  - H (4–8 cm): Black matrix; amorphous; moist; weak medium angular blocky structure; dense fine roots; sharp wavy boundary.
  - Ah (8–29 cm): Dark reddish brown sandy silt loam; weak to moderate blocky structure; very friable; many fine roots; presence of small igneous stones andesite.
  - Abg (29–40 cm): Pinkish grey/brown with mottling; coarse sandy loam; moderate angular blocky structure; many small igneous stones; numerous andesite stones.
  - B sg (40–61 cm): Brown; strong mottling; sandy silt loam; mixture of angular blocky structure; firm; abundant fine roots.
  - C gx (61–95 cm): Reddish brown; sandy clay loam; massive to platy structure; few roots; abundant very small igneous stones; medium andesite stones.

  - H: %N 2.14; %C 32.25; C:N 15.10; Ca 7.08; Mg 3.35; K 2.73; %Moisture loss 10.09; LOI 63.99; pH H2O 4.94; pH CaCl2 4.19.

  - Bs: %N 0.08; %C 1.04; C:N 12.64; Ca 0.59; Mg 0.25; K 0.12; %Moisture loss 6.80; LOI 8.00; pH H2O 4.80; pH CaCl2 3.94.

- Auger samples (4A–4F) overview
  - %N 0.56–0.58; %C 7.65–8.02; C:N 12.43–14.26; Ca 0.36–2.96; Mg 0.58–1.59; K 0.58–0.81; %Moisture loss 4.11–10.09; LOI 4.47–17.96; pH H2O 4.45–5.05; pH CaCl2 3.53–4.14.
  - General pattern: surface horizons richer in organic matter (higher LOI) with acidic pH; deeper horizons show lower Ca/Mg and lower LOI.

- Vegetation context (Block 4)
  - Dominant grasses include Agrostis capillaris and Festuca rubra; Nardus stricta more prominent in damper/moist upper blocks.
  - Community classification aligns with National Vegetation Classification U4d (Festuca ovina–Agrostis capillaris–Galium saxatile) with possible U5b in moister upper blocks.
  - Indicator species observed include Deschampsia flexuosa, Deschampsia cespitosa, and other associated taxa.

- Data provenance and management (Block 4)
  - Data linked to MLURI identifiers and barcodes; plot/subplot/horizon-level data captured.
  - Archiving includes -80°C storage for samples; data intended for ECN-linked sharing and project reports.
  - Documentation and appendices (Appendix A and related files) support reproducibility and future reuse; notes caution about incomplete subplots and block-level anomalies.

- Ecotron soil site (ECOTRON PLOT)
  - Soil analysis: Wt 6.089 mg; %N 0.59; %C 8.22; C:N 13.83; Ca 2.76; Mg 1.7; %Moisture loss 4.79; LOI 17.42; pH H2O 4.79; pH CaCl2 3.82.
  - Vegetation survey tracks multiple plots (A–F) with various species; overall coverage and composition documented for cross-site comparisons.

- Relevance for Data Stewards (Block 4)
  - Demonstrates comprehensive field data governance: standardized horizon-based sampling, multi-property soil chemistry, archiving, and metadata practices.
  - Highlights challenges in managing long-term, multi-block datasets: incomplete sampling, block-level variability, legacy formats, and need for consistent data models and dictionaries.
  - Provides a model for traceable provenance, interoperability, and reuse in multidisciplinary ecological and agricultural research.

# SOIL PROFILE OF RIGG Foot, Sourhope : BLOCK 5

- Site context and block details
  - Block 5 site details: grid NT8544019520; altitude 315 m; slope 5 degrees; imperfect drainage; major soil: Brown forest soil with gleying; rock types include undifferentiated intermediate igneous and andesite.
  - Purpose: detailed soil profile and associated data for Block 5 to support cross-block comparisons and long-term analyses.

- Soil profile and horizon descriptions (Block 5)
  - LF (0–1 cm): No identifiable mineral grains; fibrous; moist; clear smooth boundary.
  - FH (1–5 cm): No identifiable mineral grains; semi-fibrous; moist; abundant very fine fibrous roots; sharp smooth boundary.
  - H (5–7 cm): Black; moist; semi-fibrous; weak angular blocky structure; roots present; sharp wavy boundary.
  - Ah (7–25 cm): Very dark brown sandy silt loam; moderate blocky structure; friable; many very fine roots; many medium subangular andesite stones.
  - AB (25–35 cm): Grey with mottling; coarse sandy silt loam; friable; abundant fine fleshy roots; many igneous stones; smooth boundary.
  - B g (35–49 cm): Reddish brown with mottling; clay loam; many mottles; firm; roots present; abundant igneous stones.
  - BCg (49–70 cm): Redder hues with mottling; clay loam; massive to weak blocky structure; firm; very abundant igneous stones.
  - C g (70–100 cm): Light reddish brown; clay loam; massive structure; few roots; very abundant small igneous stones.

- Vegetation context (Block 5)
  - Multi-plot surveys (5A–5F) indicate varied community composition with grasses and other herbaceous species; totals per subplot show differences in species richness and abundance across plots, consistent with Block 4 patterns of grass-dominated swards with block-to-block variability.

- Data provenance and management (Block 5)
  - Block 5 data links to barcodes and horizon-level measurements; block-level site details and horizon descriptions documented to support cross-block comparability.
  - Data archiving and documentation align with Sourhope long-term data management practices; ensures traceability for reuse in multidisciplinary analyses.

- Relevance for Data Stewards (Block 5)
  - Demonstrates robust governance for multi-horizon soil data across blocks, with explicit horizon sequencing, standardized measurements, and comprehensive metadata.
  - Highlights ongoing challenges in large, multi-block datasets, including heterogeneous sampling intensity and block-level variability that require careful preprocessing and model design for integration with Block 4 data.

- Overall takeaway for data reuse
  - The Block 4 and Block 5 datasets provide a rich, traceable resource for soil-plant interactions, grazing and management effects, and climate/environmental change studies.
  - Clear provenance, standardized horizon-based data capture, and detailed appendices support reproducibility, cross-site comparisons, and long-term ecological research.
