# Macroinvertebrate sampling in the River Beas, India, 2017

- Overview
  - Supporting documentation for Macroinvertebrate kick-sampling data collected at four sites on the River Beas, India, on 3–4 November 2017.
  - Methodology follows the UK Environment Agency's standard semiquantitative protocol (Murray-Bligh, 1999).
  - Data collected by S. Ncube; correspondence: a.visser-quinn@hw.ac.uk.

- Data structure and contents
  - Columns and explanations:
    - SiteNumber: site number (1, 2, 3, 4)
    - SiteName: site name
    - Longitude: site longitude (degrees, minutes, seconds)
    - Latitude: site latitude (degrees, minutes, seconds)
    - SamplingDate: date of sampling (sites 1–2 on 3 November 2017; sites 3–4 on 4 November 2017)
    - Macro-invertebrateOrder: order of macro-invertebrates recorded
    - Macro-invertebrateFamily: family of macro-invertebrates recorded
    - NumberOfTaxa: number of macro-invertebrate taxa observed during kick sampling

- Methodology
  - Type of sampling: macroinvertebrate kick sampling.
  - Data purpose: semi-quantitative assessment of macroinvertebrate communities following standardized procedures.

- References
  - Murray-Bligh, J. 1999. Quality management systems for environmental monitoring: biological techniques, BT001. Procedure for collecting and analysing macro-invertebrate samples. Version 2.0. Environment Agency: Bristol.

- Relevance for environmental monitoring
  - Provides a standardized, clearly structured dataset to enable consistent environmental health assessments over time.
  - Metadata supports data quality assurance, interoperability, and potential integration with other monitoring outputs.
