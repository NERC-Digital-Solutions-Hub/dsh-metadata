# WildCrickets05B-CallingActivity Description of content

## Overview
- Provides basic information on each time sample of individual male calling activity in the population.
- Intended to support monitoring and understanding environmental health indicators through behavioral data.

## Key fields (content)
- Year: calendar year of the sample.
- Alive Year: the year when the cricket was alive.
- ID: unique individual identifier.
- Temp: temperature at sampling (in °C).
- Age: age at sampling (in days).
- Sings: whether the individual was singing (yes/no).

## Use in monitoring frameworks
- Facilitates tracking of behavioral responses (calling activity) to environmental conditions, particularly temperature.
- Enables longitudinal analysis at the individual level to inform population health and activity patterns.

## Data quality and governance considerations
- Metadata needs: clear definitions, units (°C, days), and sampling context.
- Data accessibility and potential sharing, which may require data governance and open data practices.
- Data standardization and transformation requirements to ensure usability across studies.
- Potential gaps or inconsistencies in sampling or measurements that require documentation and QA.

## Practical implications and limitations
- Description is focused on basic variables; may require additional context (location, method) for broader interpretation in monitoring programs.
