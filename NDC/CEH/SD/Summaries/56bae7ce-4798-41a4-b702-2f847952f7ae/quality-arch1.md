# Interview guideline

- Objective and preparation
  - Interview guidelines were prepared in Portuguese for interviews with different actor groups.
- Training of assistant/translator
  - Aimed to keep the same assistant/translator throughout; prior to each exercise, the objective and how questions should be asked were explained to the assistant.
- Triangulation and focusing
  - Information validated by discussing topics across multiple groups and perspectives; contextual information from interviews, conversations, and focus groups helped refine focus.
- Composition of group
  - Village leader typically asked to assemble a group; focus groups included both men and women; everyone was explicitly invited to contribute.
- Translation
  - The researcher was always accompanied by a translator/assistant who speaks the local language; questions translated between Portuguese and Changana as needed.
- Note taking
  - Notes taken by the lead researcher.
- Discussion
  - After each interview, information was discussed with the assistant or a colleague to improve understanding; clarification sought from locals if needed.
- Transcript in English
  - Back at the office, notes were transcribed into English.
- Back-up
  - Several backups were prepared for all documents.
