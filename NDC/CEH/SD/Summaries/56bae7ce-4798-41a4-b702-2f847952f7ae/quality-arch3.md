# Interview guideline

- 1) Interview guideline: Guidelines with questions in Portuguese prepared for interviews with different actor groups to ensure relevant and structured data collection.
- 2) Training of assistant/translator: Strive to work with the same assistant/translator; pre-brief on interview objectives and how questions should be asked to maintain consistency.
- 3) Triangulation and focusing: Use repeated discussions across various groups to validate information and capture context; diverse perspectives help refine interview focus.
- 4) Composition of group: Village leader usually gathers participants; focus groups include both men and women; explicit invitation to all to contribute.
- 5) Translation: Researcher accompanied by a native-speaking translator; questions translated between Portuguese and Changana as needed.
- 6) Note taking: Notes taken by the lead researcher during interviews.
- 7) Discussion: Post-interview discussions with the assistant or a colleague to improve understanding; seek clarification from locals when needed.
- 8) Transcript in English: After fieldwork, all notes are transcribed into English.
- 9) Back-up: Multiple backups prepared for all documents.
