# Details of data structure

## Overview
- A dataset of 12 RTF documents containing transcripts of interviews conducted in villages near the Mabalane forest, focusing on charcoal production.
- Villages are anonymized in the documents; names can be obtained from the data owner upon request.
- Data collection period spans July to September 2014.
- Contents include semi-structured interviews, focus group discussions, and informal communications with various stakeholders.

## Data files and contents
- village_camps: Four interviews with charcoal camp workers across different parts of the forest; ~10 minutes each.
- villageA_all.interviews: Seven semi-structured interviews with villagers from community A involved in charcoal production; informal notes.
- villageB_all.interviews: One focus group and one semi-structured interview with charcoal producers.
- villageC_all.interviews: One focus group, four semi-structured interviews, and notes from informal communication.
- villageD_focus.group: One focus group with charcoal producers.
- villageE_focus.group: One focus group with charcoal producers, two semi-structured interviews (community forester and association president), and seven short interviews with camp workers.
- villageF_focus.group: One focus group with charcoal producers.
- villageG_interview.fiscal: One semi-structured interview with the community forester.
- villageH.I_trucks: Interviews with truck drivers exporting charcoal from the forest; ~10 minutes each.
- villageH_focus.group: One focus group with charcoal association members.
- villageH_interview.fiscal: Informal communication with a district forester.
- villageI_interview.fiscal: Informal communication with a district forester.

- Languages: Portuguese primarily, with Changana translations where required.
- Formats: Mix of semi-structured interviews, focus groups, and informal communications.

## Anonymization and data access
- Village names are anonymized in the transcripts.
- Names can be provided by the data owner upon request.

## Participants and roles
- Charcoal camp workers, charcoal producers, association members, community foresters, district foresters, and truck drivers.
- Roles span frontline workers to management/oversight (foresters and association presidents).

## Temporal coverage
- Interviews and focus groups conducted between July 2014 and September 2014, with multiple events clustered in late July through September.

## Relevance for environmental monitoring and data reuse
- Provides qualitative insights into charcoal production dynamics, livelihoods, governance, and forest use in the Mabalane area.
- Useful for understanding drivers of forest pressure, supply chains, and stakeholder perspectives, supplementing quantitative environmental indicators.
- The structured, multi-actor dataset supports triangulation and longitudinal monitoring when combined with other data sources.
- Enables data sharing and broader analysis if access to underlying transcripts is provided, aligning with goals to increase dataset value and accessibility.
