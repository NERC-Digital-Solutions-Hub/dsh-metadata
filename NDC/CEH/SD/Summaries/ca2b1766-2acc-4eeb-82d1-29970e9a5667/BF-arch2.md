# BF Protocol

- Aim
  - Record the phenology of spawning of the common frog (Rana temporaria) in selected ponds and ditches by counting egg masses as an indicator of frog population health.
  - Identify significant changes in frog populations and measure pond characteristics that may influence breeding success.

- Rationale
  - Frogs are a representative predatory amphibian; amphibian status in Britain has declined due to drainage, habitat modification, and pollution.
  - Acidification and aluminum effects on larval stages are a long-standing concern; laboratory and field evidence links acidity to embryonic/larval stress and mortality.
  - Adult monitoring is difficult; spawning and egg masses provide a practical proxy for colony size (with assumptions about eggs per mass and sex ratio).

- Method (overview)
  - Location: one or more shallow ponds or ditches known to breed frogs, chosen for accessibility and prior breeding.
  - Monitoring frequency:
    - Weekly checks from around January 1 (or local starting date) to determine when males gather and begin calling.
    - If possible, daily visits from first spawning until eggs hatch; at minimum weekly visits when daily monitoring is not feasible.
  - Spawn tracking:
    - Record the date of first spawn appearance.
    - Daily or weekly counts of new spawn masses; mark each new mass with a colored thread to avoid double counting.
    - Estimate total surface area of water covered by spawn masses; record percentage of dead or diseased eggs.
    - If there are more than 100 masses or visits are infrequent, estimate only the total surface area covered.
    - Record the date when embryos first hatch and continue monitoring until metamorphosed frogs are seen leaving the pond or for 16 weeks from first spawn, whichever shorter.
  - Physico-chemical monitoring (at first spawning):
    - Take a 250 mL water sample from the spawning area for baseline chemical analysis.
    - Measure conductivity and pH on unfiltered water; filter sample per protocol and analyze for a suite of ions and related parameters (Na+, K+, Ca2+, Mg2+, Fe2+, Al3+, NH4+-N, Cl-, NO3--N, SO4 2-, PO4 3--P, alkalinity, dissolved organic carbon).
    - If unexplained mortality occurs, take a follow-up water sample for the same analyses.
  - In situ pond environment measurements (weekly during monitoring period):
    - pH: measure at three random positions near the pond edge, at a depth of ~50 mm.
    - Temperature: use a maximum/minimum thermometer positioned at ~50 mm depth; read weekly and reset.
    - Water depth: measure weekly at the center of the spawning area.

- Equipment
  - Field-ready pH meter and a maximum/minimum thermometer.
  - Water depth measurement pole or an equivalent improvised device.
  - Materials for marking spawn masses (colored thread, bodkin needle).

- Location and site setup
  - Select ponds/ditches with recent breeding activity and easy access for frequent visits.
  - Record site-specific details and ensure repeatable sampling locations for each core measurement.

- Biological data collection (spawn and development)
  - Record-keeping timeline:
    - Date frogs first seen congregating.
    - Date of first spawning.
    - Date of first hatching.
    - Date newly metamorphosed frogs first seen leaving the pond.
  - Data collected at each sampling event:
    - Daily spawn monitoring: number of new spawn masses; total surface area of pond covered by spawn.
    - Health indicators: percentage of dead or diseased eggs.
    - If counts exceed practical limits, shift to area-based estimates.
  - Spawn mass identification: attach a colored thread to the edge of each new mass to prevent double counting.

- Physico-chemical results (at first spawning and ongoing weekly measurements)
  - Measured variables (at initial spawning for baseline; ongoing pH and temperature weekly):
    - pH, conductivity (unfiltered water)
    - Alkalinity
    - Na+, K+, Ca2+, Mg2+, Fe2+, Al3+, NH4+-N, Cl-, NO3--N, SO4 2-, PO4 3--P
    - Dissolved organic carbon (DOC)
  - Depth, minimum and maximum temperatures, and pH at multiple pond positions are recorded as part of weekly pond environment data.

- References and context
  - The BF Protocol draws on cited literature concerning frog biology, breeding phenology, and the effects of pH and aluminum on amphibian development (Beattie et al.; Cooke; Cummins; others).

- Summary of purpose for analysts
  - Provide standardized, time-series data on frog spawning phenology and pond conditions to compare environmental health and policy performance across sites and years.
  - Enable data interoperability through defined core identifiers and recording conventions.
  - Facilitate broader data value by aligning biological indicators with chemophysical pond characteristics for ecological interpretation.
