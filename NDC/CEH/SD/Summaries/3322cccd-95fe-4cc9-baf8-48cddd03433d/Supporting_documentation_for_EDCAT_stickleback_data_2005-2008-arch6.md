# EDCAT stickleback data 2005-2008. Pottinger, T. G., Cook, A., Matthiessen, P. (2017)

## Overview
- Datasets from three-spined sticklebacks (Gasterosteus aculeatus L.) collected in the River Ray (southwest England) downstream and upstream of Rodbourne wastewater treatment works (WWTW) before (2005â€“2007) and after (2008) remediation with granular activated carbon (GAC) tertiary treatment.
- Complementary sampling from reference rivers: River Ock and Childrey Brook.
- Aims to measure biological responses to wastewater effluent and remediation, including biomarker endpoints such as EROD activity, cortisol, and nucleic acid content.

## Sampling regime and collection methods
- Fish captured with a 38 cm D-frame net; euthanized with 2-phenoxyethanol (1:1000); tissues preserved for analysis.
- Measurements recorded: weight (mg) and total length (mm); heart and kidney stored for VTG and spiggin analyses (not included in this dataset).
- Remainder homogenized in buffer, then stored frozen until assay.

## Analytical methods
- EROD (ethoxyresorufin-O-deethylase) activity measured in liver homogenate using a microplate assay; results expressed as pmol/min/mg protein.
- Cortisol extracted from whole homogenate and quantified by radioimmunoassay (RIA); dextran-coated charcoal used in the assay buffer.
- Glucose quantified in homogenate using a hexokinase microplate method.
- Nucleic acids extracted from tissue using sarcosyl buffer; total RNA + DNA quantified with a fluorescence dye (RiboGreen); RNA and DNA separately quantified after RNase treatment to derive RNA:DNA ratio.

## Data structure
- Data provided as a comma-separated values (CSV) file with the following columns:
  - Sampling_date, River, Site_within_river, Fish_code, Length_mm, Weight_mg, Sex, KF (K-factor), EROD_pmol_min_mg_protein, RNA_DNA_ratio, Whole_body_cortisol_ng_g, Whole_body_glucose_mg_g
- Descriptions accompany each column (sampling date of the first day of the two-day sampling period; river and site identifiers; fish metrics; biochemical endpoints).

## Data usage and publications
- This dataset has informed multiple publications examining wastewater effluent effects and remediation:
  - Katsiadaki et al. (2012): field surveys using stickleback biomarkers to detect anti-androgens.
  - Pottinger et al. (2011, 2012): effects of sewage effluent remediation on body size, RNA:DNA ratio, and exposure markers; indices of stress in sticklebacks related to extreme weather and effluent exposure.
- The data enable cross-site comparisons of biomarker responses before and after remediation, supporting assessments of environmental impact and data-driven policy decisions.
