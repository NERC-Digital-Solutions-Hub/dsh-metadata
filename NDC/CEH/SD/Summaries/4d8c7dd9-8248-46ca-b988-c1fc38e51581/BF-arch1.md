# BF Protocol

- Aim
  - Record the phenology of common frog spawning in selected ponds/ditches as an indicator of frog population health.
  - Identify significant changes in populations and measure pond characteristics that may affect breeding success.

- Rationale
  - Common frog biology and feeding patterns; breeding in shallow waters makes monitoring feasible.
  - historical declines linked to drainage, habitat modification, pollution, and acidification effects on embryos and larvae.
  - Spawn counts provide a rough population proxy; breeding sites are typically accessible for monitoring.

- Equipment
  - Field-ready pH meter and maximum/minimum thermometer (or equivalents).
  - Water depth measurement pole (and simple makeshift alternatives).

- Location
  - One or more shallow ponds or wet ditches known to breed frogs, chosen for convenient access and prior breeding activity.

- Biological observations (spawn monitoring)
  - Check timing: weekly from about January 1 (adjust for local knowledge); daily visits if possible until eggs hatch; minimum weekly visits if daily are not feasible.
  - Record the date frogs first congregate and begin calling; after spawning begins, monitor daily (or weekly) until first eggs hatch.
  - Mark each new spawn mass with colored thread to avoid double counting.
  - For each new spawn mass: record date seen, number of new masses, and the total surface area of water occupied by spawn.
  - Estimate percentage of dead or diseased eggs.
  - If visits are infrequent or spawn masses exceed 100, estimate total water surface area instead of counting new masses.
  - Record the date when embryos are first seen hatched; continue weekly monitoring until metamorphosed frogs leave or 16 weeks from first spawning, whichever comes first.

- Physico-chemical observations
  - At first spawning, collect a 250 ml water sample from the spawning area for baseline chemical characterization.
  - Measure conductivity and pH on unfiltered water using ECN methods; after filtration, analyze for: Na+, K+, Ca2+, Mg2+, Fe2+, Al3+, NH4+-N, Cl-, NO3--N, SO4--S, PO4 3--P, alkalinity, dissolved organic carbon.
  - If unexplained high mortality occurs, take a second sample with the same analyses.
  - Measure pH, temperature, and water depth weekly from first spawning to when metamorphosed frogs are seen leaving.
  - pH: measured weekly at three random positions just outside the spawning area at 50 mm depth using a field meter.
  - Temperature: use a maximum/min thermometer mounted on a buoyant float near 50 mm depth; recheck weekly.
  - Record the water temperature at first spawning and update weekly.

- Location and schedule details
  - Sites: shallow ponds/ditches accessible for frequent visits; historically known breeding sites preferred.
  - Sampling cadence: daily spawn monitoring during active spawning (where possible), weekly environmental measurements, and an annual pond water chemistry sample at spawning onset.

- Specification of results and recording conventions
