# DNA extraction procedure from environmental samples and bacterial isolates NERC study NE/N019555/1

## Aim
- Describe procedures for DNA extraction from environmental samples (soil, water, stool, etc.) and from bacterial isolates obtained in the study.
- Use standardized kits and methods to enable downstream analyses and comparability.

## Sample types and kits
- Soil: DNeasy PowerSoil DNA isolation Kit (Qiagen; Catalog No. 12888-50)
- Water: DNeasy PowerWater DNA Isolation Kit (Qiagen; Catalog No. 14900-5-NF)
- Stool: QIAmp DNA Stool Mini Kit (Qiagen; Catalog No. 51504)
- Bacterial isolates: included preparation of template DNA from cultured material

## General precautions
- Avoid skin contact with reagents; wear gloves; consult MSDS for emergencies.
- Handle flammable reagents away from flames; follow universal precautions.
- Store kit reagents at room temperature; extracted DNA typically stored frozen (-20°C to -80°C).

## DNA extraction from soil (PowerSoil Kit)
- Equipment: pipettors, microcentrifuge, vortex, PowerBead Tubes, horizontal securing adapter
- Key steps:
  - Add 0.25 g soil to PowerBead Tube; vortex to mix.
  - Check Solution C1; heat to 60°C if precipitated; add 60 μl Solution C1.
  - Vortex 10 minutes; centrifuge at 10,000 x g for 30 s.
  - Transfer supernatant to new tube; add 250 μl Solution C2; vortex; incubate 4°C 5 min; centrifuge 10,000 x g 1 min.
  - Transfer up to 600 μl supernatant; add 200 μl Solution C3; incubate 4°C 5 min; centrifuge 10,000 x g 1 min.
  - Transfer up to 750 μl supernatant; add 1200 μl Solution C4; mix; bind to Spin Filter; centrifuge in steps (675 μl + 675 μl + remainder).
  - Wash with 500 μl Solution C5; centrifuge; repeat; dry spin.
  - Place Spin Filter in new tube; elute with 100 μl Solution C6 (or sterile water) onto filter; centrifuge to elute.
  - Store extracted DNA frozen (-20° to -80°C).

## DNA extraction from water (PowerWater Kit)
- Equipment: filter funnels, vacuum centrifuge, 15 ml tubes, MB Spin Column, Vortex, Vortex Adapter
- Special precautions:
  - Warm Solution PW1 (55°C) to dissolve precipitates; PW3 may precipitate; warm if needed.
- Key steps:
  - Filter water; transfer filter into PowerWater Bead Tube.
  - Add 1 ml PW1; vortex 5 minutes; centrifuge ≤ 4000 x g for 1 min.
  - Transfer supernatant to new tube; centrifuge 13,000 x g 1 min (collect around 600–650 μl).
  - Add 200 μl Solution IRS; incubate 4°C 5 min; centrifuge 13,000 x g 1 min.
  - Transfer supernatant; add 650 μl PW3; load 650 μl onto MB Spin Column; centrifuge 13,000 x g 1 min; discard flow-through; repeat until all supernatant processed.
  - Place MB Spin Column in new tube; wash with 650 μl PW4; centrifuge 13,000 x g 1 min; wash with 650 μl PW5; centrifuge 1 min; dry spin 2 min.
  - Elute DNA with 100 μl PW6 at center of membrane; centrifuge 1 min at 13,000 x g.
  - Store DNA frozen (-20° to -80°C).

## DNA extraction from stool (QIAamp DNA Stool Mini Kit)
- Sample prep: weigh 180–220 mg stool; suspend in 1.4 ml Buffer ASL; homogenize thoroughly; heat 70°C 5 min; vortex; pellet large debris.
- Inhibitor removal: transfer 1.2 ml supernatant to new tube; add InhibitEX Tablet; vortex 1 min; incubate 1 min; centrifuge to pellet inhibitors; transfer supernatant to new tube.
- Lysis and purification:
  - Add 15 μl proteinase K to new tube; add 200 μl supernatant to PK tube; add 200 μl Buffer AL; mix thoroughly; incubate 70°C 10 min.
  - Add 200 μl ethanol (96–100%); mix; apply lysate to QIAamp spin column; centrifuge 1 min.
  - Bind, wash with 500 μl Buffer AW1; centrifuge 1 min; wash with 500 μl Buffer AW2; centrifuge 3 min; optional dry spin.
  - Elute with 200 μl Buffer AE; centrifuge 1 min to elute.
- Storage: store extracted DNA frozen (-20° to -80°C).

## Preparation of Template DNA (bacterial isolates)
- From TSA plate: transfer 1 loopful to 500 μl autoclaved dH2O; vortex; boil 10 min; place on ice 5–10 min.
- Centrifuge 6,000 rpm 5 min; collect ~200 μl supernatant; store at -20°C to -80°C (noted as -200 C in text).

## Storage of extracts and references
- Recommended storage for DNA: frozen at -20° to -80°C.
