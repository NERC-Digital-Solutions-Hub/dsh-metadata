# Archetype: GIS Specialists

## Summary

### Aim
- Create data products, primarily map-based, that enable their audience to understand and explore relevant information.
- Focus on GIS data analysis to produce map-based visualisations for policy colleagues, clients, or the public.

### Who they are
- Data analysts specializing in Geographical Information Systems (GIS).
- Produce map-based visualisations for diverse audiences (policy teams, clients, public).

### How they meet their aims
- Work closely with end users throughout the process to understand audience needs.
- Source and verify data appropriate for their purposes.
- Clean, transform, and quality-assure data; often integrate datasets at different resolutions.
- Analyze data to identify significant features and determine effective presentation.
- Present data as maps using GIS to clearly show spatial features.
- Test early prototypes and iterate based on feedback.

### Particular Challenges
- Reacting to requests that overlook data complexity rather than proactively prioritising work.
- Data dispersed across many locations.
- Insufficient data, especially at needed resolutions.
- Inconsistent data standards.
- Large or complex datasets not packaged into manageable chunks.
- Significant effort required to clean and transform data.
- Building teams with sufficient GIS skills.
