# Data Leaders

## Overview
- Managers responsible for implementing an effective data strategy across their organisation, often spanning partner networks.
- Apply prior data analysis knowledge to shape the data function and consider dataâ€™s role for a wider community.

## How they meet their aims
- System-wide thinking: address the whole data ecosystem and interdependencies.
- User needs and co-ownership: understand policy colleagues and other users as an ongoing process; foster co-ownership of data products.
- Data availability and gaps: identify what data exists, assess suitability, and prioritise efforts.
- Data sourcing: discover data via platforms, online searches, and outreach; may commission new data and explore creative gathering methods.
- Analytical quality: support strong analytics, clear presentation, and effective dissemination within the team.
- Data asset management: ensure data formats, quality, metadata, discoverability, and timely updates.
- Iterative delivery: ensure products meet user needs through feedback and iteration.
- Collaboration: work with the wider data community to support an effective system.

## Capabilities and practices for success
- End-to-end data system perspective and governance.
- Active engagement with users and stakeholders; fostering co-ownership.
- Robust data discovery, accessibility, and metadata management.
- Data quality improvement and adherence to evolving standards.
- User-centered design and iterative product development.
- Cross-team collaboration and building communities of practice.

## Challenges
- Understanding diverse user needs across public audiences.
- Gaining a comprehensive view of scattered data sources.
- Data gaps, especially at required granularity.
- Paywalls and data protection barriers restricting access.
- Verifying data content and format quickly.
- Inconsistent data standards, variable quality, and unclear metadata.
- Sector coordination gaps, duplication of effort, and need for stronger communities of practice.

## Takeaways for practice
- Develop a holistic view of the data system and governance.
- Invest in data discovery, catalogs, and metadata to improve access.
- Foster partnerships and communities of practice to reduce duplication.
- Implement processes for continuous user feedback and iterative delivery.
- Prioritise data quality, standards, and clear documentation to support decision-making.
