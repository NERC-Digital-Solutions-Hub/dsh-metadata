## Data quality issues encountered
- Data suspected or compromised by external interference (sheep access, weather conditions, snow, freezing).
- Sensor and hardware issues: blocked/unblocked gauge, worn cables, securing bolts loosening, battery/seal/desiccant degradation.
- Timekeeping and synchronization problems: time stamp misalignments, GMT/BST transitions, occasional overlaps.
- Structural and environmental challenges: pedestal collapse, base erosion, soil movement affecting leveling.
- Calibration and data integrity: periods marked for calibration, tips ignored during calibration or rebalancing.

## Mitigation and actions taken
- Physical protections implemented (trench to deter sheep; wooden stand after pedestal issues).
- Regular maintenance: rebalancing, calibration, cable replacement, battery/seal/desiccant changes.
- Data handling practices: marking and ignoring data during calibration windows; adjusting timestamps where necessary.
- Documentation of issues and events to support data cleaning and quality assurance.
- Post-event reviews to ensure continuity of data collection and to flag questionable periods for downstream analysis.

## Metadata, governance, and monitoring framework implications
- The entry highlights the importance of detailed maintenance logs and calibration records for data usability.
- Time-stamp consistency and timezone transitions (GMT/BST) require explicit metadata and adjustments in analyses.
- Environmental and structural vulnerabilities must be documented to interpret data gaps accurately.
- Data governance needs to support transparent data repair, exclusion rules during calibration, and clear notation of sensor health and installation changes.
- Sharing of underlying data and clear communication of data quality issues are essential for external scrutiny and policy-relevant interpretation.
