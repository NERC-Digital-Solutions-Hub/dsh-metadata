Title

NERC Soil Biodiversity Programme

Description

The NERC Soil Biodiversity Thematic Programme was established in 1999 and was centred upon the intensive study of a large field experiment located at the Macaulay Land Use Research Institute (now the James Hutton Institute) farm at Sourhope in the Scottish Borders (Grid reference: NT8545019630). During this time, the site was monitored to assess changes in aboveground biomass production (productivity), species composition and relative abundance (diversity). The primary aims of the Programme were to achieve simultaneously an understanding of the biological diversity of the soil biota and the functional roles played by soil organisms in key ecological processes. In seeking to achieve these aims, 24 separate research projects were funded, providing a range of data regarding soil structure, soil processes (such as the carbon and nitrogen cycles) and the roles of micro-fauna and flora (Bacteria, Nematoda, Protozoa and Fungi), microarthropods (including Collembola and Acari), invertebrate root feeders (Tipulid, Bidionid and Scarabeid larvae), meso-fauna (such as Enchytraeidae) and macro-fauna (including Megradili, Mollusca and Coleoptera).

Lineage

A series of datasets generated by the NERC Soil Biodiversity Thematic Programme, an integrated programme of research on the biological diversity of soil biota and the functional roles played by soil organisms in key ecological processes. The soil sampling, measurements and experiments took place at a single site: Sourhope, in the Scottish Borders. The data were managed and maintained by CEH on behalf of NERC who funded the programme, and on behalf of the various participating organisations who own the data.
