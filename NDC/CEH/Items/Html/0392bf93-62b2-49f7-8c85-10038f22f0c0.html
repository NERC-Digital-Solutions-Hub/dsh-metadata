Title

Plynlimon Hydrochemistry data

Description

These datasets include rainfall, cloud and stream hydrochemistry of the Plynlimon research catchments in Mid Wales.

Stream samples were taken using a grab technique. Rain samples were collected using bulk precipitation collectors. Cloud samples were collected using a UK Centre of Ecology & Hydrology (UKCEH) Edinburgh pattern lidded harp type passive collector.

Fieldwork was carried out by UKCEH members of staff trained in the fieldwork techniques required. Chemical analysis was carried out by qualified UKCEH chemists at laboratories at UKCEH Lancaster and UKCEH Bangor.

The Plynlimon research catchments lie within the headwaters of the River Severn and the River Wye in the uplands of mid-Wales. Intensive and long-term monitoring within the catchments underpins a wealth of long term hydrological and hydro-chemical research. Monitoring is funded by the UK Centre for Ecology & Hydrology, and is ongoing since 1968.

Lineage

Sampling was carried out weekly from May 1983 to December 2011 and then fortnightly from January 2012. Stream samples were taken using a grab technique. Rain samples were collected using bulk precipitation collectors. Cloud samples were collected using a Centre of Ecology and Hydrology (CEH) Edinburgh pattern lidded harp type passive collector. From 1988 to 1999 water sampling was carried out by members of staff from the Institute of Hydrology based at Dolydd near the Plynlimon catchments. From 1999 sampling has been carried out from CEH Bangor. The laboratory analysis has been undertaken at different locations using different equipment during the years of monitoring represented within the datasets. Details of the analytical methods used for each determinand are included within the supporting information of the datasets.
