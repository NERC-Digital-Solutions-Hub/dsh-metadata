# (In)ability to Recover

**(In)ability to Recover** — A person's or community's ability to recover from climate and extreme weather events is governed primarily by social factors. With respect to floods, the map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within the following domains:

- Income
- Language
- Internet
- Social networks
- Mobility
- Pharmacy access
- House prices

## Z-Score Classification

The Inability to Recover Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher inability to recover than average; values around 0 denote around the average for England; negative values denote lower inability to recover than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight

## Inability to Recover Index Indicators

### omain: Income

*Employment deprived people in an area (% of working population)*
Source: IMD 2019. Employment Deprivation Domain — number of employment deprived people in an area (converted to % of working population). Department for Work and Pensions. Weight: 0.333.

*Income deprived people in an area (% of zone population)*
Source: IMD 2019. Income Deprivation Domain — number of income deprived people in an area (converted to % of zone population). Department for Work and Pensions, HMRC and the Home Office. Weight: 0.333.

*Income deprived older people in an area (% of people 60 or over)*
Source: IMD 2019. Income Deprivation Affecting Older People — number of income deprived older people in an area (converted to % of people 60 or over). Department for Work and Pensions. Weight: 0.333.

### Domain: Language

*Level of proficiency in English (% population)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). Table QS205SC. Weight: 1.000.

## Domain: Internet

*Sub-standard broadband (% premises below the Universal Service Obligation)*
Source: Ofcom 2018. % premises below the Universal Service Obligation (USO). Weight: 0.500.

*Availability of superfast broadband (% premises)*
Source: Ofcom 2018. SFBB availability in January 2018 (% premises). These data have been reconfirmed but should be used with caution (see note). Weight: 0.500.

### Domain: Social Networks

*Single pensioner households (%)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). % single pensioner households. Weight: 0.500.

*Lack of school-related networks (% children not of primary school age)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). % children not of primary school age. Weight: 0.500.

### Domain: Mobility

*Comparative illness and disability ratio*
Source: IMD 2019. Health Deprivation and Disability Domain: Comparative Illness and Disability Ratio — Department for Work and Pensions. Weight: 0.200.

*Medical and Care Residents (Score)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). Taken from Sayers et al. (2017). Weight: 0.200.

*Lack of private transport (% households with no car or van)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). % households with no car or van. Weight: 0.200.

*Workplace accessibility by walking or public transport (travel time, destination and origin indicators)*
Source: Journey Time Statistics 2017. JTS0501: Travel time, destination and origin indicators for employment centres by mode of travel, LSOA. Higher travel times equate to higher vulnerability. Weight: 0.200.

*Workplace accessibility by car (travel time, destination and origin indicators)*
Source: Journey Time Statistics 2017. JTS0501: Travel time, destination and origin indicators for employment centres by mode of travel, LSOA. Higher travel times equate to higher vulnerability. Weight: 0.200.

### Domain: Pharmacy Access

*Mean distance to a pharmacy (km)*
Source: CDRC Data 2021. AHAH2 (pharm_dist) — mean distance in km to a pharmacy. Higher distances equate to higher vulnerability. Weight: 1.000.

### Domain: House Prices

*Median price paid for residential properties (£)*
Source: HPSSA Dataset 46. Median price paid for residential properties by LSOA, England and Wales, year ending December 1995 to year ending December 2020. Reversed to indicate higher prices are associated with greater ability to recover. Weight: 1.000.

## Notes

The availability of superfast broadband indicator does not take account of the availability of ultrafast broadband. It is recommended that users refer to the latest data from Ofcom.
