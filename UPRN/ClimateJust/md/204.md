# Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI)

**Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI)** refers to mapped social vulnerability with respect to heat-related hazard. The map shows how the personal, social and environmental factors which help to explain uneven impacts on people and communities come together in particular neighbourhoods, showing where negative social impacts are more likely. This information can then be combined with the likelihood of events occurring to understand how social vulnerability and potential for negative impacts translates into disadvantage.

The SSHVI map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level Z-scores for indicators within each of the five dimensions of socio-spatial vulnerability:

- **Sensitivity** — personal biophysical characteristics affecting the likelihood that a heat wave will have negative health and welfare impacts. Domains: Age, Health.
- **(In)ability to Prepare** — governed primarily by social factors including income and tenure. Domains: Income, Tenure, Language, Internet, Local knowledge.
- **(In)ability to Respond** — governed primarily by social factors including social networks, mobility and general infrastructure. Domains: Income, Language, Internet, Social networks, Mobility, Crime, General Infrastructure.
- **(In)ability to Recover** — governed primarily by social factors including social networks, mobility and the availability of services. Domains: Income, Language, Internet, Social networks, Mobility, GP access, Hospital access, Pharmacy access.
- **Enhanced Exposure** — aspects of the physical environment which tend to accentuate or offset the severity of extreme heat events. Domains: Physical environment, Housing characteristics.

## Z-Score Classification

The SSHVI Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher vulnerability than average; values around 0 denote around the average; negative values denote lower vulnerability than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight
