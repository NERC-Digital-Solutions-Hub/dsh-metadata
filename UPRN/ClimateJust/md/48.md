
**Heat Disadvantage** — combines climate data for extreme heat over the UK with the Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI) to provide a measure of disadvantage with respect to extreme heat risk mapped onto Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs). Climate data is derived from the analysis of Kennedy-Asser et al. (2022), where regional climate model simulations from the UK Climate Projections 2018 (UKCP18) are used to calculate average daily maximum temperatures for the 5% of warmest days under recent past conditions and under global mean surface temperature increases of 1.5°C, 2°C and 3°C compared to pre-industrial conditions. Relative Z-scores are calculated for the whole of Wales over all four temperature scenarios to enable comparison between them. Disadvantage is calculated as an equally weighted combination of the average daily maximum temperature for the 5% of hottest days in the LSOA and the SSHVI for the LSOA as a whole.

## Z-Score Classification

All-relative Z-scores for average daily maximum temperature of top 5% hottest days and relative heat disadvantage are classified as follows. Positive values denote higher temperatures/disadvantage than average; values around 0 denote around the average for Wales; negative values denote lower temperatures/disadvantage than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight
