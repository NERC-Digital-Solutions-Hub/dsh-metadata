**Heat Disadvantage**- combines climate data for extreme heat over the UK with Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI) to provide disadvantage with respect to extreme heat risk mapped on to Lower Super Output Area (LSOAs). Climate data is derived from the analysis of Kennedy-Asser et al (2022), where regional climate model simulations from the UK Climate Projects 2018 (UKCP18) are used to calculate average daily maximum temperatures for the 5% of warmest days under recent past conditions and under global mean surface temperature increases of 1.5°C, 2°C and 3°C compared to pre-industrial conditions. Relative Z-scores are calculated for the whole of Wales over all four temperature scenarios, in order to enable comparison between the scenarios. Disadvantage is calculated as an equally weighted combination of the average daily maximum temperature for 5% of hottest days in the LSOA and the SSHVI for the LSOA as a whole.
