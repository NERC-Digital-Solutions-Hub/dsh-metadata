# (In)ability to Respond

**(In)ability to Respond** — A person's or community's ability to respond to climate and extreme weather events is governed primarily by social factors. With respect to floods, the map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within the following domains:

- Income
- Language
- Internet
- Local Knowledge
- Social networks
- Mobility
- Crime

## Z-Score Classification

The Inability to Respond Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher inability to respond than average; values around 0 denote around the average for Wales; negative values denote lower inability to respond than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight

## Inability to Respond Index Indicators

### Domain: Income

*Employment deprived people in an area (% of working population)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. Employment Deprivation Domain — working-age people in employment deprivation (converted to % of working population). Weight: 0.333.

*Income deprived people in an area (% of zone population)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. People in Income Deprivation (%) — number of income deprived people in an area (converted to % of zone population). Department for Work and Pensions, HMRC and the Home Office. Weight: 0.333.

*Income deprived older people in an area (% of people 60 or over)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. IDAOPI Score — number of income deprived older people in an area (converted to % of people 60 or over). Department for Work and Pensions. Weight: 0.333.

### Domain: Language

*Level of proficiency in English (% population)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). Table QS205EW, used for England/Wales consistency, though a more appropriate replacement statistic for Wales may exist. Weight: 1.000.

### Domain: Internet

*Sub-standard broadband (% premises below the Universal Service Obligation)*
Source: Ofcom 2018. % premises below the Universal Service Obligation (USO). Weight: 0.500.

*Lack of superfast broadband (% premises)*
Source: Ofcom 2018. SFBB availability in January 2018 (% premises), reversed (subtracted from 100) to represent lack of availability rather than availability. These data have been reconfirmed but should be used with caution (see note). Weight: 0.500.

### Domain: Local Knowledge

*Population Transience (Residential Mobility Index)*
Source: CDRC 2021. Residential Mobility Index (average 2012–2020). Weight: 0.333.

*Reversed percentage of properties within historical flood boundaries (-%)*
Source: Sayers et al. (2017). E1 from Sayers et al.'s estimates of properties in historical flood zones — reversed to represent local knowledge from previous events. More properties previously affected represents more ability to prepare, therefore lower vulnerability. See <http://www.sayersandpartners.co.uk/flood-disadvantage.html>. Weight: 0.333.

*Flood alert and warning areas (% area not covered)*
Source: Environment Agency 2020. Flood Alert and Warning Areas, last updated August 2021. Higher proportions of coverage represent more ability to prepare, therefore lower vulnerability. Indicator processed to show proportion of area not covered, therefore higher values indicate higher vulnerability. Weight: 0.333.

### Domain: Social Networks

*Single pensioner households (%)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). % single pensioner households. Weight: 0.500.

*Lack of school-related networks (% children not of primary school age)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). % children not of primary school age. Reversed (100 minus % children aged 4–11). Weight: 0.500.

### Domain: Mobility

*GP-recorded chronic condition (rate per 100)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. Health Deprivation and Disability Domain: GP-recorded chronic condition (rate per 100). Weight: 0.333.

*Medical and Care Residents (Score)*
Source: Census table QS421SC. Uses indicator m2 from Sayers et al. Note: dated information, less reliable. Weight: 0.333.

*Lack of private transport (% households with no car or van)*
Source: Census 2011 (no direct update available). % households with no car or van. Reversed (100 minus % car ownership). Weight: 0.333.

### Domain: Crime

*Index of Multiple Deprivation crime*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. Community Safety Domain. Higher score/ratio = higher vulnerability. Weight: 1.000.

## Notes

The lack of superfast broadband indicator does not take account of the availability of ultrafast broadband. It is recommended that users refer to the latest data from Ofcom.
