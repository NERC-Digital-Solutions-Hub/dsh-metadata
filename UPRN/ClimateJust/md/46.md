
**(In)ability to Recover** — A person's or community's ability to recover from climate and extreme weather events is governed primarily by social factors. With respect to heat, the map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within the following domains:

- Income
- Language
- Internet
- Social networks
- Mobility
- GP access
- Hospital access
- Pharmacy access

## Z-Score Classification

The Inability to Recover Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher inability to recover than average; values around 0 denote around the average for England; negative values denote lower inability to recover than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight
