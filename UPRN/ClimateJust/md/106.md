# Income

**Reference:** I1: % Unemployed, I2: Long-term unemployed, I3: Low income occupations, I4: Households with dependent children and no adults in employment, I5: Income deprivation index

**Theme:** Vulnerability

**Hazard reference:** Flood (Revised Data, 2017)

**Characteristic:** Inability to Prepare, Respond and Recover

**Indicator:** Income

**Supporting Variable:** See Data Sources below

**Assumption:** Higher proportions of people on low incomes in an area indicate a higher vulnerability. Low income households are more vulnerable to flooding due to the effect of low income on people's ability to adapt (to prepare for events, respond to them when they occur and recover from them afterwards).

## Evidence

More information is available at: [http://www.climatejust.org.uk/messages/people-low-incomes](http://www.climatejust.org.uk/messages/people-low-incomes)

- Low income households are less likely to have the capacity to fully prepare for future floods (through insurance and property level measures).
- Low income households are less likely to own their own home. Housing tenure together with low income may restrict people's ability to make modifications to the home they do not own (Fielding and Burningham, 2005).
- A lack of savings restricts the ability of households to respond immediately to flood damage, e.g. through spending on repairs and replacements that would kick-start a recovery process (Tapsell et al., 2002).
- Disruption of transport systems by flood events is likely to particularly affect people who depend upon (rather than choose to use) public transport to get to their place of work or to access other services (for example, public transport is typically more used by low income households).

## Data Sources

**Unemployed (% unemployed)**
Census, ONS (2011). KS501, % unemployed in population aged 16–74.

**Long-term unemployed (% who are LTU or who have never worked)**
Census, ONS (2011). Table KS611. Number of people aged 16–74 who never worked or are long-term unemployed, as a % of all aged 16–74.

**Low income occupations (% in routine or semi-routine occupations)**
Census, ONS (2011). Table KS611. People aged 16–74 in routine or semi-routine occupations as a % of all aged 16–74.

**Households with dependent children and no adults in employment (%)**
Census, ONS (2011). Table KS106. Households with no adults in employment and dependent children as a % of all households.

**People income deprived (%)**
ONS, National Records of Scotland, NI Department for Communities (2010). England & Wales: IMD average weekly household net income (equivalised after housing costs). Scotland: SIMD Income index. Northern Ireland: NIIMD 2010.

**Geographic subdivisions:** Eng: LSOA; Wales: LSOA; Scot: DZ; NI: SOA.
