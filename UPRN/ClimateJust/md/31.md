# Sensitivity

**Sensitivity** refers to personal biophysical characteristics which affect the likelihood that a heat wave or flood event will have negative health and welfare impacts. For example, older people tend to be more susceptible to the effects of high temperatures. The map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within two domains:

- Age
- Health

## Z-Score Classification

The Sensitivity Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher sensitivity than average; values around 0 denote around the average for England; negative values denote lower sensitivity than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight
