# Enhanced Exposure

**Enhanced Exposure** — Enhanced exposure refers to aspects of the physical environment, such as the availability of green space or housing characteristics, which tend to accentuate or offset the severity of extreme heat events. The map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within the following domains:

- Physical environment
- Housing characteristics

## Z-Score Classification

The Enhanced Exposure Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher enhanced exposure than average; values around 0 denote around the average for England; negative values denote lower enhanced exposure than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight

## Enhanced Exposure Index Indicators

### Domain: Physical Environment

*Built up area (% not greenspace)*
Source: UKCEH 2021. Urban land cover extracted from UKCEH Land Cover Maps. Recalculated from 2011 LSOA spatial units by area-weighted averages. Higher value = higher enhanced exposure. Weight: 0.330.

*Lack of domestic gardens (% dwellings without gardens)*
Source: ONS Greenspace. Access to public green space in Great Britain — Office for National Statistics. Weight: 0.330.

*Median combined size of parks, public gardens and playing fields within 1,000m radius (m²)*
Source: ONS Greenspace. Access to public green space in Great Britain — Office for National Statistics. Weight: 0.330.

### Domain: Housing Characteristics

*Household overcrowding indicator*
Source: IMD 2019. Household overcrowding indicator. Weight: 0.333.

*High rise homes (% households with lowest floor 5th floor or above)*
Source: ONS Greenspace. Access to public green space in Great Britain — Office for National Statistics. Weight: 0.333.

*Homelessness indicator (rate per 1,000 households)*
Source: IMD 2019. Homelessness indicator (rate per 1,000 households). Weight: 0.333.
