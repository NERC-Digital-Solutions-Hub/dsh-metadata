### % Caravan or other mobile or temporary structures in all households

**Description:**
This field represents the percentage of all households in a given area that are situated in caravans or other mobile or temporary structures. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Housing Characteristics" indicator, which is used to assess the "Community Support" characteristic of the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). 

**Vulnerability Rationale:**
A higher proportion of households living in these structures indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. The inclusion of this metric is based on several key assumptions and evidence:

* **Limited Physical Protection:** Temporary or mobile housing provides significantly less structural protection against floodwaters than permanent buildings, meaning floods can easily devastate these homes and place lives at risk.
* **Lower Response Capacity:** Residents of these properties are often less able to effectively respond to flood warnings or move their possessions to a place of safety.
* **Lack of Local Knowledge:** Communities living in caravans are more likely to have a transient population with limited knowledge of the local area and its historical flood risks. 
* **Exclusion from Appraisals:** Because caravans are considered "moveable" during floods, they are often excluded from standard economic appraisals for flood damage and do not benefit from the cost-justification of new flood defences.

**Data Source:**

The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically Census table KS401. It is calculated by dividing the number of household spaces identified as a "Caravan or other mobile or temporary structure" by the total number of households, and multiplying by 100. Data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
