### New migrants from outside local area (%)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of people in a given area who lived outside the local area one year prior. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Local Knowledge" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Inability to Prepare" and "Inability to Respond" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of people who are new to an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. These individuals generally have a lower ability to adapt to extreme weather events due to the following factors:
* **Lack of Hazard Awareness:** People who have recently moved into an area are less likely to be aware that their new community has a flood risk issue, as they lack the historical context and informal clues provided by long-term residents and family.
* **Unfamiliarity with Responses:** Newer residents often have less knowledge about how to properly respond to a flood and where to seek appropriate support or services. 
* **Population Turnover:** Neighbourhoods with highly transient populations or high turnover rates generally have weaker community networks, making collective awareness and resilience lower compared to areas with longer residence times (such as many rural areas).

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table UKMIG001. It is calculated by taking the number of people who 'Lived elsewhere one year ago outside the area but within the associated area' (where the associated area is the next level up in the census geography hierarchy, such as the local authority) plus those who 'Lived elsewhere one year ago outside the associated area but within the UK', dividing this sum by the total number of residents, and multiplying by 100. 

*Note on Geography:* The data was originally processed at the Middle Super Output Area (MSOA) level in England and Wales. To map this to the smaller neighbourhood level, the MSOA results were sampled down, assigning the MSOA value to the corresponding Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs) that lie entirely within its boundaries. In Scotland, it is mapped to Data Zones (DZ), and in Northern Ireland to Super Output Areas (SOA).
