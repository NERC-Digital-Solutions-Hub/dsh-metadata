### Surface water | Group | Future 2050s 2℃ scenario

**Description:** 
This field (underlying alias: `SFRI_2050s_2Deg_SW_G` or `SFRI2SWG`) represents the Social Flood Risk Index (SFRI) for surface water (pluvial) flooding under a future 2050s climate scenario, which assumes a 2°C rise in Global Mean Temperature (GMT) from a 1961-90 baseline. The SFRI is a measure of geographic flood disadvantage, identifying areas where physical exposure to flooding and high social vulnerability coincide.

**The "Group" Measure:** 
This metric is calculated as a population-weighted "group" measure at the neighbourhood scale. It provides an estimate of the total accumulated risk by incorporating three key factors:
1. The chance of flooding occurring in the floodplain (accounting for any existing flood defences).
2. The number of people living within that floodplain.
3. The overall social vulnerability of the neighbourhood, as determined by the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale / Score Interpretation:** 
The SFRI is a relative index and has no defined units. 
* **High positive scores** identify highly disadvantaged neighbourhoods where large numbers of the most socially vulnerable people are exposed to surface water flooding. 
* **High negative scores** result from high numbers of people living in the floodplain but within a neighbourhood that has low overall social vulnerability (falling below the UK average). 
* **A value of zero** indicates that no one in that neighbourhood lives in the floodplain.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2017 UK-scale assessment of present and future flood vulnerability, risk, and disadvantage produced by Sayers and Partners LLP for the Joseph Rowntree Foundation. The spatial data is mapped down to the neighbourhood level, corresponding to Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs) in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZs) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOAs) in Northern Ireland.
