### % children of primary school age (4-11) in the population

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of people aged 4 to 11 years (primary school age) out of the total population in a given area. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Social networks" indicator, which is used to assess the "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
Unlike many other metrics in the dataset, a higher proportion of primary school-age children in an area actually indicates a *lower* social vulnerability to flooding (and is thus assigned a negative weighting in the index calculations). This variable acts as a proxy for positive social connectivity based on the following evidence:
* **Stronger Social Networks:** While families with pre-school age children can sometimes become socially isolated, evidence shows that those with school-age children tend to have significantly better local social networks. 
* **Community Cohesion:** Areas with good social networks and community cohesion are less vulnerable because residents are more likely to benefit from shared community knowledge, community activities, and collective responses during a crisis.
* **Access to Assistance:** Socially isolated people are more vulnerable to flooding because they are less likely to ask for help; conversely, the social ties built around primary schools increase the likelihood of residents supporting one another and asking for assistance. 

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically using Census table QS103. It is calculated by dividing the number of people aged 4 to 11 years by the total population, and multiplying by 100. Spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
