### Number of properties within historical flood boundary

**Description:** 
This field represents the number of properties in a given area that fall within a historical flood boundary. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Direct flood experience" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
Unlike most indicators in the dataset, a higher proportion of properties within the historical flood boundary indicates a *lower* level of social vulnerability. Because of this, it is assigned a negative weighting in the NFVI calculation. The rationale for its inclusion is based on the premise that communities with collective experience of past floods generally have more knowledge and better-established support networks to cope with future events. Factors reducing vulnerability include:
* **Better Preparedness and Response:** Those with direct experience of flooding are more likely to know what to do, have a willingness to take preventative action, and respond more seriously to flood warnings. 
* **Greater Awareness:** Households that have previously flooded show a higher level of understanding of official flood warning codes.
* **Increased Institutional Support:** Areas with a history of flooding are more likely to see higher levels of targeted activity, intervention, and support from both government and non-government organizations.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the Environment Agency (EA), Natural Resources Wales (NRW), the Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA), and the Northern Ireland Rivers Agency. It is calculated based on a query of property datasets against historical flood outlines, limited to events within the past 50 years where date information is available. The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
