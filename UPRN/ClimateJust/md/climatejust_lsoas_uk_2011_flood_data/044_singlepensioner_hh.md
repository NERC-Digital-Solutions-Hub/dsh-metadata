### % single-pensioner households

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of households in a given area that consist of a single person aged 65 and over. It serves as a supporting variable (often referenced as N1) for the "Social networks" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of single-pensioner households in an area acts as a proxy for social isolation, which indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. The rationale for this includes:
*   **Reduced Propensity to Seek Help:** Socially isolated people are generally less likely to ask for assistance during an emergency.
*   **Lack of Community Integration:** They are less likely to benefit from shared community knowledge, community-led activities, and collective responses to flood events.
*   **Lower Social Cohesion:** A high prevalence of social isolation within a neighbourhood can indicate that its overall social cohesion is less strong, thereby lowering the community's collective ability to support its vulnerable residents.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table QS113. It is calculated by taking the number of households classified as 'One-person household: Aged 65 and over', dividing that by the total number of households, and multiplying the result by 100. The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
