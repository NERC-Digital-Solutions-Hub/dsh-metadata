### Lack of private transport (% households with no car or van)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of households in a given area that do not have access to a private car or van. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Physical mobility" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Ability to Respond" and "Ability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of households without private transport in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. The inclusion of this metric is based on the practical challenges restricted mobility creates during and after an extreme event:
* **Evacuation Difficulties:** A lack of personal transport restricts residents' ability to evacuate independently or to travel to assist dependents during a flood.
* **Reliance on Public Transport:** Households without cars are disproportionately affected when public transport systems, services, and routes are disrupted by floodwaters.
* **General Mobility Constraints:** It acts as a proxy for broader mobility issues within a community, indicating a greater reliance on external assistance or community-based support (such as shared or dedicated emergency transport) to safely respond to and recover from disaster events.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table KS404SC. It is calculated by taking the number of households categorized as having "No cars or vans", dividing it by the total number of households, and multiplying by 100. The resulting data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
