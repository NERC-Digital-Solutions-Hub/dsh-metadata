### Vulnerability due to exposure of services to flooding (Service Availability)

**Description:** 
This field represents the composite indicator for "Service availability," which is used to assess a community's "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). It measures the extent to which essential community infrastructure is threatened by flood risk, aggregating four specific supporting variables: the percentages of emergency services, care homes, GP surgeries, and schools exposed to flooding.

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of local services at risk of flooding (specifically at a probability level of 1:75 or greater) indicates a higher level of social vulnerability. A community's ability to cope and recover is severely compromised if its institutional support network is also directly affected by the disaster. The rationale is based on the specific impacts to these four services:
*   **Emergency Services:** If police stations, fire stations, or ambulance depots are flooded, the coordination of emergency assistance and the broader relief effort is significantly hampered, slowing down the pace of recovery for the entire community.
*   **GP Surgeries:** Health services are essential components of relief plans; their loss makes it difficult for residents to access critical medical help during and after a flood.
*   **Care Homes:** If flooded, highly vulnerable residents must be evacuated and relocated, causing severe distress. Furthermore, it prevents the care home from acting as a temporary safe haven for other vulnerable individuals evacuated from their own homes.
*   **Schools:** Flooding causes severe disruption to family routines, as children often have to be temporarily transferred to other schools located significant distances away while repairs are made.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2015 Climate Change Risk Assessment (CCRA) by Sayers et al., using a 2011 baseline. It is calculated by querying specific community site locations against hazard data to identify the proportion of sites at risk of a 1:75 or greater flood event. 

*Note on Geography:* To avoid explicitly identifying the vulnerabilities of specific individual community services, the data for these indicators is processed and mapped at the broader Local Authority (LA) level, rather than the highly localized LSOA or Data Zone neighbourhood level.
