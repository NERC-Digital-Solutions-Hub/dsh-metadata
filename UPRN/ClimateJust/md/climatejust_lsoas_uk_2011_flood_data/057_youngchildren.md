### Young children (% people under 5 years)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of the population that is under 5 years of age in a given neighbourhood. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Age" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Susceptibility" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). It measures a population's physical and psychological predisposition to experience a loss of well-being when exposed to a flood based on its age profile.

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of young children (under 5 years of age) in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. Specific factors driving this heightened vulnerability include:
*   **Health and Disruption Impacts:** Numerous studies have highlighted the association between flooding and increased mental health and behavioural problems in young children. It also causes severe disruption to their home-life and schooling.
*   **Inability to Adapt:** Young children are less able to adapt their own behavior to cope with extreme events and rely entirely on parents and carers during an emergency.
*   **Lack of Hazard Awareness:** Children in this age bracket may not recognize the inherent dangers of flood waters. 

**Data Source:** 
The underlying data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics). It specifically utilizes Census table 102, and is calculated by dividing the number of people aged 0-4 years by the total population, and multiplying the result by 100. This forms part of the 2017 UK-scale assessment by Sayers et al. for the Joseph Rowntree Foundation. The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the neighbourhood level, corresponding to Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs) in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZs) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOAs) in Northern Ireland.
