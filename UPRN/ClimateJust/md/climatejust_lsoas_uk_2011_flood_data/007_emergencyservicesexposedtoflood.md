### % of emergency services exposed to flooding

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of emergency services in a given area that are at risk of flooding at a probability level of 1:75 or greater. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Service availability" indicator, which is used to assess the "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of emergency services exposed to flood risk indicates greater social vulnerability, as a community will suffer more severe overall impacts and face difficulties accessing urgent help if its local institutional support network is compromised. The inclusion of this metric is based on the following factors:
* **Compromised Relief Efforts:** Emergency services (such as the police, fire brigade, and ambulances) aim to target the most vulnerable households during a crisis, but the ability to do this effectively relies entirely on the flood resilience of the services themselves. 
* **Operational Disruption:** If an emergency facility is flooded, it severely hampers the coordination of the local response. For example, during the 2010 floods in Cockermouth, Cumbria, the police station itself was flooded, which hindered the relief effort and increased the vulnerability of the affected population.
* **Slower Recovery:** Strong institutional support from emergency services is critical not only for immediate rescue but also for accelerating the overall pace of a community's recovery following a flood.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the Climate Change Risk Assessment (CCRA) by Sayers et al. (2015), utilizing a 2011 baseline. It is calculated by querying the geographic locations of emergency service sites against flood hazard data to identify the proportion of sites at risk. Note that to protect the specific identity and locations of these critical community services, the data is reported and mapped at the wider Local Authority (LA) level across the UK.
