### Level of proficiency in English (%)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of people in a given area who have low proficiency in English, specifically those who report that they do not speak English well or do not speak English at all. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Information Use" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Inability to Prepare," "Inability to Respond," and "Inability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of people with low English proficiency in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. The inclusion of this metric is based on the following key assumptions and evidence:
* **Information Access:** Individuals who cannot read, write, or speak English, or who are less proficient, are significantly more likely to experience difficulties obtaining and using vital information, early warnings, and emergency guidance provided to the general public.
* **Familiarity with Support Systems:** People with language barriers are often recent arrivals who may possess less local knowledge and be less familiar with the national and local services available to support them during a crisis.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table QS205. It is calculated by taking the number of people who state they "Does not speak English at all" plus those who state they "Does not speak English well," dividing this sum by the total number of people, and multiplying by 100. The resulting data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
