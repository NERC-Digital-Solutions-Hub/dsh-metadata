## Recent arrivals to UK (% people with <1 yr residency coming from outside UK)

The field **'Recent arrivals to UK (% people with <1 yr residency coming from outside UK)'** (internal name `ArrivalsInUK`) represents the percentage of a neighborhood's population that has arrived in the UK from abroad within the last year.

### **Description**

Within the **UK Neighborhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI)** framework, this metric is a key indicator of **information-related vulnerability**. It identifies populations that may face significant barriers to building resilience against flooding due to their short duration of residence in the country.

* **Assumption:** Areas with higher proportions of recent arrivals indicate higher social vulnerability because these individuals are less likely to be familiar with local flood risks or the specific national and local services available to assist them.
* **Vulnerability Dimension:** This indicator directly informs the **Inability to Prepare, Respond, and Recover** characteristics of the NFVI. Recent arrivals may have difficulty obtaining or understanding official emergency guidance and may lack the established **community networks** essential for support during and after a flood event.
* **Data Source:** Derived from the **2011 Census** (Table QS801), specifically counting residents who arrived in the UK between 2010 and 2011.
