### Index of Multiple Deprivation - Crime Score

**Description:** 
This field represents the level of crime in a given area, typically measured using the crime domain of the Index of Multiple Deprivation (IMD) or equivalent national indices. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Crime" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Ability to Respond" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher level of crime in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding, as it lowers the community's overall ability to effectively respond to an extreme event. The inclusion of this metric is based on several key factors:
* **Reluctance to Evacuate:** Where crime rates are high, residents may hesitate or refuse to evacuate their properties during floods for fear of looting. For example, during the 2014 floods on the Somerset Levels, it was reported that empty homes were targeted by thieves.
* **Delayed Evacuation and Rescue:** Residents in high-crime areas are more likely to have extra security mechanisms on their homes, such as multiple locks on doors and windows, which can cause critical delays during evacuation and rescue attempts.
* **Distrust of Preventative Measures:** People living in areas with higher crime may be more wary of taking preventative measures against flooding out of fear that the proposed measures or services are 'scams'.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from various national government departments and statistics agencies. Specifically, it incorporates the Crime Domain Score from the English Indices of Deprivation (2015), the Scottish Index of Multiple Deprivation (SIMD) Crime Score (2012), the Welsh SIMD Crime score (2014), and the Northern Ireland Multiple Deprivation Measure (NIIMD) Crime Score (2010). The resulting data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
