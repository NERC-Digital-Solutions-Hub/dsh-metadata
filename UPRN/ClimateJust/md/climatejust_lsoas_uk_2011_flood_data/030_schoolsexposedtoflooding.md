### % no. of schools exposed to flooding

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of schools in a given area that are exposed to a flood risk of 1:75 or greater. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Service availability" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of local services at risk of flooding indicates a higher level of social vulnerability, as a community will experience much greater overall impacts if its essential services are affected. The inclusion of schools specifically is based on the following impacts:
* **Family Disruption and Dislocation:** If a school floods, children often have to be temporarily transferred to other schools that may be a significant distance away while the original building is restored. 
* **Increased Stress:** This logistical relocation adds significant disruption to daily family life, routines, and community networks, thereby increasing the overall vulnerability of those affected.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the 2015 Climate Change Risk Assessment (CCRA) by Sayers et al., using a 2011 baseline. It is calculated by querying school site locations against flood hazard data to identify the proportion of sites at risk of flooding at a 1:75 probability level or greater. 

*Note on Geography:* Unlike many other supporting variables that are mapped to the highly localized neighbourhood level (LSOA/Data Zone), this data is reported and mapped at the broader Local Authority (LA) level to avoid explicitly identifying the vulnerabilities of particular community services.
