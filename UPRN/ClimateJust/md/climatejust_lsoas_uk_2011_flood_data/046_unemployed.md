### % Unemployed

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of the economically active population aged 16 to 74 who are unemployed. It serves as a supporting variable (often referenced as i1) for the "Income" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Inability to Prepare," "Inability to Respond," and "Inability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
Unemployment is a recognized proxy indicator for financial deprivation and low income. A higher proportion of unemployed people in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. A lack of financial resources significantly restricts a person's ability to adapt to extreme weather events. Specific factors include:
*   **Reduced Preparation Capacity:** Lower-income households are less likely to have the financial means to fully prepare for future floods, such as by purchasing comprehensive home insurance or installing property-level flood protection measures.
*   **Housing Limitations:** Unemployed individuals on lower incomes are less likely to own their own homes; being a tenant can further restrict a person's autonomy to make physical modifications to their property. 
*   **Restricted Recovery:** A lack of savings drastically limits a household's ability to respond immediately to flood damage, as they may not be able to afford the initial repairs and replacements needed to kick-start the recovery process.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table KS501 (percentage of unemployed people in the population aged 16-74). The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
