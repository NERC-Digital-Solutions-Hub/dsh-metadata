### % no. of GP surgeries exposed to flooding

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of GP surgeries in a given area that are at risk of flooding at a probability level of 1:75 or greater. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Service availability" indicator, which is used to assess the "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of services exposed to flood risk in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability. The inclusion of this metric is based on the assumption that a community will suffer more severe overall impacts if its local services are compromised. Specifically, if GP surgeries are flooded, residents will face significant difficulties accessing essential medical care and health services during and immediately following an extreme weather event. 

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the Climate Change Risk Assessment (CCRA) by Sayers et al. (2015), utilizing a 2011 baseline. It is processed by querying the geographic locations of GP surgeries against flood hazard data to identify the proportion of sites at risk of a 1:75 or greater flood. Note that to avoid identifying the specific locations of particular community services, this data is reported and mapped at the wider Local Authority (LA) level across England, Wales, Scotland, and Northern Ireland.
