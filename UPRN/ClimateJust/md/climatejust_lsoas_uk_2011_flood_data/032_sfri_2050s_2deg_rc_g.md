### River & Coastal | Group | Future 2050s 2℃ scenario

**Description:** 
This field (underlying alias: `SFRI_2050s_2Deg_RC_G`) represents the Social Flood Risk Index (SFRI) for combined river (fluvial) and coastal flooding under a future 2050s climate scenario, which assumes a 2°C rise in Global Mean Temperature (GMT) from a 1961-90 baseline. The SFRI is a measure of geographic flood disadvantage, highlighting areas where high social vulnerability and physical exposure to flooding coincide.

**"Group" Measure:** 
This metric is calculated as a population-weighted "group" measure at the neighbourhood scale. It incorporates three factors: 
1. The chance of flooding occurring in the floodplain (accounting for existing flood defences).
2. The number of people living within that floodplain.
3. The overall social vulnerability of the neighbourhood, determined by the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). 

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
The SFRI is a relative index with no defined units. 
* **High positive scores** identify highly disadvantaged neighbourhoods where large numbers of the most socially vulnerable people are exposed to flooding. 
* **High negative scores** result from high numbers of people living in the floodplain but within a neighbourhood that has low overall social vulnerability. 
* **A value of zero** indicates that no one in that neighbourhood lives in the floodplain.

**Data Source:** 
The data stems from the 2017 UK-scale assessment of present and future flood vulnerability, risk, and disadvantage produced by Sayers and Partners LLP for the Joseph Rowntree Foundation. The data is mapped to census geographies, corresponding to Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs) in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZs) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOAs) in Northern Ireland.
