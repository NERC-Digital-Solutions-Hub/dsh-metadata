### River & Coastal | Individual | Future 2050s 4℃ scenario

**Description:** 
This field (underlying alias: `SFRI4FCI`) represents the Social Flood Risk Index (SFRI) for combined river (fluvial) and coastal flooding under a future 2050s climate scenario,. This specific scenario assumes a 4°C rise in Global Mean Temperature (GMT) from a 1961-1990 baseline, assuming a continuation in current levels of adaptation and high population growth,,. The SFRI acts as a measure of geographic flood disadvantage by identifying areas where social vulnerability and physical exposure to flooding coincide,.

**The "Individual" Measure:** 
Unlike the "Group" measure, which looks at the total accumulated risk for a population, the "Individual" measure provides an "average" or "per person" risk estimate for the neighbourhood,,. It incorporates the chance of flooding occurring in the floodplain (accounting for existing defences) combined with the overall social vulnerability of the neighbourhood (NFVI),. It is calculated by dividing the SFRI Group measure by the total floodplain population,,. This metric is particularly useful for identifying neighbourhoods where the vulnerability of the exposed residents is very high, even if the total number of people actually exposed to the hazard is relatively small,,.

**Vulnerability Rationale / Score Interpretation:** 
The SFRI is a relative index and has no defined units,,. 
* **High positive scores** indicate neighbourhoods where the individuals exposed to flooding are highly socially vulnerable,,. 
* **Negative values** indicate that although people are exposed to flooding in that area, the neighbourhood's overall social vulnerability is relatively low (falling below the UK average),,. 
* **A value of zero** indicates that no one in that neighbourhood lives in the floodplain for this scenario,,.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2017 UK-scale assessment of present and future flood vulnerability, risk, and disadvantage produced by Sayers and Partners LLP for the Joseph Rowntree Foundation,,.
