### % households with at least one person with long term limiting illness

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of households in a given area that contain one or more persons with a long-term health problem or disability. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Health" indicator, which is used to assess the "Susceptibility" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of households with individuals in poor health indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. The inclusion of this metric is based on the evidence that the long-term sick are more vulnerable to the impacts of a flood, as the experience can exacerbate their pre-existing conditions either as a sudden shock or by accelerating an adverse trajectory. Specific vulnerabilities include:
* **Restricted Medical Access:** Flooding and emergency evacuations can result in the loss of, damage to, or having to leave behind essential medicines.
* **Disruption of Home Care Equipment:** Flood damage and power outages can completely prevent the use of complex, life-sustaining home-based healthcare systems, such as home dialysis machines.
* **Interrupted Caregiver Support:** During a flood, disrupted transport routes and telephone connectivity often make it extremely difficult for carers to contact and physically reach patients receiving care at home.
* **Exacerbated Psychological Stress:** The stress of a flood event can have severe and long-lasting mental health impacts—such as PTSD, depression, and anxiety—and those with pre-existing mental or physical health conditions are likely to suffer the most.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table KS106. It is calculated by dividing the number of households containing one or more persons with a long-term health problem or disability by the total number of households, and multiplying the result by 100. The spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
