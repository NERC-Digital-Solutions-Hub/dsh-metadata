### Long-term unemployed (% who are LTU or who have never worked)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of people aged 16 to 74 in a given area who are long-term unemployed or have never worked. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Income" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Inability to Prepare," "Ability to Respond," and "Inability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of people who are long-term unemployed or have never worked indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. This metric serves as a strong proxy for low income and financial deprivation. The rationale for its inclusion is based on the following key factors:
* **Severe Financial Constraints:** While general unemployment restricts a household's capacity to prepare for, respond to, and recover from a flood, these challenges are significantly heightened for the long-term unemployed. 
* **Lack of Accumulated Wealth:** Evidence shows that the average levels of wealth for households headed by someone who is long-term unemployed or has never worked are just 3% of the wealth held by those in 'large employer or higher managerial' occupations. This drastic lack of savings or financial buffer makes it incredibly difficult to replace damaged goods, pay for repairs, or find alternative accommodation after an extreme weather event.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table KS611. It is calculated by taking the number of people aged 16 to 74 classified as 'never worked and long-term unemployed', dividing it by the total population of people aged 16 to 74, and multiplying by 100. The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
