### Disability (% people whose day-to-day activities are limited a lot)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of people in a given area who report that their day-to-day activities are "limited a lot" due to a long-term health problem or disability. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Physical mobility" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Ability to Respond" and "Ability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). 

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
Areas with larger proportions of people whose day-to-day activities are highly limited are more likely to be communities with broader mobility problems, which translates to higher social vulnerability during an extreme weather event. Limited physical mobility creates several practical challenges in preparing for, responding to, and recovering from a flood:
* **Reliance on Assistance:** People with reduced mobility are often highly reliant on others to assist them during an evacuation, whether they are living independently in their own homes or in serviced accommodation. 
* **Disruption of Care and Equipment:** Disruption caused by flooding and power loss can prevent carers from physically reaching those they care for, and can leave vital assistance tools—such as electronic lifts—completely unusable. This severely reduces a person's capacity to help themselves.
* **Increased Resource Needs:** Someone with a severe disability generally requires a higher amount of resources and specific planning to reach a safe level of well-being during a crisis, which must be factored into disaster management and evacuation plans.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table KS301. It is based on the percentage of the population who answer that their day-to-day activities are "limited a lot". The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
