### River & Coastal | Group | Present day

**Description:** 
This field (underlying alias: `SFRI_PresentDay_RC_G`) represents the Social Flood Risk Index (SFRI) for combined river (fluvial) and coastal flooding under present-day conditions. The SFRI serves as a measure of geographic flood disadvantage by identifying areas where social vulnerability and physical exposure to flooding coincide.

**The "Group" Measure:** 
This metric is calculated as a "group" measure at the neighbourhood scale. It provides an estimate of total accumulated risk for the population by combining three key factors:
1. The chance of flooding occurring in the floodplain (the expected annual probability of flooding, accounting for existing flood defences).
2. The number of people living within that floodplain.
3. The overall social vulnerability of the neighbourhood, as determined by the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale / Score Interpretation:** 
The SFRI is a relative index and has no defined units. 
* **High positive scores** identify highly disadvantaged neighbourhoods where large numbers of the most socially vulnerable people are exposed to river and coastal flooding. 
* **Negative values** indicate that although high numbers of people are exposed to flooding in that area, the neighbourhood's overall social vulnerability is relatively low (falling below the UK average). 
* **A value of zero** indicates that no one in that neighbourhood lives in the floodplain.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2017 UK-scale assessment of present and future flood vulnerability, risk, and disadvantage produced by Sayers and Partners LLP for the Joseph Rowntree Foundation. The spatial data is mapped down to the neighbourhood level, corresponding to Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs) in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZs) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOAs) in Northern Ireland.
