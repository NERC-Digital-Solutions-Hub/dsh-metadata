### Households with dependent children and no adults in employment (%)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of households in a given area that have dependent children but no adults in employment. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Income" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Inability to Prepare," "Inability to Respond," and "Inability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of unemployed households with dependent children indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding, primarily because these households are highly likely to be on lower incomes relative to others. The inclusion of this specific metric is based on the following factors:
* **Financial Restrictions:** Unemployment is a recognised indicator of financial deprivation. A lack of financial resources significantly restricts a household's ability to afford the preparations for, immediate response to, and long-term recovery from flood events.
* **Compounded Stress:** When unemployed individuals have additional caring responsibilities for dependent children, it places further practical and emotional stresses upon them during an extreme event.
* **Insufficient Financial Support:** Despite potentially receiving additional financial assistance compared to unemployed households without children, this support is often insufficient to fully compensate for the extra financial burdens required to effectively manage and recover from the impacts of a flood.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically using Census table KS106. It is calculated by taking the number of households categorized as "No adults in employment in household: With dependent children," dividing it by the total number of households, and multiplying by 100. Data is mapped to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
