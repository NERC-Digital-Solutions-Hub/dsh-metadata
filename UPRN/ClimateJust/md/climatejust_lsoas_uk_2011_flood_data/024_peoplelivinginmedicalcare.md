### % people living in medical and care establishments

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of the total population in a given area who reside in medical and care establishments. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Physical mobility" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Ability to Respond" and "Ability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of people living in medical and care establishments indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. The inclusion of this metric is based on the practical challenges posed by limited physical mobility during an extreme event:
* **Evacuation Constraints:** People living in these facilities generally have lower personal and physical mobility, making them significantly less capable of independently responding to a crisis or moving out of an affected area during a flood.
* **Reliance on Assistance:** Individuals in care are highly reliant on others to assist them with evacuation and daily needs. During a flood, disrupted transport routes can prevent carers from reaching them, and power outages can leave vital assistance tools—such as electronic lifts or medical equipment—completely unusable.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table QS421SC. It is calculated by taking the number of people living in 'Medical and care establishments', dividing it by the total population, and multiplying by 100. The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
