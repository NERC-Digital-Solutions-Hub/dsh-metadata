### Older people (% people over 75 years)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of people aged 75 years or older out of the total population in a given area. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Age" indicator, which is used to assess the "Susceptibility" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of people over 75 in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. This demographic is considered highly sensitive to the impacts of extreme weather events due to the following factors:
* **Higher Mortality Rates:** Historical evidence shows that older people suffer the most severe physical consequences during extreme floods. For example, the number of deaths caused by the 1953 'Big Flood' was highest among older demographics, with people over 60 accounting for 42% of the resulting deaths in Essex.
* **Reluctance to Evacuate:** Older people are generally less likely than other social groups to respond to flood warnings and may be much more reluctant to leave their homes during a crisis.
* **Limited Physical Mobility:** Restricted physical mobility makes it difficult for older individuals to safely move belongings or implement flood defence measures, such as putting up property-level flood gates.
* **General Vulnerability:** In-depth analyses of various UK case studies have consistently shown that individuals over the age of 75 are significantly more vulnerable to the overall impacts of flooding.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics), specifically utilizing Census table 102. It is calculated by taking the number of people aged 75 years or more, dividing it by the total population, and multiplying the result by 100. The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
