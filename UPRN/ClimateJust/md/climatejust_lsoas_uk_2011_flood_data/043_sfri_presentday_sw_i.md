### Surface water | Individual | Present day

**Description:** 
This field (underlying alias: `SFRIPSWI`) represents the Social Flood Risk Index (SFRI) for surface water (pluvial) flooding under present-day conditions. The SFRI serves as a measure of geographic flood disadvantage by identifying areas where physical exposure to flooding and social vulnerability coincide.

**The "Individual" Measure:** 
While the "Group" measure evaluates the total accumulated risk for a population, the "Individual" measure provides an "average" or "per person" risk estimate for the neighbourhood. It incorporates the chance of flooding occurring in the floodplain (accounting for existing flood defences) combined with the overall social vulnerability of the neighbourhood, as determined by the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). It is calculated by dividing the SFRI Group measure by the total floodplain population. This specific metric is highly useful for identifying neighbourhoods where the vulnerability of the exposed residents is very high, even if the actual number of people exposed to the surface water hazard is relatively small.

**Vulnerability Rationale / Score Interpretation:** 
The SFRI is a relative index and has no defined units. 
* **High positive scores** indicate neighbourhoods where the individuals exposed to flooding are highly socially vulnerable. 
* **Negative values** indicate that although people are exposed to surface water flooding in that area, the neighbourhood's overall social vulnerability is relatively low (falling below the UK average). 
* **A value of zero** indicates that no one in that neighbourhood lives in the floodplain.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2017 UK-scale assessment of present and future flood vulnerability, risk, and disadvantage produced by Sayers and Partners LLP for the Joseph Rowntree Foundation. The spatial data is mapped down to the neighbourhood scale, which corresponds to Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs) in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZs) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOAs) in Northern Ireland.
