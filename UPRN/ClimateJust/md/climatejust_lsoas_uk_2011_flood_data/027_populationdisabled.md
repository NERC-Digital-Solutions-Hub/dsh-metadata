### High levels of disability (% of population who are disabled)

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of the population in a given area who are disabled, specifically those who report that their day-to-day activities are "limited a lot". It serves as a supporting variable for the "Physical mobility" indicator, which is used to assess a community's "Ability to Respond" and "Ability to Recover" characteristics within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI).

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of people with low personal, physical mobility in an area indicates a higher level of social vulnerability to flooding. Limited physical mobility creates numerous practical challenges during an extreme event, meaning the overall impacts tend to be greater. The inclusion of this metric is based on the following factors:
* **Evacuation Constraints:** Disabilities result in a significantly impaired ability to independently move out of danger or safely evacuate. 
* **Reliance on Assistance:** People with reduced mobility are often heavily reliant on others to assist them during an evacuation.
* **Disruption of Care and Equipment:** Flooding and power outages can prevent carers from reaching the people they care for, and can render vital assistance tools—such as electronic lifts—completely unusable.
* **Increased Resource Requirements:** Someone with a severe disability typically requires a higher amount of targeted resources, planning, and support to reach a safe level of well-being during a disaster compared to someone without a disability.

**Data Source:** 
The data is derived from the 2011 UK Census (Office for National Statistics). It is calculated based on the percentage of the population who indicate their activities are "limited a lot". The resulting spatial data is mapped down to the Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) level in England and Wales, Data Zones (DZ) in Scotland, and Super Output Areas (SOA) in Northern Ireland.
