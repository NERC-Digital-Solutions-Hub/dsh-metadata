### % no. of care homes exposed to flooding

**Description:** 
This field represents the percentage of care homes in a given area that are at risk of flooding at a probability level of 1:75 or greater. It serves as a supporting variable for the "Service availability" indicator, which is used to assess the "Community Support" characteristic within the Neighbourhood Flood Vulnerability Index (NFVI). 

**Vulnerability Rationale:** 
A higher proportion of services exposed to flood risk indicates greater social vulnerability, as a community will suffer more severe overall impacts if its local care and health services are compromised. The specific inclusion of care home exposure is based on several factors:
* **Evacuation of Vulnerable People:** If a care or nursing home floods, its highly vulnerable residents must be evacuated and suitable alternative placements must be found for them.
* **Loss of Safe Havens:** Care homes frequently act as temporary safe havens, taking in vulnerable residents who have been evacuated from their own flooded homes. This community support mechanism is severely hampered if the care home itself is flooded.
* **Disruption to Staffing:** When a care home is situated in a flood-prone area, its employees are also likely to live in the flood risk area or face significant travel difficulties trying to reach the facility to provide care.

**Data Source:** 
The data is sourced from the Climate Change Risk Assessment (CCRA) by Sayers et al. (2015), using a 2011 baseline. It is processed by querying the geographic locations of care homes against flood hazard data. Note that to protect the identity and locations of specific vulnerable community services, this data is reported and mapped at the wider Local Authority (LA) level.
