### Lack of local greenspace access (%)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `LackLocalGreenspace` in the spatial dataset) provides the raw percentage of the population or households lacking access to public greenspaces within a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator feeds into the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension of the Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index. 

The core assumption is that a community's capacity to respond to extreme weather, particularly severe heatwaves, is influenced by its surrounding physical environment. Public parks and local greenspaces act as essential informal refuges that provide a cooling function when residential buildings overheat. Neighbourhoods with a high percentage of residents lacking access to these outdoor spaces restrict people's ability to find cooler environments, thereby exacerbating the risk of heat stress and increasing social vulnerability.

**Interpretation:**
Because this field measures the absolute percentage of the *lack* of a resource, it is interpreted as follows:
*   **High values:** Denote a high percentage of the area's population lacking access to local greenspace. This indicates limited options for finding cool outdoor refuges during a heatwave, resulting in **higher social vulnerability**.
*   **Low values:** Denote a low percentage of people lacking access (meaning the community has good, widespread access to public greenspaces). This indicates better capacity to cope with extreme heat and translates to **lower social vulnerability**.

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset update, the data for this indicator is derived from the Office for National Statistics (ONS) dataset: "Access to public green space in Great Britain".
