### Flood alert and warning areas (% area not covered)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `FloodWarnings` in the spatial dataset) represents the percentage of a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA) that is *not* covered by official flood alert and warning areas. It serves to measure the local lack of formal early warning coverage for flood events within a neighbourhood.

**Vulnerability Rationale:**
This metric is an indicator utilized to evaluate a community's "(In)ability to Prepare" and "(In)ability to Respond" to flooding. 

Applying necessary adaptive measures—such as safely evacuating, moving valuable possessions, or installing temporary property-level flood defences—requires advance warning that an event may occur. There is often very little time (sometimes two hours or less) to react to a rapid flood event. If an area lacks formal coverage by official warning systems (like the Environment Agency's Floodline), residents are less likely to receive the timely alerts needed to trigger a response, which significantly increases their overall social vulnerability and risk of experiencing severe impacts. 

**Interpretation:**
Because having flood warning coverage provides a protective function (i.e., higher coverage equals better ability to prepare and lower vulnerability), the data for this indicator is specifically processed to show the proportion of the area *not* covered. 
*   **Higher percentages** denote a greater lack of official flood warning coverage, indicating less community ability to prepare or respond, and thus a **higher** level of social vulnerability.
*   **Lower percentages** signify that a larger proportion of the area is well-covered by flood alerts and warnings, indicating greater capacity to act and lower relative vulnerability.

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset, the underlying data for this indicator is derived from the Environment Agency (specifically the 2020 Flood Alert and Warning Areas dataset, last updated in August 2021). When used to calculate specific composite vulnerability indices, such as the "Inability to Prepare" or "Inability to Respond" indices, this indicator carries a weight of 0.333.
