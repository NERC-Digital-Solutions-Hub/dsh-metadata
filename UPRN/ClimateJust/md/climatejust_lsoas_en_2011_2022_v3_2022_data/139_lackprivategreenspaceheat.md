### Lack of domestic gardens (% dwellings without gardens)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally in the spatial dataset under names such as `LackPrivateGreenspaceHeat` and `LackPrivateGreenspaceFlood`) represents the raw percentage of residential dwellings that do not have access to a private domestic garden within a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator is utilized across multiple dimensions of the socio-spatial climate vulnerability framework. It is a core component of the **Physical Environment** domain within the **Enhanced Exposure** index for both flood and heat hazards. Additionally, it feeds into the **General infrastructure** domain within the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension for heat vulnerability.

The underlying assumption is that domestic gardens provide vital environmental services that offset the severity of climate events. Gardens offer essential cooling functions during severe heatwaves and act as permeable surfaces that improve water drainage during heavy rainfall. Furthermore, they provide a private outdoor refuge for residents during hot weather. Consequently, neighbourhoods with a high proportion of homes lacking private gardens are more likely to experience accentuated impacts from these extreme weather events.

**Interpretation:**
Unlike its standardized Z-score counterpart, this field provides the absolute percentage rate for the neighbourhood:
*   **High values:** Denote an area with a high percentage of dwellings without private gardens. This indicates a built environment that accentuates heat and flood exposure and provides fewer natural cooling or drainage benefits, translating to **higher social vulnerability**.
*   **Low values:** Denote an area where a large majority of dwellings possess private gardens. This points to an environment that helps offset hazard exposure, translating to **lower social vulnerability**.

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset update, the data for this indicator is derived from the Office for National Statistics (ONS) Greenspace dataset ("Access to public green space in Great Britain").
