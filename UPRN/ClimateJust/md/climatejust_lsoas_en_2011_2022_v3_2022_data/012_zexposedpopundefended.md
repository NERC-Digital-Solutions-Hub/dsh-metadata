### Exposed Population Undefended (Z-Score)

**Description:**
This field represents the standardized Z-score for the estimated count of the population living within designated flood zones that are *not* protected by recorded flood defences in a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). It measures how the scale of potential undefended flood exposure in a specific neighbourhood compares to the national average across England. 

**Interpretation:**
The Z-score allows for a direct relative comparison across different geographic areas:
*   **Positive values** denote that more people are potentially exposed to flooding without recorded defences than the national average.
*   **Values around 0** indicate that the number of undefended people exposed is approximately average for England.
*   **Negative values** signify that fewer people are potentially exposed without defences than the national average.

To help categorize the relative severity of this exposure, the Z-scores are grouped into the following standard classifications used throughout the dataset:
*   **≥ 2.5:** Acute
*   **1.5 to 2.5:** Extremely high
*   **0.5 to 1.5:** Relatively high
*   **-0.5 to 0.5:** Average
*   **-1.5 to -0.5:** Relatively low
*   **-2.5 to -1.5:** Extremely low
*   **≤ -2.5:** Slight

**Methodological Caveats:**
Because this metric is a standardized score derived from the underlying "Exposed Population Undefended (Count)", it inherits the same limitations. It serves as a broad area-based estimate, and because not all defended areas are mapped (such as small river and coastal defences, as well as surface water interventions), some populations driving a high Z-score may actually benefit from unmapped local protections.
