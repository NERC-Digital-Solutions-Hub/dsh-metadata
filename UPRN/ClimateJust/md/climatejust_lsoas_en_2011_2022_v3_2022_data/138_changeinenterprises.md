### Change in the number of enterprises (%)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `ChangeInEnterprises` in the spatial dataset) represents the percentage change in the number of active businesses and enterprises within a local area. 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator is a component of the **General infrastructure** domain, which feeds into the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension of the socio-spatial heat vulnerability index. 

The core assumption behind this metric is that local shops, retail units, and other community-related commercial infrastructure can act as vital informal refuges during extreme weather events (for example, by providing temporary air-conditioned or cooler spaces during a severe heatwave). 

**Interpretation:**
Because this field measures a trajectory of growth or decline, its interpretation in terms of vulnerability is the inverse of many other indicators in the dataset:
*   **Positive values (Growth):** Denote an area experiencing an increase in the number of enterprises. Higher positive values indicate **lower social vulnerability**, as the community is maintaining or expanding its local infrastructure and potential refuges.
*   **Negative values (Decline):** Denote an area experiencing a loss in the number of enterprises. A declining level of infrastructure indicates **higher social vulnerability**, as residents have fewer informal refuges available, which is also generally indicative of wider community deprivation and decline.

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset update, the data used to calculate this metric is derived from the UK Business Counts 2021 (enterprises by industry and employment size band).
