### Socio-Spatial Flood Vulnerability Index (SSFVI)

**Description:**
This field represents the overall mapped social vulnerability of a neighbourhood with respect to flood-related hazards. It illustrates how various personal, social, and environmental factors come together to explain the uneven impacts of flooding on different people and communities, highlighting specific neighbourhoods where negative social impacts are most likely to occur. 

When combined with data on the likelihood of actual flood events occurring, this index helps determine the overall "climate disadvantage" of an area.

**Domains:**
The SSFVI is calculated as an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level Z-scores across the five key dimensions of socio-spatial vulnerability:
*   **Sensitivity:** Personal biophysical characteristics (such as age and health) that affect the likelihood of experiencing negative health and welfare impacts during a flood.
*   **(In)ability to Prepare:** Social factors (such as income, property tenure, language proficiency, internet access, and local knowledge) that govern a community's capacity to take precautions before a flood.
*   **(In)ability to Respond:** Social factors (such as social networks and physical mobility) that affect the capacity to take immediate, effective action during a flood.
*   **(In)ability to Recover:** Social factors (such as income, housing mobility, and access to services like pharmacies) that dictate the ability to bounce back after a flood event has passed.
*   **Enhanced Exposure:** Aspects of the physical and built environment (such as the availability of green space or specific housing characteristics) that tend to accentuate or offset the physical severity of flood events.

**Interpretation:**
The index is represented as a Z-score to measure relative vulnerability. The scores are classified into the following categories to indicate the level of vulnerability:
*   **≥ 2.5:** Acute
*   **1.5 to 2.5:** Extremely high
*   **0.5 to 1.5:** Relatively high
*   **-0.5 to 0.5:** Average
*   **-1.5 to -0.5:** Relatively low
*   **-2.5 to -1.5:** Extremely low
*   **≤ -2.5:** Slight
