### Inability to Prepare Index

**Description:**
This field represents the "(In)ability to Prepare" dimension of the Socio-Spatial Climate Vulnerability Index. It evaluates the underlying social factors that govern a person's or community's capacity to take precautionary measures ahead of climate and extreme weather events, such as floods and heatwaves.

**Domains:**
The index is calculated as an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores across five key domains:
*   **Income:** Measures financial deprivation, including the proportion of employment-deprived and income-deprived people in the area.
*   **Tenure:** Accounts for the proportion of households renting from private, social, or council landlords, as renters typically have less autonomy or financial incentive to permanently modify their homes to withstand extreme weather.
*   **Language:** Measures the level of proficiency in English within the population, which affects the ability to access and understand preparedness information.
*   **Internet:** Evaluates the proportion of premises with sub-standard broadband or a lack of superfast broadband, impacting access to online resources and alerts.
*   **Local Knowledge:** Considers factors such as population transience (residential mobility) and a lack of historical flood experience, which can leave residents less aware of local environmental risks.

**Interpretation:**
The index is represented as a Z-score, which classifies the relative vulnerability of a neighbourhood compared to the national average. The scores are grouped into categories ranging from **Slight** (≤ -2.5) to **Acute** (≥ 2.5). Higher positive scores identify communities that face significant social and resource barriers to preparing for extreme weather events.
