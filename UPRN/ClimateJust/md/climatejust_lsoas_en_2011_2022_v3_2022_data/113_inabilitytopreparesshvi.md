### Inability to Prepare Index

**Description:**
This field represents the standardized score (Z-score) for the **Inability to Prepare** dimension of socio-spatial vulnerability within a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). A person’s or community’s ability to prepare for climate and extreme weather events, such as heatwaves and floods, is governed primarily by underlying social factors. 

**Vulnerability Context:**
The index is a composite metric calculated as an equally weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores from several key socio-economic domains:
1.  **Income:** Indicators include the percentage of the population experiencing employment deprivation, general income deprivation, and income deprivation specifically affecting older people.
2.  **Tenure:** Indicators include the percentage of households renting from social or council landlords, as well as those renting privately, since renters often have a lower ability to adapt or modify their homes.
3.  **Language:** Assessed by the level of proficiency in English within the population, which affects the ability to obtain and use information or guidance.
4.  **Internet:** Accounts for the percentage of premises with sub-standard broadband or a lack of access to superfast broadband, which can impact access to emergency information and alerts.
5.  **Local Knowledge:** Includes factors such as population transience (residential mobility). In the context of flood vulnerability, this domain also accounts for a lack of past experience with historical flooding and the proportion of the area not covered by official flood alerts and warnings.

**Interpretation:**
Because the index is presented as a standardized Z-score, it evaluates how a specific neighbourhood's inability to prepare compares to the overall average across England:
*   **Positive values (High scores):** Denote a higher inability to prepare compared to the English average, indicating greater vulnerability. Scores ≥ 2.5 are classified as "Acute", scores between 1.5 and 2.5 are "Extremely high", and scores between 0.5 and 1.5 are "Relatively high".
*   **Values around 0 (-0.5 to 0.5):** Denote that the area's inability to prepare is approximately equal to the national "Average".
*   **Negative values (Low scores):** Denote a lower inability to prepare (i.e., the community is relatively more able to prepare) compared to the English average, scaling down through "Relatively low" (-0.5 to -1.5), "Extremely low" (-1.5 to -2.5), and "Slight" (≤ -2.5).
