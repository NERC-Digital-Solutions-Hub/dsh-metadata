### Change in the number of enterprises (%) (Z-Score)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `ZChangeInEnterprises` in the spatial dataset) represents the standardized score (Z-score) for the percentage change in the number of active businesses and enterprises within a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator falls under the **General Infrastructure** domain within the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension of socio-spatial heat vulnerability. 

The core assumption behind using this metric is that local shops, retail units, and other community-related infrastructure can act as vital informal refuges during extreme weather events—for example, by providing access to air-conditioned or cooler spaces during a severe heatwave. 

*   **Declining Infrastructure:** Neighbourhoods experiencing a loss or decline in the number of enterprises are assumed to have **higher social vulnerability**. The loss of such infrastructure erodes community resilience, reduces available refuges, and is often indicative of wider neighbourhood decline and deprivation. 
*   **Growing Infrastructure:** Conversely, areas where retail and community infrastructure is increasing tend to exhibit enhanced community resilience and a better overall capacity to respond to extreme events.

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset, the raw data used to calculate this standardized score is derived from the UK Business Counts 2021 (enterprises by industry and employment size band).
