### Enhanced Exposure Index

**Description:**
This field represents the "Enhanced Exposure" dimension of the Socio-Spatial Climate Vulnerability Index. It evaluates aspects of the local physical and built environment that tend to accentuate or mitigate the severity of climate hazards, such as extreme heat and flooding. This dimension accounts for socially-derived physical factors that can be managed through local adaptation, which may not be fully captured in standard biophysical hazard-exposure mapping. 

**Domains:**
Depending on whether it is evaluating flood or heat vulnerability, the index is calculated as an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores across the following domains:
*   **Physical Environment:** Measures land cover characteristics, including the proportion of built-up areas (lacking greenspace), the lack of private domestic gardens, and the median size of available public parks and playing fields within a 1,000m radius.
*   **Housing Characteristics:** Evaluates the types of buildings present that may amplify hazard impacts, such as the proportion of residents living in caravans or temporary mobile structures (vulnerable to floods), or high-rise homes on the 5th floor or above (vulnerable to heat).
*   **Physical Geography:** Encompasses location-based physical factors such as average elevation or distance to the coast (which can influence cooling during heatwaves).

**Interpretation:**
The index is represented as a Z-score to measure relative enhanced exposure compared to the national average. The scores are classified into categories ranging from **Slight** (≤ -2.5) to **Acute** (≥ 2.5). Higher positive scores identify neighbourhoods where the physical environment significantly amplifies the impact of extreme weather events. Conversely, negative scores indicate areas where the local environment helps to offset or mitigate the hazard, such as neighbourhoods with abundant greenspace that provides better natural drainage and cooling functions.
