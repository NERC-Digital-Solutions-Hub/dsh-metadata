### Density of retail units (Number of enterprises divided by the area of MSOA) (Z-Score)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `ZRetailUnitsDensity` in the spatial dataset) represents the standardized score (Z-score) of the density of retail businesses within a local area. It is calculated by dividing the number of active retail enterprises by the geographic area of the Middle Layer Super Output Area (MSOA). 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator is a component of the "General infrastructure" domain within the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension of the Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI). 

The core assumption behind this metric is that local shops and community-related commercial infrastructure can act as informal refuges during extreme weather events. For example, during a severe heatwave, retail units with air conditioning can provide temporary cooler spaces for residents who are struggling with heat stress in their own homes. Therefore, communities with a relative lack of retail infrastructure have a lower capacity to respond to heatwaves.

**Interpretation:**
Because this field directly measures the *density* of retail units as a standardized Z-score, its interpretation differs slightly from the overall vulnerability indices:
*   **Positive values (High scores):** Denote an area with a *higher* density of retail units compared to the national average. In the context of climate disadvantage, higher values for this specific indicator represent **lower social vulnerability**, as the community has better access to potential informal refuges.
*   **Negative values (Low scores):** Denote an area with a *lower* density of retail units than average, indicating a lack of commercial infrastructure and therefore **higher social vulnerability**. 

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England dataset update, the underlying data for this indicator is derived from the UK Business Counts 2021 (enterprises by industry and employment size band).
