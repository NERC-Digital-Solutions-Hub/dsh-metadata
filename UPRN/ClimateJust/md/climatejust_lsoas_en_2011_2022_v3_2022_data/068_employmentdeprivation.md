### Employment deprived people in an area (% of working population)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `EmploymentDeprivation` in the spatial dataset) represents the percentage of the working-age population who are involuntarily excluded from the labour market within a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). It measures the local concentration of employment deprivation to serve as a proxy for financial disadvantage. 

**Vulnerability Rationale:**
This metric is a core indicator within the "Income" domain, which is utilized to evaluate a community's "(In)ability to Prepare", "(In)ability to Respond", and "(In)ability to Recover" from extreme climate events, such as floods and heatwaves. 

Employment deprivation acts as a strong proxy for general financial deprivation. A lack of financial resources significantly restricts a person's adaptive capacity during an extreme weather event for several reasons:
*   **Preparation:** Low-income or unemployed households are less likely to have the financial capacity to prepare for future floods or heatwaves, such as by affording property-level protection measures, cooling systems, or adequate home insurance.
*   **Response and Recovery:** A lack of financial resources makes it much harder to cope with the immediate aftermath of an extreme event, such as replacing damaged goods, affording temporary relocation, or paying for increased energy bills. 
*   **Compounding Factors:** Unemployment is strongly linked to other forms of social vulnerability, including a greater tendency to experience poor physical and mental health, and a higher likelihood of living in rented accommodation (where tenants are often restricted from making physical adaptations to the property).

**Interpretation:**
When used to calculate the final vulnerability indices, this raw percentage is standardized to allow for direct relative comparisons across different geographic areas:
*   **Higher percentages** denote a higher concentration of employment-deprived individuals, indicating lower average incomes and a higher level of social vulnerability.
*   **Lower percentages** signify higher employment rates, indicating greater financial capacity and lower relative vulnerability.

**Data Source:**
For the updated 2022 England LSOA dataset, the underlying data for this indicator is derived from the English Indices of Deprivation 2019 (IMD 2019). Specifically, it utilizes the Employment Deprivation Domain, sourced via the Department for Work and Pensions, converted into a percentage of the working population. It carries a weight of 0.333 within the broader "Income" domain, sharing this dimension with the indicators for the percentage of income-deprived people and the percentage of income-deprived older people.
