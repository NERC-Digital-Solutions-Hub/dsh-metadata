### Lack of local greenspace access (Z-Score)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `ZLackLocalGreenspace` in the spatial dataset) represents the standardized score (Z-score) for the relative lack of access to local public greenspaces for residents within a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator is a component of the **General infrastructure** domain, which falls under the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension of the Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index. 

The ability of a community to respond to a heatwave is heavily governed by the local built environment. Public parks and greenspaces act as vital informal refuges that offer a cooling function during extreme heat events. Neighbourhoods that lack these local greenspaces restrict the ability of residents to leave dangerously hot homes and find cooler outdoor environments nearby, thereby increasing the community's overall vulnerability to heat stress.

**Interpretation:**
Because this field is presented as a standardized Z-score specifically measuring the *lack* of access, it evaluates how a specific neighbourhood compares to the overall average across England:
*   **Positive values (High scores):** Denote a higher *lack* of local greenspace access compared to the national average. This indicates poorer access to cool outdoor spaces and, consequently, **higher social vulnerability**.
*   **Negative values (Low scores):** Denote a lower *lack* of greenspace access (meaning the area has better-than-average access to public parks and greenspaces). This indicates greater community resilience and **lower social vulnerability**. 

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset, the underlying data used to calculate this standardized score is derived from the Office for National Statistics (ONS) "Access to public green space in Great Britain" dataset.
