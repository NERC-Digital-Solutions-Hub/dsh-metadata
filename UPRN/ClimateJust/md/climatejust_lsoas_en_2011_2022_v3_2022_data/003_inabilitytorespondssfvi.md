### Inability to Respond Index

**Description:**
This field represents the "(In)ability to Respond" dimension of the Socio-Spatial Climate Vulnerability Index. It evaluates the underlying social factors that govern a person's or community's capacity to take immediate, effective action during climate and extreme weather events, such as floods and heatwaves.

**Domains:**
The index is calculated as an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores across several key domains:
*   **Income:** Measures financial deprivation, including the proportion of employment-deprived people, income-deprived people, and income-deprived older adults.
*   **Language:** Evaluates the level of English proficiency within the population, which affects the ability to understand emergency warnings and guidance.
*   **Internet:** Assesses digital connectivity based on the proportion of premises with sub-standard broadband or a lack of superfast broadband, which impacts access to online emergency alerts.
*   **Local Knowledge:** Considers population transience (residential mobility), a lack of historical flood experience, and a lack of coverage by flood alert and warning areas.
*   **Social Networks:** Evaluates potential social isolation, primarily indicated by the percentage of single-pensioner households and a lack of school-related networks.
*   **Mobility:** Assesses general physical mobility and accessibility, incorporating comparative illness and disability ratios, populations living in medical and care facilities, lack of private transport, and workplace accessibility via walking, public transport, or car.
*   **Crime:** Considers the Index of Multiple Deprivation crime score, as high crime rates or fear of crime can make residents reluctant to evacuate or implement protective measures (such as leaving windows open during heatwaves or deploying flood gates if away from home).

**Interpretation:**
The index is represented as a Z-score to measure relative vulnerability compared to the national average. The scores are classified into categories to indicate the level of vulnerability:
*   **≥ 2.5:** Acute
*   **1.5 to 2.5:** Extremely high
*   **0.5 to 1.5:** Relatively high
*   **-0.5 to 0.5:** Average
*   **-1.5 to -0.5:** Relatively low
*   **-2.5 to -1.5:** Extremely low
*   **≤ -2.5:** Slight

Higher positive scores identify neighbourhoods that face significant social barriers to responding during a crisis, indicating communities that may require more targeted emergency support.
