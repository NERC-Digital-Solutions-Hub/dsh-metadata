### Recent past 30 year mean 1990 - 2019 (℃)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `TmaxP95RecentPast` in the spatial dataset) represents the average daily-maximum temperature (in degrees Celsius) of the top 5% hottest days, using climate data from the recent past period of 1990 to 2019. 

**Hazard and Vulnerability Context:**
This metric serves as a baseline indicator of extreme heat exposure. Within the dataset, this climate hazard data is combined equally with the Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI) to calculate the overall "Heat Disadvantage" for a given Lower Super Output Area (LSOA). 

Furthermore, this 1990–2019 baseline allows users to compare recent past conditions against projected future conditions, specifically scenarios where the global mean surface temperature is 1.5°C, 2°C, and 3°C warmer than pre-industrial levels.

**Interpretation:**
*   **Higher values (℃)** denote geographical areas that historically experienced more severe high temperatures during their hottest days between 1990 and 2019, indicating a greater baseline exposure to extreme heat hazards.
*   To facilitate direct comparison between this recent past baseline and the warmer future scenarios, the raw temperature data is also calculated into an "All-relative Z-score" (using the mean and standard deviation across all of the temperature scenarios). 

**Data Source:**
For the updated 2022 England LSOA dataset, this climate data is derived from the analysis of Kennedy-Asser et al. (2022). Their research utilizes regional climate model simulations on a 12km grid over the UK from the Met Office Hadley Centre's UK Climate Projections 2018 (UKCP18).
