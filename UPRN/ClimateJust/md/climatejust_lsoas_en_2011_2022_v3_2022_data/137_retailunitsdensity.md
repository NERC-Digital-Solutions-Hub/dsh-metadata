### Density of retail units (Number of enterprises divided by the area of MSOA)

**Description:**
This field (identified internally as `RetailUnitsDensity` in the spatial dataset) represents the absolute density of retail businesses within a local area. It is calculated by dividing the number of active retail enterprises by the geographic area of the Middle Layer Super Output Area (MSOA). 

**Vulnerability Context:**
This indicator is a component of the **General infrastructure** domain, which feeds into the **(In)ability to Respond** dimension of the Socio-Spatial Heat Vulnerability Index (SSHVI). 

The core assumption behind this metric is that local shops, retail units, and other community-related infrastructure can act as vital informal refuges during extreme weather events. For instance, during a severe heatwave, retail units equipped with air conditioning can provide temporary cooler spaces for residents struggling with heat stress in their own homes. Communities with lower densities of infrastructure capable of performing this function are assumed to have a lower capacity to respond to heat events.

**Interpretation:**
Because this field measures the *availability* of a resource (retail density), its interpretation is the inverse of most other indicators in the ClimateJust portal (where high values typically mean high vulnerability):
*   **High values:** Denote an area with a high density of retail units. In the context of climate disadvantage, higher values for this indicator represent **lower social vulnerability**, as the community has greater access to potential informal refuges.
*   **Low values:** Denote an area with a low density of retail units. This lack of commercial infrastructure translates to **higher social vulnerability**.

**Data Source:**
For the 2022 England LSOA dataset update, the data used for this indicator is derived from the UK Business Counts 2021 (enterprises by industry and employment size band).
