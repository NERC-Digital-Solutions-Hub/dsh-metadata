### Sensitivity Index

**Description:**
This field represents the "Sensitivity" dimension of the Socio-Spatial Climate Vulnerability Index. It measures the personal biophysical characteristics of a neighbourhood's population that affect the likelihood that a heatwave or flood event will have negative health and welfare impacts (for example, older people tend to be more susceptible to the effects of high temperatures). 

The index is calculated as an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores across two main domains:
*   **Age:** Driven by the proportion of young children (under 5 years) and older people (over 75 years).
*   **Health:** A composite of indicators that includes emergency hospital admissions, comparative illness and disability ratios, mood and anxiety disorder indicators, medical and care residents, years of potential life lost, and homelessness rates.

**Interpretation:**
The Sensitivity Index is a relative measure represented by a Z-score, which is classified into the following categories to indicate the level of vulnerability compared to the national average:
*   **≥ 2.5:** Acute
*   **1.5 to 2.5:** Extremely high
*   **0.5 to 1.5:** Relatively high
*   **-0.5 to 0.5:** Average
*   **-1.5 to -0.5:** Relatively low
*   **-2.5 to -1.5:** Extremely low
*   **≤ -2.5:** Slight
