**Population Exposure and Disadvantage**- combines population-based exposure to risk of flooding with Socio-Spatial Flood Vulnerability Index (SSFVI) to provide disadvantage with respect to flood risk mapped on to Lower Super Output Area (LSOAs). Disadvantage is calculated as an equally weighted combination of the degree of exposure in the LSOA and the SSFVI for the LSOA as a whole.

  

The table below summarises the indicators shown and their descriptions:

| Indicator Name | Further Information |

**Notes:**Uses data from the Flood Map for Planning (FMfP) (last updated July 2022). It includes climate change information to show how this will affect flood risk extents for rivers, the sea and surface water and small watercourses over the next century. Data are considered the best available as of time of production "The FMfP can be considered as the best available information on flood risk and will replace the Development Advice Map for planning purposes in June 2023. Although it currently has no official status for planning purposes it is a useful data source when considering flood risks." Data include climate change information but does not have a set of scenarios which can be compared. No version without climate change was found but may be available. All flood types and return periods have been merged. Method has involved attributing 10m population estimates 2011 into the flood zones. Some of the figures do not match perfectly due to removal of population counts <2 people which are likely to be spurious due to matching 10m cells to LSOAs. Some LSOAs had count of those defended as slightly higher than those exposed, so this was corrected to the exposed total. Defences relate to River and coastal flooding only.
