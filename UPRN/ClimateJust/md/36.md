# Population Exposure and Disadvantage

**Population Exposure and Disadvantage** — combines population-based exposure to flood risk with the Socio-Spatial Flood Vulnerability Index (SSFVI) to provide a measure of disadvantage mapped onto Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs). Disadvantage is calculated as an equally weighted combination of the degree of exposure in the LSOA and the SSFVI for the LSOA as a whole.

## Indicators

**Population**
Lower Super Output Area Population (2011), confirmed match for the population data used.

**Population Exposed (Count)**
Estimated count of population living within a flood zone (irrespective of defences).

**Exposed Population Defended (Count)**
Estimated count of population living within defended areas. Estimate only: not all defended areas are mapped, e.g. for small river and coastal defences and surface water interventions.

**Exposed Population Undefended (Count)**
Estimated count of population living within undefended areas. Estimate only: not all defended areas are mapped, e.g. for small river and coastal defences and surface water interventions.

**Population Exposed (Z-Score)**
Z-score of Population Exposed.

**Exposed Population Undefended (Z-Score)**
Z-score of Exposed Population Undefended.

**Disadvantage (all potentially exposed, Z-Score)**
Final Flood Disadvantage taking account of all potentially exposed people (irrespective of whether defended or not). Null = not exposed.

**Disadvantage (all potentially undefended, Z-Score)**
Final Flood Disadvantage taking account of all potentially undefended people (i.e., taking account of published data on areas protected by river and coastal defences). Null = not exposed or all the population of the LSOA is defended.

## Z-Score Classification

Z-Scores for Population Exposed and Disadvantage are classified as follows. Positive values denote more people potentially exposed/disadvantaged than average; values around 0 denote around the average for Wales; negative values denote fewer people than average potentially exposed/disadvantaged.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight

## Notes

Uses data from the Flood Map for Planning (FMfP), last updated July 2022. It includes climate change information showing how flood risk extents for rivers, the sea, surface water and small watercourses will be affected over the next century. The FMfP is considered the best available information on flood risk and will replace the Development Advice Map for planning purposes from June 2023. Data include climate change information but without a comparable set of scenarios. All flood types and return periods have been merged. Population estimates (2011) have been attributed using a 10m grid into flood zones. Some figures may not match perfectly due to removal of population counts below 2, which are likely spurious. Some LSOAs had defended counts slightly higher than exposed counts, which was corrected to the exposed total. Defences relate to river and coastal flooding only.
