# Income

**Reference:** I1, I2, I3, I4 & I5

**Theme:** Vulnerability

**Hazard reference:** Flood (Revised Data, 2017)

**Characteristic:** Inability to Prepare, Respond and Recover

**Indicator:** Income

**Supporting Variable:** See Data Sources below

**Assumption:** Higher proportions of people on low incomes in an area indicate a higher vulnerability. Low income households are more vulnerable to flooding due to the effect of low income on people's ability to adapt — to prepare for events, respond to them when they occur, and recover from them afterwards.

## Evidence

More information is available at: [http://www.climatejust.org.uk/messages/people-low-incomes](http://www.climatejust.org.uk/messages/people-low-incomes)

- Low income households are less likely to have the capacity to fully prepare for future floods (through insurance and property level measures).
- Low income households are less likely to own their own home. Housing tenure together with low income may restrict people's ability to make modifications to the home they do not own (Fielding and Burningham, 2005).
- A lack of savings restricts the ability of households to respond immediately to flood damage, e.g. through spending on repairs and replacements that would kick-start a recovery process (Tapsell et al., 2002).
- Disruption of transport systems is likely to particularly affect people who depend upon public transport to get to work or access services.

## Data Sources

**i1 — Unemployed (% unemployed)**
Census, ONS (2011). KS501, % unemployed in population aged 16–74. Available at LSOA (Eng/Wales), DZ (Scotland), SOA (NI).

**i2 — Long-term unemployed (% who are LTU or never worked)**
Census, ONS (2011). KS611. People aged 16–74 never worked or long-term unemployed as a % of all aged 16–74. Available at LSOA (Eng/Wales), DZ (Scotland), SOA (NI).

**i3 — Low income occupations (% in routine or semi-routine occupations)**
Census, ONS (2011). KS611. People aged 16–74 in routine or semi-routine occupations as a % of all aged 16–74. Available at LSOA (Eng/Wales), DZ (Scotland), SOA (NI).

**i4 — Households with dependent children and no adults in employment (%)**
Census, ONS (2011). KS106. Households with no adults in employment and dependent children as a % of all households. Available at LSOA (Eng/Wales), DZ (Scotland), SOA (NI).

**i5 — People income deprived (%)**
ONS, National Records of Scotland, NI Department for Communities (2010). England & Wales: IMD average weekly household net income (equivalised after housing costs). Scotland: SIMD Income index. Northern Ireland: NIIMD 2010. Available at LSOA (Eng/Wales), DZ (Scotland), SOA (NI).

Additional census boundary data downloaded from: borders.ukdataservice.ac.uk, gov.scot/Topics/Statistics/sns/BoundMap, and [http://statistics.gov.scot/](http://statistics.gov.scot/). Licensed under the Open Government Licence.
