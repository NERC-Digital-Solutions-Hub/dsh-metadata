
**Enhanced Exposure** — Enhanced exposure refers to aspects of the physical environment, such as the availability of green space or housing characteristics, which tend to accentuate or offset the severity of flood events. The map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within the following domains:

- Physical environment
- Housing characteristics

## Z-Score Classification

The Enhanced Exposure Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher enhanced exposure than average; values around 0 denote around the average for Wales; negative values denote lower enhanced exposure than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight

Source: ONS Greenspace. Column AH — lack of private greenspace (% dwellings without gardens). Weight: 0.330.

Source: ONS Greenspace. Column R — median combined size of parks, public gardens and playing fields within 1,000m radius (m²). Weight: 0.330.
