**Population Exposure and Disadvantage** - combines population-based exposure to risk of flooding with Socio-Spatial Flood Vulnerability Index (SSFVI) to provide disadvantage with respect to flood risk mapped on to Lower Super Output Area (LSOAs). Disadvantage is calculated as an equally weighted combination of the degree of exposure in the LSOA and the SSFVI for the LSOA as a whole.

  

The table below summarises the indicators shown and their descriptions:

| Indicator Name | Further Information |
| --- | --- |
| Population | Lower Super Output Area Population (2011), confirmed match for the population data used |
| Population Exposed (Count) | Estimated count of population living within a flood zone (irrespective of defences) |
| Exposed Population Defended (Count) | Estimated count of population living within defended areas. Estimate only: not all defended areas are mapped, e.g. for small river and coastal defences and surface water interventions |
| Exposed Population Undefended (Count) | Estimated count of population living within undefended areas. Estimate only: not all defended areas are mapped, e.g. for small river and coastal defences and surface water interventions |
| Population Exposed (Z-Score) | Z-score of Population Exposed |
| Exposed Population Undefended (Z-Score) | Z-score of Exposed Population Undefended |
| Disadvantage (all potentially exposed, Z-Score) | Final Flood Disadvantage taking account of all potentially exposed people (irrespective of whether defended or not). Null = not exposed |
| Disadvantage (all potentially undefended, Z-Score) | Final Flood Disadvantage taking account of all potentially undefended poeple (i.e., taking account of published data on areas protected by river and coastal defences). Null = not exposed or all the population of the LSOA is defended |

Uses data from the Flood Map for Flood Zones 2 and 3 for river and coastal flooding and surface water flooding. Method has involved attributing 10m population estimates 2011 into the flood zones.
