# Sensitivity

**Sensitivity** — Sensitivity refers to personal biophysical characteristics which affect the likelihood that a heat wave or flood event will have negative health and welfare impacts. For example, older people tend to be more susceptible to the effects of high temperatures. The map shows the result of an equally-weighted combination of neighbourhood-level scores for indicators within the following domains:

- Age
- Health

## Z-Score Classification

The Sensitivity Index Z-Score is classified as follows. Positive values denote higher sensitivity than average; values around 0 denote around the average for England; negative values denote lower sensitivity than average.

- **≥ 2.5** — Acute
- **1.5 to 2.5** — Extremely high
- **0.5 to 1.5** — Relatively high
- **-0.5 to 0.5** — Average
- **-0.5 to -1.5** — Relatively low
- **-1.5 to -2.5** — Extremely low
- **≤ -2.5** — Slight

## Sensitivity Index Indicators

### Domain: Age

*Young children (% people under 5 years)*
Source: Population estimation ONS 2020. Mid-2019 Population Estimates for Lower Layer Super Output Areas in England and Wales by Single Year of Age and Sex, Persons. Weight: 0.500.

*Older people (% people over 75 years)*
Source: Population estimation ONS 2020. Mid-2019 Population Estimates for Lower Layer Super Output Areas in England and Wales by Single Year of Age and Sex, Persons. Weight: 0.500.

### Domain: Health

*Premature death (rate per 100,000)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. Health Deprivation and Disability Domain: Premature death (rate per 100,000). Weight: 0.250.

*GP-recorded chronic condition (rate per 100)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. Health Deprivation and Disability Domain: GP-recorded chronic condition (rate per 100). Weight: 0.250.

*GP-recorded mental health condition (rate per 100)*
Source: Welsh Index of Multiple Deprivation, Welsh Government 2019. Health Deprivation and Disability Domain: GP-recorded mental health condition (rate per 100). New indicator. Weight: 0.250.

*Medical and Care Residents (Score)*
Source: Census table QS421SC. Uses indicator m2 from Sayers et al. Note: dated information, less reliable. Weight: 0.250.
