Population Exposure and Disadvantage - combines population-based exposure to risk of flooding with Socio-Spatial Flood Vulnerability Index (SSFVI) to provide disadvantage with respect to flood risk mapped on to Lower Super Output Area (LSOAs). Disadvantage is calculated as an equally weighted combination of the degree of exposure in the LSOA and the SSFVI for the LSOA as a whole.

Notes:Data are the latest available from the Environment Agency (data.gov) as of Nov 2022. Data are considered the best available. Data have not been used to assess property level exposure though have been overlaid with a 10m population grid as a broad indicator of population exposure. There is some data cleaning (min population value 2) and data are aggregated to LSOA level. All flood types and return periods have been merged. Uses data from the Flood Map for Flood Zones 2 and 3 for river and coastal flooding and surface water flooding. Method has involved attributing 10m population estimates 2011 into the flood zones. Some of the figures do not match perfectly due to removal of population counts < 2 people which are likely to be spurious due to matching 10m cells to LSOAs. Defences relate to River and coastal flooding only. Note that these data were due to be superceded in Dec 2022 therefore improved datasets may now be available for estimating flood exposure.
